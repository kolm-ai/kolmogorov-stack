01 · Build path

Building and compiling

What you can compile, how big it goes, how long it takes, and what the artifact actually contains. Every number below is from a verified run; numbers cite the GPU.

What bit-widths can I compile to?

From 1.0-bit ternary (BitNet-style via AngelSlim) through INT2, INT3, INT4, INT5, INT6, INT8, FP8, BF16, and FP16. The forge enumerates every supported combination per base family. Default is NF4 + Unsloth Dynamic 2.0 because it consistently lands the best K-score retention per gigabyte across the bases we test against. Pick the row from the matrix on /compile if you want a different target.

Below 2 bits, expect K-score retention to fall off a cliff outside reasoning-heavy bases that were trained with ternary in mind. We don't hide it: the forge prints the expected K-score envelope before you commit a run.

What's the largest base model kolm can quantize?

We have verified runs up to DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B: 61 GB BF16 compressed to 17.9 GB INT4 in 125.3 seconds on a single RTX 5090, NF4 + double quantization via bitsandbytes 0.49.2 on torch 2.11.0+cu128. Loads in 13.3 s, sits at 19.22 GB VRAM, hits 11.5 tok/s, and reasoned correctly through the standard snail-on-wall problem end-to-end.

Larger MoEs and dense bases work if BF16 fits on disk; the quantize worker is layer-streamed and never holds the full weight tensor in VRAM. We are actively testing 70B and 405B; the matrix on /compile is updated when a size is reproducibly green.

Does kolm support multi-token prediction (MTP)?

Yes, on bases that support it natively (the Qwen3.6 family). The compiler emits MTP-aware student weights when you pass --mode=mpt. The MTP head ships inside the .kolm compile and the runtime picks it up automatically; you don't need to wire anything else.

Can I use my own teacher API key?

Yes. KOLM_DISTILL_TEACHER accepts a comma-separated list, e.g. anthropic,openai,deepseek,xai. The compiler attempts each teacher in order and retries across the list if one is down or rate-limited. Token spend goes to your own keys; we never proxy paid teacher traffic through our infra.

The receipt records which teacher actually produced each batch, so if you mix teachers across a run you can audit the mix after the fact.

How long does a typical compile take?

On a single RTX 5090: 11.6 s for Qwen2.5-0.5B INT4 (0.93 GB to 0.44 GB), 13.9 s for Qwen2.5-32B INT4 (5.8 GB to 1.9 GB), 29.2 s for Qwen2.5-7B INT4 (14.2 GB to 5.2 GB), and 125.3 s for DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B INT4 (61.0 GB to 17.9 GB). Multi-teacher reasoning runs are longer because the distill loop walks more captures; budget 5 to 20 minutes for a 7B reasoning compile.

What's "K-score"?

K-score is a composite quality score in the Kolmogorov tradition: it weights reasoning, coding, instruction-following, and fluency against a held-out eval pack and emits a single 0 to 100 number with a per-axis breakdown. The full spec, the per-axis weights, the contamination check, and the reference eval packs are on /k-score. The math is in the open source.

The runtime refuses to load any artifact whose K-score is below the configured floor at load time. Default floor is 80.

Does the compiled artifact ship the weights?

Yes. A .kolm is a self-contained, signed bundle: weights + spec + receipt + eval pack snapshot. The whole thing is verifiable offline with kolm verify <cid>; no network call required. You can ship a .kolm to a customer behind a firewall and they can prove it came from you without ever phoning home.

Does kolm support mergekit?

Yes. --mode=merge-first runs TIES, DARE, or SLERP across your chosen teachers and bases before the distill step begins. The merged interim is consumed by the distill loop; the final output is a single distilled .kolm whose receipt records the merge recipe. You can reproduce the merge from the receipt alone.

Can I distill on a reasoning teacher (CoT)?

Yes. Chain-of-thought transfers cleanly when the student is in the same family as the teacher's trace style. Phi-4-Mini-Reasoning pairs particularly well with Claude Opus 4.7; DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B is already trained on R1 traces and accepts further specialization without losing its reasoning surface. The forge will warn you when a teacher and base family don't match well.

How do I author my own eval pack?

An eval pack is a JSONL file plus a manifest naming the per-axis weights and the I/O schema. Any pack conforming to the kolm-eval-pack-1 schema is accepted by the compiler. We ship reference packs for prior auth, claims adjudication, SOAP notes, FWA triage, and the Frontier methods baseline. The compiler holds out the eval pack at distill time and emits eval_contamination:true on any content-hash collision; you can't defeat it by rewording.

02 · Legal & provenance

Legality and provenance

The direct answers about distillation from frontier APIs, the signature chain, and what an artifact attests to. Provider ToS vary; we don't bypass any of them and we mark synthetic data explicitly.

Is it legal to distill from Claude, GPT, or Gemini?

kolm's recommended path is to distill only from your own captured API traffic — outputs that came back to your account against your own prompts, on your own paid keys. Provider ToS vary across releases and across regions; we don't bypass any of them and we don't host a shared scrape. Read your provider's terms and act accordingly.

For synthetic-trace augmentation (where the compiler generates extra prompts from a teacher to fill a coverage gap), the receipt explicitly flags synthetic_augmentation:true with the teacher id and the augmentation ratio. You can configure the compiler to refuse any artifact whose synthetic ratio exceeds your policy.

How do I prove an artifact came from me?

Every .kolm carries an Ed25519 signature over a JCS-canonical SHA-256 of the spec + weights + receipts. The public key lives in your registry, in the artifact sidecar, and on your team's /verify-prod page. kolm verify <cid> re-checks the full chain offline. A signature mismatch refuses to load.

If you self-host the registry, the key never leaves your infrastructure. Customers verify with the public half and the math says everything they need to know.

Can I publish a .kolm on Hugging Face?

Yes. kolm push hf writes a Hugging Face-compatible repo with the .kolm compile, a model card, and a kolm "verified artifact" badge that links back to the on-chain verification record. Pulling it down with kolm pull hf://<owner>/<repo> re-verifies the signature before the artifact is usable.

What's in the receipt chain?

capture-id → dataset hash → teacher trace → eval pack hash → weight hash → Ed25519 signature. Each link verifiable independently, each cited by content-hash, and each printable from kolm verify --pretty. The receipt also records compiler version, build timestamp, K-score and per-axis scores, the teacher mix, the merge recipe (if any), and whether synthetic augmentation was used.

What SLSA level are you at?

Currently SLSA Level 2 attested. Level 3 (CI provenance plus reproducible builds end-to-end) is in progress; the gap is the deterministic GPU kernel pinning, which is on the W6xx engineering line. The current attestation lives on /security.

Are .kolm artifacts reproducible bit-for-bit?

Spec, eval pack snapshot, and metadata: yes, deterministic. Weights: deterministic given the same teacher trace, which is the part outside our control if you use a hosted teacher. The receipt records every seed we control. We are working with kernel maintainers on full determinism across CUDA driver versions.

How do I detect a forged or tampered artifact?

Any byte flip in a .kolm breaks the SHA-256 over the bundle, which breaks the Ed25519 signature, which causes kolm verify to refuse the file and the runtime to refuse to load it. There is no in-band path to bypass this check; the verifier is in the open source and the math is checked at every load.

03 · Privacy & compliance

Privacy and compliance

Where data lives, what crosses our network, and exactly what compliance posture we hold today. We mark what's shipped, what's in progress, and what's on the roadmap.

Do you see my prompts?

On the hosted gateway, prompts transit through our network. The Privacy Membrane runs PHI and PII redaction in-line before persistence; redacted captures land in your local lake, not ours. We do not retain raw prompts on hosted infra beyond the request lifetime; the lake is yours and the encryption key never leaves your tenant.

On self-host or air-gap deployments, nothing leaves your network. The gateway, the compiler, the verifier, and the runtime are all running entirely inside your perimeter. See /enterprise for the air-gap kit.

Is HIPAA support certification-gated?

Yes, under BYOC and on-prem deployment patterns. A BAA is available on the Business and Enterprise tiers. The full HIPAA Security Rule mapping lives on /hipaa-mapping and the BAA template is at /baa. PHI does not leave your VPC under the recommended deployment pattern.

What's your SOC 2 status?

SOC 2 Type I available — the Type I report is shareable under NDA today. Type II in progress — the observation window is open; the report is expected in the next reporting cycle. Until Type II is final we do not represent it as shipped. See /security for the timeline.

ISO 27001?

The ISO 27001 mapping is shipped and shareable under NDA. Certification itself is on the enterprise roadmap; we will publish the certificate when the audit is complete. We don't claim certification we haven't earned.

Can I run kolm fully air-gapped?

Yes. The Enterprise air-gap kit ships the CLI, the gateway, the runtime, and a curated set of pre-approved base artifacts as a single tarball. The verifier runs locally against your registry's public key; no traffic crosses the air gap. We have customers running this pattern today.

EU AI Act — what's your posture?

kolm is a tool, not a deployed model; classification depends on what you build with it. The receipt chain (capture provenance, eval pack, K-score, teacher mix) is designed to map cleanly to the Act's transparency and documentation requirements for high-risk systems. We are tracking the Code of Practice and will publish a mapping when the final text settles. Talk to the field team for the current draft.

ITAR / export control?

kolm is general-purpose open-source software; the core (compiler, runtime, verifier, K-score math) is Apache-2.0 and the public registry is accessible from the U.S. and most jurisdictions. For ITAR-controlled workloads, the air-gap kit is the supported path; we sign a contractual data-handling addendum on Enterprise. The hosted control plane is not a path for ITAR data.

04 · Pricing & tiers

Pricing and tiers

Flat compile credits, your own teacher API spend, no per-token markup. Self-hosting is always included. The full breakdown is on /pricing.

Is there a free tier?

Yes. The free tier includes the full CLI, gateway, runtime, and verifier; 5 compiles per month; and 1 GB of capture lake storage. Pro is $49/mo: 50 compiles per month, access to higher K-score teachers, and the hosted bake-off API. Full ladder is on /pricing.

What counts as a "compile"?

One distill + quantize + sign + benchmark cycle counts as one compile. Re-runs of a verified recipe with the same spec (same captures, same teacher, same base, same target bit-width) reuse the cached receipt and don't count against your quota. Failed compiles don't count either; you only pay for green output.

Do you charge per token?

No. Compile credits are flat. Token spend on teacher APIs goes to your own keys (Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepSeek, xAI, etc.); we never proxy or mark up paid teacher traffic. If you self-host a teacher, the compile is free of token cost entirely.

What's the marketplace revenue share?

Creators keep 70%; kolm takes 30% on paid recipe downloads from the registry. Free recipes are free in both directions. The split is the same for all tiers; we don't claw it back at scale.

Do you accept POs and invoicing?

Yes, on Business and Enterprise tiers. Net-30 standard. Multi-year commits are offered at a discount; the terms live in the order form, not in a back-channel email. Legal is on the kickoff call and stays through the redline.

Am I locked in?

No. The .kolm format is open, the verifier is open-source, and the runtime is yours. If kolm.ai disappeared tomorrow, your compiled artifacts would continue to load and verify against any conforming runtime. The captures in your lake are JSONL on your disk. There is no proprietary serialization to escape.

05 · Comparisons

How kolm compares

Where kolm overlaps with adjacent tools, where it differs, and where it composes with them rather than replacing them. We don't take cheap shots at peers.

How does kolm differ from Unsloth?

Unsloth is a quantization and fine-tuning engine; we use it. kolm uses Unsloth Dynamic 2.0 as one of its default quantization backends and adds the capture pipeline, the K-score gate, the signed artifact format, the marketplace, and the audit-ready receipt chain on top. If you are happy with Unsloth alone, keep using it; kolm is the compiler layer that sits around tools like Unsloth.

How does kolm differ from LiteLLM?

LiteLLM is a gateway. kolm is a gateway + compiler + signed artifact format. The kolm gateway can sit in front of LiteLLM or replace it. After kolm runs, you have a portable .kolm file that you own and that any conforming runtime can verify and load. LiteLLM doesn't try to do that; it doesn't need to.

How does kolm differ from OpenAI fine-tuning?

OpenAI fine-tuning produces a model that lives on OpenAI infrastructure, behind an OpenAI API key, with usage gated by their tenancy. kolm produces a .kolm file that lives on your disk, signed by your key, loadable by any conforming runtime, on hardware you control. Yours stays yours. The full side-by-side is at /how-vs-openai-fine-tune.

vs OpenPipe / Predibase / LoRAX?

OpenPipe and Predibase are managed fine-tuning platforms; LoRAX is a multi-tenant LoRA server. kolm overlaps with the fine-tuning step but adds the K-score gate, the signed receipt chain, the air-gap path, and the marketplace. See /how-vs-openpipe and /how-vs-predibase for the row-by-row comparisons.

vs Hugging Face TGI / vLLM / SGLang / TRT-LLM?

Those are inference runtimes. kolm is a compiler; the artifact it emits runs on those runtimes. The gateway has adapters for all four; the runtime contract loads a .kolm into any of them and runs the verifier at load time. Use what you already run; kolm sits one layer up.

vs writing the whole pipeline myself?

The capture, distill, quantize, score, sign, verify loop is something a small team can put together in a quarter or two. The value kolm adds is that we have already done it, the receipts are auditable, the K-score math is open, the registry is shipped, and the air-gap path is supported. If you prefer to roll your own, fork it; the license permits it.
