Company entity status.

kolm is an open-source project today, not a chartered company. The codebase is Apache-2.0, the maintainers contribute under their own names, and there is no legal entity called "Kolm Inc." or "Kolm Labs" yet. This page is the honest record of where we are and the decision matrix for which entity form we will adopt.

Incorporation pending Q3 2026 No entity exists yet. No equity has been issued. No ToS in the name of a company has been published.

Current status

Currently an open-source project. Incorporation pending Q3 2026.

  • No legal entity exists. "kolm" and "kolm.ai" are an open-source project name and a domain registration, not a corporation or LLC.
  • Code license: Apache-2.0 across the stack. Contributions come in under the same license; there is no CLA assigning rights to a non-existent company.
  • Domain: kolm.ai is registered to a maintainer in their personal capacity. At incorporation it will be assigned to the new entity.
  • Billing and contracts: There is currently no paid product line that requires a corporate counterparty. Any future enterprise contract that requires a legal entity will trigger or follow incorporation, not precede it.
  • Trademarks: No registered trademarks. The brand-anchor disambiguation that appears on every page exists precisely because the unqualified word "Kolm" overlaps with Kolm therapeutics, Kolm band, Kolm engines, and Petter Kolm.

Decision matrix

Three forms under consideration. The choice has consequences for tax treatment, fundraising path, ownership clarity, and how we write the eventual Terms of Service. None of the three has been chosen.

What happens when we incorporate

When we incorporate, a small set of user-facing artifacts change. Most of the codebase, the artifact format, the CLI, and the runtime path are unchanged.

  • Privacy policy is republished naming the new entity as the data controller (today there is no data controller because there is no entity collecting data centrally - captures stay on customer infrastructure).
  • Terms of Service are republished in the name of the entity. Today there is no ToS because there is no counterparty to one.
  • Billing structure moves from "no commercial relationship" to a tiered pricing page in the entity's name. Existing self-hosted users see no change; nothing in the open-source distribution becomes paid.
  • BAA, MSA, and DPA templates become signable instruments. Today these pages describe what would be in scope; after incorporation, they become executable.
  • Trademark filings on "kolm.ai" and the logo mark begin. The brand-anchor disambiguation span stays on every page regardless - even with a trademark, the surface disambiguation is the polite path for search engines and screen readers.
  • Domain registration transfers from the maintainer's personal Tucows account to the entity's name. DNS does not change.
  • User-facing artifacts unchanged. The .kolm artifact format, the Ed25519 receipt chain, the K-score formula, the CLI surface, the SDK install paths, and the public registry all behave identically. Incorporation is a paperwork event, not a product event.

Why we publish this

Incorporation status is the kind of question an enterprise procurement reviewer or a careful contributor wants a straight answer to. Hiding "we are not yet a company" behind a polished About page would be the kind of theater the rest of the stack is built to avoid. The honest version is on this page.
