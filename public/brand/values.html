Company values.

Three commitments we hold ourselves to and ask you to hold us to. Each one shows up as a concrete property of the codebase, not a wall poster. If we drift from any of them, you will be able to point at the drift in the open repo and call it out.

Value 01 / 03

Transparency

Every claim has a public artifact you can read. There are no "trust us" benchmark numbers, no closed grading rubric, no proprietary scoring formula. If we publish a number, you can reproduce the number.

  • Public source code. Apache-2.0 across the entire stack - CLI, gateway, distill pipeline, compile path, K-score formula, the frontend you are reading this on. No private fork ships better behavior than the public one.
  • Public bench. The benchmark methodology, the prompt set, the grader, and the raw scores are in the repo. Re-running them on your own hardware is a documented quickstart, not a sales-engineering favor.
  • Public K-score formula. The composite quality gate that decides whether a compiled artifact ships is a deterministic function of inputs you can inspect. Same inputs in, same score out, every time.
  • loud-fail envelopes by default. Endpoints return {ok:false, error, install_hint} when a feature is not installed or not configured, never silent passthrough. A redactor that is missing fails loud with no_detector_installed rather than returning the original media as if it had been redacted.
Concrete propertyThe K-score formula is in src/k-score.js. The bench harness is in scripts/bench.cjs. Both are reachable from a fresh git clone in under sixty seconds.
How you call driftIf you reproduce a published number on the same artifact and get a different score, open an issue. The repo history will tell you when the formula last changed and why.
Value 02 / 03

Reproducibility

If we say we built a thing, the receipt is content-addressed. Every artifact carries its own manifest. Every distill carries its own seed split. There are no "trust us" claims about what was trained on what.

  • Every artifact has an artifact_hash. The hash binds the model weights, the manifest, the K-score, the receipt chain, and any attestation report into a single content-addressed identity. Tamper with any field and the hash no longer matches, and the receipt no longer verifies.
  • Every distill has a manifest. Teacher model, capture set hash, seed split, training config, eval rubric, hardware, exact wall-clock duration, and an Ed25519 signature over the whole thing. The manifest is the answer to "what model is in production?"
  • Every receipt is offline-verifiable. kolm verify <cid> works without network. The signature chain validates against the artifact bytes you have on disk.
  • No "trust us" claims. If we cannot point at an artifact_hash, a manifest, a public bench score, or a public commit that proves a claim, we do not make the claim.
Concrete propertyRun kolm verify <cid> against any public artifact. Network off. Receipt either validates or it doesn't - no third state.
How you call driftIf a marketing claim cannot be tied back to an artifact_hash or a public bench row, that is the drift. The no-silent-pass contract on every endpoint is "show the receipt or say it is not yet shipped."
Value 03 / 03

User ownership

Your captures stay yours. Your distilled artifacts stay yours. We claim no intellectual property in either, and the export path is documented as a first-class feature - not an afterthought tucked behind a sales call.

  • Your captures stay yours. By default they are stored at ~/.kolm/events on your own machine, tenant-scoped, and never leave your infrastructure. The hosted control plane is opt-in; even when opted in, raw inputs never cross tenant boundaries (see the federated approval-row sharing design).
  • Your distilled artifacts stay yours. The .kolm file is yours. The model weights inside it are yours. The signed manifest, the K-score, the receipt - all yours. We do not retain a copy on the hosted side unless you explicitly publish.
  • No IP claim on your captures or your distilled artifacts. Apache-2.0 across the code path, no separate "data license" that quietly transfers rights. The captures and the artifacts are your property under the same legal framework that governs the rest of your engineering work.
  • You can export and leave anytime. kolm export dumps your captures, your artifacts, your manifests, and your receipts in their native formats. There is no proprietary container you have to negotiate out of. The model weights export to GGUF, ONNX, vLLM, MLX, or TRT-LLM from the same compile.
Concrete propertyThe default storage path is on your filesystem, not ours. The export command works without an account. The artifact format is documented in the open repo, not a binary spec behind a NDA.
How you call driftIf a future release moves capture storage off your machine by default, or adds a "data license" rider to the SDK, or breaks kolm export, that is the drift. Open an issue and we will own it.

Why publish values at all

Values pages are usually decoration. We publish ours because each one of the three maps to a concrete, auditable property of the codebase - and if we ever drift, the public source code is the receipt. Transparency means the formula is in the repo. Reproducibility means the artifact has a hash. User ownership means your data sits on your disk.
