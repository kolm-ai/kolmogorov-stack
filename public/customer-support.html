Three commands, one pipeline

Distill, run, verify.

The same three verbs do the work on every workload. Capture your tickets, compile a specialist, and verify every reply against a signed chain.

01 · Distill

Compile a specialist from your tickets.

Point your tier-1 traffic at the kolm gateway. Approved tickets become training pairs. A small open-weight base learns the patterns. Output is a single signed .kolm file.

$ kolm distill --namespace support --base qwen2.5-7b
Read the distill flow →
02 · Run

Deploy on hardware that hits SLA.

Serve the .kolm on your own GPU or CPU. Fleet manager rolls updates and auto-rolls back on drift. SLA latency is your hardware's call, not a vendor queue.

$ kolm run support-7b --target prod-cluster
Read the run flow →
03 · Verify

Every reply gets a signed receipt.

Ed25519-signed .kolm artifact. Per-reply receipt chains the input hash, the output hash, and the artifact CID. QA replays any answer from the audit log without recreating state.

$ kolm verify rcpt_01KYC1ZVTGDCW3FX06JQSC

What it covers

Standards your security and compliance teams already track.

Kolm runs entirely on your infrastructure on-prem or BYOC. No vendor-managed inference path. Map controls to the frameworks your customers already audit you against.

SOC 2 Type II
Type II report available

Audit trail, access control, change-management, and incident-response controls inherited by every kolm tenant. Letters available under NDA.

GDPR / DSR
Right-to-erasure surfaced as a CLI verb

kolm erase --user user_id removes the user from captures + eval sets + the searchable audit index. Immutable log retains the redaction receipt only.

PII redaction
Boundary redaction before persist

Presidio + a custom NER overlay strip 18 PII identifier classes before a ticket is persisted to your capture lake. Configurable; you can swap the redactor.

Tenant isolation
Per-tenant Ed25519 keys

One signing key per tenant, KMS-resident. Receipts are tenant-scoped. Cross-tenant replay fails signature verification.

What support teams ship with kolm

Patterns we see in production.

Every workflow ships with a defer-on-low-confidence path. Human gates are on by default; turning them off is a deliberate choice.

Tier-1 deflection

Answer-first triage

Reads the inbound ticket, drafts the reply in your brand voice, attaches the citation from your help-center article that justifies the answer.

input: customer-message
output: draft-reply + citation
defer: low-confidence → human
Macro suggestion

Macro picker for human agents

Suggests the closest macro from your library, pre-fills variables, leaves the human to send. No customer-facing output without an agent click.

input: ticket + agent-cursor
output: ranked-macro + variables
send: human-approved
Refund + RMA

Policy-bounded answer

Reads the order, applies your refund policy, drafts the response. Anything outside the explicit policy window defers to a queue.

input: order + ticket
output: response + decision
out-of-policy: defer
Voice / chat triage

Intent classifier with escalation

Classifies inbound voice transcripts or chat messages, routes to the right queue, escalates anything containing escalation keywords or low-confidence intents.

input: transcript
output: intent + queue
escalate: regex-list + low-conf
Backoffice followup

SLA-breach notifier

Watches the queue, drafts the SLA-apology and next-action email when a ticket nears breach. Operates against your SLA contract, not a vendor default.

input: queue-state + SLA-policy
output: breach-mitigation-draft
send: human-approved
Multilingual reply

In-language answer

Same task, the customer's language. Distilled per language so latency is uniform; no round-trip translation. Defers on low-confidence to the language-specific human queue.

input: ticket + locale
output: in-language-reply
defer: low-conf → locale queue
