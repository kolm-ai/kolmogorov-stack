01Embed framing

Controls your enterprise customer's security review reads.

Each control below is described as the kolm posture today. We name the Wave marker for every control so your customer's vendor-risk team can cite the artifact.

Per-customer compile namespace
tenant-scoped

Each customer gets their own compile namespace. Their captures, their evals, their signed artifact. Tampering with another tenant's receipt chain breaks verification at the namespace boundary.

BYO base model
open-weight roster

Customer chooses the base from the kolm model registry (Qwen, GLM, Gemma, DeepSeek, Step, Kimi K2.5, and more). Locked into the artifact at compile time. No surprise upgrade breaks downstream.

Multi-tenant isolation
enforced at compile

One tenant's training data cannot leak into another tenant's artifact because each artifact is a separate signed binary. Compile receipts attest to the exact corpus hash that produced the weights.

Audit log per tenant
receipt chain

Every inference is a structured receipt. Tenants can stream their slice into their SIEM via webhook. SOC 2 evidence is byte-identical re-verification of any output.

04Architecture

One compile bridge, one artifact per tenant.

The compile bridge runs in your SaaS infrastructure. Customer captures, evals, and labels live in their tenant namespace. The output is a signed binary file you ship as part of their deployment.

05Why embed

Three things change at the contract table.

When you embed kolm instead of renting tokens, three line items in your enterprise contract move in your favor.

Margin survives growth.

Per-token cost stops being linear in usage. Compile is a fixed cost per tenant; runtime is your own hardware. Gross margin compounds as the customer ramps.

Security review shrinks.

Customer security reviews stop asking "does data leave our boundary." The answer is the signed artifact running on hosts they already own or you already operate inside their VPC.

Procurement gets shorter.

"Bring your own model" is now a checkbox you can offer. Enterprise procurement teams that block frontier-API products approve a signed dependency under change control.
