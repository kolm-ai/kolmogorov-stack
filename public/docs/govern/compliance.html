Run & Govern

Compliance mapping.

The kolm evidence DAG is regime-agnostic. The compliance layer is a re-projection of the same DAG into the controls that auditors actually ask about. One artifact, one DAG, three exports: NIST AI RMF crosswalk, ISO/IEC 42001 statement of applicability, and EU AI Act Annex IV technical documentation. No double-entry.

Supported regimes

RegimeExportWhat it covers
NIST AI RMF 1.0kolm compliance export --regime nistMap · Measure · Manage · Govern function crosswalk with evidence pointers.
ISO/IEC 42001:2023kolm compliance export --regime iso42001Annex A control statement of applicability + DAG-backed rationale.
EU AI Act (high-risk)kolm compliance export --regime eu-ai-actAnnex IV technical documentation, including post-market monitoring plan.
SOC 2 (model-system)kolm compliance export --regime soc2CC controls bound to capture, key, and audit log evidence.

How controls bind to evidence

Each compliance control in the kolm catalog points at one or more DAG node types and one or more receipt fields. The export walks the DAG once and projects each control's evidence into the regime-shaped report:

# NIST AI RMF GOVERN-1.2 (legal/regulatory requirements)
control:  NIST_AI_RMF.GOVERN_1_2
evidence: spec.toml#govern.compliance, audit_log:LEGAL_REVIEW_*
status:   satisfied

# NIST AI RMF MEASURE-2.3 (system performance against intended deployment context)
control:  NIST_AI_RMF.MEASURE_2_3
evidence: DAG -> eval:c11a..., DAG -> eval:e83f...
status:   satisfied (last verified 2026-05-12)

# NIST AI RMF MANAGE-2.3 (decommissioning)
control:  NIST_AI_RMF.MANAGE_2_3
evidence: lifecycle:retired (when applicable), audit_log:ARTIFACT_RETIRE
status:   policy_in_place

Open gaps

Where a control has no DAG evidence the export marks it gap not satisfied. The compliance panel surfaces the gap with a one-click way to either add the missing evidence (re-run an eval, add a policy doc) or to mark the control not_applicable with a written justification that itself becomes a DAG node.
