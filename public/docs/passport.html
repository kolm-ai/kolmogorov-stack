Compliance

Passport — the compliance manifest.

The passport is a signed, machine-readable description of a running kolm gateway: every dependency, every key fingerprint, every namespace, every redaction mode, every receipt anchor. Hand it to risk review and they can audit the system without logging in.

Export

kolm passport export --format compliance > passport.json
kolm passport export --format sbom > sbom.cdx.json    # CycloneDX
kolm passport export --format spdx > passport.spdx.json
kolm passport verify passport.json                    # checks signature + freshness

Freshness

Every passport carries generated_at and is signed by the gateway's current key. Re-export after any config change, any key rotation, or any release upgrade. The signature is verifiable offline with the public key published at /.well-known/kolm-keys.json.

What the passport is not

  • Not a SOC 2 report. Auditors still need the attestation letter from your auditor of record.
  • Not a substitute for a DPA. Sub-processor lists live in the DPA; the passport links to them but does not replace them.
  • Not a guarantee of policy. It reports what is configured, not whether the configuration matches your written policy. That mapping is the job of the assurance case.
