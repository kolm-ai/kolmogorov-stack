Receipts.

Every distill, train, eval, and inference run kolm performs emits a signed receipt. The receipt is Ed25519-signed, includes the input hash, the model hash, the recipe hash, the timestamp, and the operator's public-key fingerprint. The receipt is the proof.

Why receipts are the proof

Most AI tooling logs what a model did. kolm signs what a model did, so the log cannot be retroactively edited. The receipt binds an input, a model, a recipe, and a timestamp under one signature. If any of the four changes, the signature breaks. If the signature verifies, the run happened as recorded.

Three uses this enables in practice.

  • Regulator-ready evidence. SOC 2 and HIPAA audits ask for tamper-evident logs. A receipts export is that, in JSONL, with a sealed manifest your auditor can replay through the verifier.
  • Customer-facing trust. When a customer asks “did your model actually train on my data and only my data,” you point them at the receipt for the train run. They verify it themselves with kolm verify or hit /v1/verify/<cid>.
  • Internal debugging. When prod regresses, the receipt for the bad inference is one click from the receipt for the model deployed, which is one click from the receipt for the train run, which is one click from the capture corpus hash. The chain is the trail.

Verify a receipt right now

kolm verify rcpt_01KYC1ZVTGDCW3FX06JQSC --pretty

Or against the public endpoint:

curl https://kolm.ai/v1/verify/rcpt_01KYC1ZVTGDCW3FX06JQSC

Either path returns the full receipt, the verification status, and the chain of related receipts (capture → distill → quantize → deploy → inference).

Caveats / Limitations

  • The signature proves the recording, not the behavior. A receipt confirms that the run happened with the inputs and model recorded. It does not certify that the model's output was correct. Pair receipts with the eval harness for behavior gates.
  • Operator-key custody matters. A receipt is only as trustworthy as the key that signed it. Read the signing reference for the recommended custody pattern (hardware-backed key, rotation cadence, witness signatures for sensitive runs).
  • Audit-export retention is policy, not silicon. Receipts live in your kolm tenant for the retention window you configure. Export to an immutable bucket if you need WORM-grade durability beyond the tenant's default.
