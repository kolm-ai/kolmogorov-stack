How a foundation student becomes a team student

The foundation students are pre-trained on the merged W751 fingerprint of public + opt-in capture corpora for each vertical. When your team runs kolm pull kolm-<vertical>-7b and then distills against your own captured workload (via the W144 capture pipeline + W715 cross-namespace transfer), you get a team student that inherits the vertical's base competence and specialises on your specific terminology, conventions, and workflow.

The transfer math is W715 — cross-namespace transfer learning lets the team distill amortize across the foundation's pre-train, so you typically need 10-100x fewer captures than distilling from scratch to hit the same K-Score on your team's eval set.

Why we ship items 3+4 before items 1+2

Items 1+2 (per-vertical fingerprint pipeline + per-vertical foundation pre-train) require W757 (which ships the fingerprint primitive shared by all 5 verticals) and W715 (which ships the cross-namespace transfer math the foundation needs to be useful). Both are queued in the W707-W806 plan. Items 3+4 (marketplace stub listing + landing pages) are independently shippable and let teams:

  • see which verticals are coming and reserve interest via the marketplace stub
  • start capturing per-vertical corpora now through the kolm wrapper so the eventual distill has the corpus it needs on day one
  • get the case-study skeleton in place so first-mover teams have a publishing template ready

We register the marketplace stubs with pending_distill:true, not_kolm_compiled:true, and kscore:null. The W737 marketplace surface treats kscore:null as "not yet measured" (it never returns a fake number). The status pill on every landing page reads pending compilation. When W757 + W715 land and the real distill runs, the marketplace listing flips atomically — the same slug, a real sha256 content hash, a real K-Score eval, and the case-study slot pre-wired.
