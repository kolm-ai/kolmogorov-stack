How a recipe works

Every recipe follows the same five-step loop. The CLI commands are stable across recipes. The only thing that changes is the corpus, the target model, and the evaluator you use to decide ship-or-no-ship.

  1. Capture — kolm capture log records real inputs and outputs from a teacher model into a namespaced lake on your machine.
  2. Bake-off — kolm bake-off scores candidate small models against your captures so you pick the right student before you waste GPU hours.
  3. Compile — kolm compile --target qwen-7b-int4 distills onto the winning student and quantizes to INT4 in one pass.
  4. Verify — kolm verify produces a signed manifest with K-Score, eval coverage, and the attested compile environment.
  5. Deploy — kolm deploy ships the .kolm artifact to the runtime of your choice: vLLM, SGLang, TGI, TensorRT-LLM, or your own GPU.
