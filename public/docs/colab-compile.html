Free-tier compile path

Compile in Colab. No install, no card, no CUDA setup.

Open the notebook, paste a free Kolm API key, and produce a signed .kolm specialist on Google's free GPU. Same artifact, same verifier, same K-Score. For Persona B (no local GPU) and Persona E (hobbyist / learner).

How it works

The notebook walks the full distill loop on Colab. It detects whether you got a Colab GPU (T4, L4, or A100) and either compiles locally on that GPU, or relays the job to Modal/RunPod through the Kolm cloud route. Either way you end up with a signed .kolm artifact you can verify offline, push to the hub, or serve over OpenAI-compatible HTTP.

captures → spec.json → kolm compile (Colab GPU OR cloud) → artifact.kolm

What you get

  • A signed .kolm artifact — identical bytes whether the compile ran on Colab, Modal, or your own RTX.
  • A K-Score envelope — accuracy on the frozen eval set, size, latency, cost, coverage. Same gate (>=0.85) as paid plans.
  • Receipts — every compile step is recorded so kolm verify can reproduce the score.
  • Optional hub publish — one command sends the artifact to your namespace.

For 32B+ models on Colab Free, use the cloud relay path: the notebook posts the spec to POST /v1/compile/cloud with backend modal or runpod. You'll need either a paid Kolm plan or your own Modal/RunPod token. Free Kolm accounts get $5/mo of cloud-relay credit included.

Verification (still pure CPU)

Verification doesn't need a GPU. Even on the free Colab tier, after the compile finishes you can:

kolm verify ~/.kolm/artifacts/colab_demo.kolm

The verifier replays the frozen eval set, recomputes the K-Score, and confirms the signature. If you compiled via the cloud relay, the artifact ships to your hub namespace; pull it locally with kolm pull yourname/colab_demo.

Persona B (no local GPU) recommended path

  1. Capture calls locally with the gateway (any laptop, no GPU needed).
  2. Open the Colab notebook, paste your Kolm key.
  3. Run all cells. The notebook auto-picks Colab-local GPU compile vs. cloud relay.
  4. Download the .kolm artifact back to your laptop (one-line cell at the bottom of the notebook).
  5. On your laptop: kolm export gguf-q4km artifact.kolm → kolm run via llama.cpp or ollama.

CPU throughput numbers for the resulting GGUF artifact are tabled in hardware compatibility.

Constraints. Colab Free occasionally evicts long-running runtimes; if your compile exceeds ~12 hours you should split it. Free-tier GPU access is best-effort — Google rotates between T4 / L4 based on load and may downgrade to CPU during peak hours. The notebook's first cell prints what you actually got so the next steps reflect the actual runtime. There is no telemetry from the notebook to Kolm beyond the standard /v1/compile/cloud POST when the cloud relay is used.
