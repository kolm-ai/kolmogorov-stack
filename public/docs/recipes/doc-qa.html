Docs / Recipes / Document Q&A

Open-source AI compiler

Distill a doc-grounded Q&A model.

Point kolm at a directory of markdown, PDFs, and HTML and compile a small student that answers questions in your house voice, with your terminology, and with citations back to source chunks. The retriever still runs at inference time — what the student learns is your corpus tone, your section conventions, and how your team phrases an answer when the source is right in front of them.

What you need

  • kolm CLI — npm i -g github:kolm-ai/kolm or build from source. Confirm with kolm version.
  • API key — kolm login or sign up at /signup.
  • Teacher provider keys — ANTHROPIC_API_KEY, OPENAI_API_KEY, and CEREBRAS_API_KEY exported in the shell. A missing key removes the matching teacher from the council, with a warning, but does not stop the run.
  • GPU for the training step — one card with 16 GB+ VRAM (RTX 4090 / 5090) or a $0.39/hr RunPod community GPU. Corpus ingest, spec, and collect are CPU-bound.
  • Corpus shape — one or more directories of .md, .pdf, .html, or .txt files. The ingester chunks at heading boundaries and discards binary noise. A handbook of 50–500 pages distills cleanly in under 10 minutes.
Read this first. The recipe does not bake your corpus into the weights. The chunked corpus stays on disk; an in-process retriever pulls relevant chunks at inference time and the student answers grounded in them. What the student learns is the tone, the section conventions, the citation style, and how to handle "the doc does not say" with a clear refusal instead of a hallucinated answer. That separation keeps the corpus updatable without re-distilling.

Step-by-step

Step 1

Ingest the corpus

The corpus command walks the path globs, parses every supported file type (markdown, PDF text-layer, HTML, plain text), chunks at heading boundaries with a 256–512 token overlap window, and writes the chunk index under ~/.kolm/corpora/<namespace>/.

kolm corpus add ./docs/**/*.{md,pdf,html} --namespace handbook

Inspect the result with kolm corpus stats --namespace handbook: it reports chunk count, total tokens, file-type breakdown, and the SHA-256 of the chunked index (pinned into the receipt at distill time).

Step 2

Generate a doc-Q&A distill spec

The --task doc-qa hint tells the spec generator to wire a doc-grounded prompt template, a retrieval block that pulls relevant chunks at training time, and a citation-faithfulness eval. The --grounded flag enforces "answer only from the cited chunks; say so when the corpus does not cover it."

kolm distill spec \
  --base qwen2.5-7b-instruct \
  --task doc-qa \
  --grounded \
  --teachers anthropic:claude-sonnet-4-6,openai:gpt-4o,cerebras:llama-3.3-70b \
  --out spec.json

The default teacher weights (0.40 / 0.40 / 0.20) balance two slow but careful teachers against one fast carrier. Override with --teacher-weights 0.5,0.3,0.2 if you have a lineage preference.

Step 3

Collect doc-grounded training pairs

The collector samples chunks from the handbook namespace, asks each teacher "here is the chunk, here is a realistic question a user would ask — answer it," and records every teacher's grounded response. The output JSONL carries the chunk hash, the question, the three teacher responses, the council-merged response, and per-teacher provenance.

kolm distill collect \
  --spec spec.json \
  --out captures.jsonl

A 200-chunk handbook with 3-question-per-chunk sampling produces 600 training rows in 4–6 minutes across three teachers. Cost typically lands at $2–$5.

Step 4

Train the student with QLoRA

QLoRA on Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct fits in roughly 16 GB of VRAM with grad-checkpointing and microbatch 1. The trainer signs the resulting adapter with your tenant's Ed25519 key and pins the spec hash + corpus index SHA + base-model commit into the receipt chain.

kolm distill train \
  --spec spec.json \
  --captures captures.jsonl \
  --qlora

One epoch on 600 grounded pairs takes about 40 seconds on an RTX 5090 and around 2 minutes on an RTX 4090. The signed .kolm artifact is written to ~/.kolm/artifacts/; the path is printed on success.

Step 5

Export to GGUF for local serving

Convert the signed adapter to a Q4_K_M GGUF for Ollama, LM Studio, llama.cpp, or any other GGUF-aware runtime. Q4_K_M is a strong default for doc Q&A: small enough for an 8 GB card, large enough to keep faithful grounding when the retrieved chunk is long.

kolm export ~/.kolm/artifacts/<artifact>.kolm \
  --backend gguf \
  --quant q4_k_m \
  --out handbook-qa.gguf

Pair with --backend mlx for Apple Silicon, --backend onnx for browser WebGPU, --backend tensorrt for NVIDIA serving, or --backend coreml for iPhone/iPad.

Step 6

Test the model end-to-end

kolm run wires the GGUF, attaches the handbook corpus retriever, and answers a one-shot question. The output includes the answer text plus the cited chunk paths so you can verify the grounding.

kolm run handbook-qa.gguf "Where do I file a PTO request?"

or serve as an OpenAI-compatible endpoint:

ollama create handbook-qa -f Modelfile
ollama run handbook-qa "What is the parental leave policy?"

Point your internal tooling at http://localhost:11434/api/generate (Ollama) or wrap with a thin RAG layer if you want to swap the retriever for a vector DB you already operate.

What you ship

Artifacthandbook-qa.gguf (~4 GB Q4_K_M on Qwen2.5-7B) + signed .kolm manifest with spec, corpus index SHA, teacher provenance, and Ed25519 signature.
ModelfileOne Ollama Modelfile that pins the chat template and stop tokens. Drops straight into any Ollama-aware tool.
Retriever index~/.kolm/corpora/handbook/ directory with chunked sources, chunk-to-file map, and BM25 + dense index. Updateable without re-distilling.
ReceiptHMAC chain (corpus → seeds → recipes → evals → package) verifiable offline with kolm verify handbook-qa.kolm.
Deploy targetsOllama (local + macOS), vLLM (production GPU serving), llama.cpp (edge + CPU), LM Studio (desktop UI), WebGPU via kolm export --backend onnx.

Caveats

Retrieval still happens at inference time. If your corpus changes, re-run kolm corpus add with --rebuild-index; you do not need to re-distill unless the corpus tone shifts materially. The student learns voice, not facts.
PDF parsing reads text layers, not scanned images. For scanned PDFs, run kolm extract <file> --ocr first and feed the extracted markdown into kolm corpus add.
A small student grounded in retrieval will refuse to invent answers when the corpus is silent. That is the design. If you want broader open-domain answers, swap the base for a larger student or pair this artifact with a general-purpose model behind a router.
The receipt verifies the artifact's signature and chain. Document confidentiality is enforced by where you ship the GGUF and where the corpus index lives — both stay on your hardware by default; nothing crosses the network at inference time.
