Publish your .kolm to the marketplace

You compiled a .kolm artifact that you want others to use. This page covers the three-step publishing flow, the 70/30 revenue split that locks in publisher economics, how the ratings and reviews system works, and how buyers can layer transfer learning on top of your published artifact using their own captures.

Publish your .kolm

The publishing flow is three steps. None of them require leaving the CLI.

Step 1 — Register a publisher account

You need a kolm.ai account so your listing carries a stable publisher_id. The account is the same one you use for the rest of the platform — there is no separate "publisher" account type.

kolm signup            # one-time
kolm whoami            # confirm your tenant_id (this is your publisher_id)

Step 2 — Publish the artifact

Point kolm marketplace publish at the .kolm file. The CLI uploads the artifact, computes the cid (content-addressed identifier), and registers a listing under your publisher_id. You declare the four facet axes that drive faceted search at /marketplace: --vertical, --task, --hardware, plus the price.

kolm marketplace publish \
  --cid my-clause-extractor \
  --vertical legal \
  --task extraction \
  --hardware m-series \
  --price 0

Valid values are pinned in the W737 spec: vertical ∈ {medical, legal, code, finance, support, general}; task ∈ {extraction, generation, reasoning, support}; hardware ∈ {m-series, rtx, h100, cpu}. A fat-finger fails loud at publish time, not at search time.

Step 3 — Set price + verify the listing

Price is in whole US cents per call (use 0 for free). Confirm your listing is browsable by re-running the search the way a buyer would:

kolm marketplace search --vertical legal --min-kscore 0.80

You should see your listing in the JSON envelope. If you see marketplace_empty, your filters returned zero rows — broaden them and try again.

Revenue split — 70/30 publisher/platform

This is the TOS. By publishing to the kolm.ai marketplace, you agree to the 70/30 revenue split below. The split is hard-coded in the publishing path (src/marketplace.js: W737_PUBLISHER_SHARE = 0.70) so it cannot drift between deploys.
partyshareexample: $100 revenue
publisher (you)70%$70.00
platform (kolm.ai)30%$30.00

The platform share covers hosting the .kolm bytes, running the K-Score gate that powers the Verified pill, serving the catalog manifest, and the ratings/reviews infrastructure described below.

Payout integration is on the public roadmap. This page is the TOS that locks in the contract for when payouts ship in a follow-up wave. Today the computeRoyalty() function returns the math (you can call it via POST /v1/marketplace/listings) and no Stripe connection writes a balance. Publishing today is free, and any revenue accrued before the payout pipe ships will be honored at the rates above when it does.

PAYOUT PIPE PENDING — the Stripe payout pipe is a roadmap item. computeRoyalty() emits the split math today; the bank transfer half lands in a follow-up wave. Publishers are protected at the rates above for any accrual that happens before the pipe goes live.

Reviews and ratings

Buyers can leave one review per listing using a 1–5 star scale plus a text body. The aggregate ratings_avg + ratings_count on each listing card is computed live from the underlying event-store rows — no denormalised counter that can drift from the truth.

How the 1–5 scale works

starsguidance
5Solves the task end-to-end on my workload. I would recommend it without caveats.
4Works well; minor friction (a corner case I had to work around, a missing feature).
3Mixed. Useful for some inputs, fails on others. Read the review text for the specifics.
2Mostly does not work for my use case. Specific failure modes documented in the review.
1Broken / misrepresented. The K-Score on the card overstates real-world performance.

Abuse mitigation

Reviews are persisted with the reviewer's tenant_id and a timestamp. We do not surface the tenant_id on the public review card (only the rating and text), but every row is auditable internally if a publisher reports a coordinated-attack pattern. A future wave will layer rate-limiting + duplicate-content detection on top; for W737-v1 we ship the raw scaffold so the feedback loop opens immediately.

kolm marketplace review --cid my-clause-extractor --stars 5 --text "Works great on contract addenda."

Transfer learning — buyers fine-tune on their own captures

The W737-4 spec ("Fine-tune on their own captures (transfer learning)") rides on the W715 namespace-fingerprint primitive. When a buyer downloads your .kolm, they can layer their own captures on top by pinning the buyer's namespace as a fingerprint and re-running the distill loop against the merged corpus.

The artifact you publish ships its training corpus signature in the manifest. A buyer's kolm distill --base <cid> --namespace <ns> reads that signature, pulls the buyer's captures from the namespace, and re-runs the distill stage starting from your model's weights. The resulting child artifact is content-addressed under its own cid and never overwrites yours.

Privacy: the buyer's captures never reach you. Transfer learning is a one-way pull on the buyer's side — you see only the public download counter, not the buyer's data.
