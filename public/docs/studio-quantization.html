Studio / Quantization

The full quant ladder. Pick by bits and target.

Quantization is the act of compressing weights from fp16 (16 bits per parameter) to fewer bits without losing too much quality. kolm supports the eight production-grade formats in use today — from GGUF Q2_K (~2.6 bpw) up to FP8 (8 bpw) — plus the bleeding-edge NVFP4 for Blackwell. Every quantize run measures the quality delta vs fp16 baseline on the eval set and records it in the artifact passport.

How to pick bits

  1. Calculate your VRAM budget — kolm hardware fit qwen-7b --vram 24 tells you what fits.
  2. Pick the largest model that fits at Q4_K_M or better — bigger model at 4 bits beats smaller model at 8 bits, almost always.
  3. Round up if you have headroom — Q5_K_M over Q4_K_M is cheap insurance.
  4. Read your own quality-delta number — the passport shows what your distilled student lost in this specific quant. If ΔK-Score is >3, step up a bit.

imatrix — calibration for IQ-quants

The IQ-quants (IQ2_S, IQ3_S, IQ4_XS, IQ4_NL) require an importance matrix computed from a representative calibration set. kolm uses your eval holdout by default, padded with the FineWeb-Edu sample if the holdout is small. The imatrix file is regenerated automatically when the compile target is an IQ-quant and no fresh .imatrix exists in the run dir.

# Manual imatrix generation
kolm compile artifact.kolm --target gguf-iq4nl --imatrix-calibration my-eval.jsonl --output q4.gguf

# Reuse an existing imatrix
kolm compile artifact.kolm --target gguf-iq3s --imatrix runs/last/calibration.imatrix --output q3.gguf

Mixed-precision policy

For MoE models (see studio-moe), kolm applies a different quant policy per layer class: routers + embeddings stay at Q6_K or higher; experts go to the user-requested bpw. This avoids the ≈5-point K-Score cliff that uniform quantization causes on MoE.

# MoE-aware quant: experts Q4_K_M, routers Q6_K
kolm compile mixtral-distill.kolm --target gguf-q4km --moe-policy router-high --output mixtral-distill-q4km.gguf
Caveats. Delta-K-Score numbers above are typical for instruction-tuned 7B models on general eval sets. Your specific distill may behave differently — always read the passport's quality_delta_vs_fp16 field after compile and treat the table as a planning estimate, not a guarantee.
