How a row reads

Each vendor row is resolved from its live Trust link: readiness is the signed percentage over assessed controls only (for example 86% means 86% of the controls in scope passed, not 86% of every possible control), grade is the evidence tier (how the proof was obtained)The evidence tier says how the finding was proven: A captured from live logs, B hash-verified against an artifact, C asserted by the vendor. A is the strongest., freshness states when the report last re-attested, and delta is the movement since the prior signed report (for example +4 pts means readiness rose four points since you last looked). A vendor older than its cadence shows a STALE marker; a lapsed subscription shows LAPSED while staying verifiable. kolm maps findings to standards; it does not certify a vendor.

strongestGrade A · captureThe finding was read straight from the vendor's live logs. Nothing was taken on assertion.
verifiedGrade B · hashThe finding is backed by an artifact whose hash the report verifies, so the bytes are pinned.
weakestGrade C · assertedThe vendor stated it and signed the statement. Signed, but not independently captured.
