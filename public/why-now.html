why now

The lines crossed at the same time.

An AI compiler became urgent in 2026 because the gap between frontier and open models closed, regulators started asking for provenance, and the per-token bill broke unit economics. kolm is what happens when those problems are the same problem.

# compile the cheapest current frontier into a student you own
$ kolm compile spec.json --teacher claude-opus-4-7 --base qwen2.5-7b
# → signed .kolm, K-score gate passed, $0 per-token from now on

The forces that converged

01

Frontier capability commoditized.

Through 2025, the gap between top frontier models (GPT-4 / Claude / Gemini / Mistral / Hermes) collapsed to within a few points on most benchmarks that matter. Open-weight 70B-class models reached parity with hosted frontier of 18 months prior. "Use the best frontier" stopped being a strategy. Picking the right teacher is now a routing decision, not an exclusive purchase.

02

Per-token economics broke.

For agent workloads, code review, document classification, content generation at scale, $5 to $15 per million tokens compounds into five- and six-figure monthly invoices. Teams that started in 2023 with frontier-everything are migrating tier-by-tier to local. The compile step is that migration: pay the frontier once, run the student forever.

03

Sovereignty got regulated.

EU AI Act enforcement starts in 2026 for general-purpose AI providers and 2027 for high-risk use. Article 50 demands provenance for AI-generated content. NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001, and the FTC's deceptive-pattern rulemaking all converge on the same requirement: you must be able to prove what your AI did. A signed receipt chain over a local artifact is the cleanest answer.

The compounding pressure

Each force alone justifies a niche product. Together they justify a primitive. A team that adopted a frontier API for a real workload in 2024 hits all three within 12 months: the model that was special is now one of many equivalent options; the bill that was small is now hard to justify; the auditor is now asking how the AI made that decision.

The compile step answers all of them in one operation. Compile takes whichever frontier is cheapest this quarter and uses it as a teacher. Compile turns the per-token bill into a one-time charge. Compile emits a signed manifest the auditor can verify.

"We moved high-volume tasks from frontier APIs to local-first inference not because frontier got worse, but because $40k/mo for token spend wasn't justifiable when an 8B open-weight model fine-tuned on our data hit 95% of the same quality." common pattern, 2025–2026

Why "now" and not 2024

For the compile model to work, certain preconditions had to be true. They all became true within an 18-month window:

A

Open-weight bases got good enough.

Qwen2.5, Llama-3, Hermes-3, Phi-3 (all released within an 18-month window) cleared the threshold where a LoRA-distilled student of a frontier teacher actually wins on the user's task. Pre-2024, the floor was too low. Compile would have produced a sub-quality artifact.

B

Phones became real inference targets.

iOS Neural Engine and Android NPUs in 2025 silicon (Apple A18, Snapdragon 8 Gen 4, Tensor G4) cleared the bar where a 3B-class INT4 model runs at usable speed for agentic workloads, not just chat. The deployment surface "compile-and-run-anywhere" needs now exists.

C

Cryptographic provenance reached general expectation.

Code-signing in CI/CD (Sigstore, SLSA), supply-chain attestation (in-toto), reproducible builds: every adjacent area of software shipped this in 2023–2025. The pattern is familiar enough that demanding the same for AI outputs is a question of when, not whether.

What this means for you

If you operate a real LLM workload today, you will hit at least one of these forces in the next 12 months. The earliest movers are already migrating. The compile step is the route they take.

# the migration, in one command
kolm compile "$YOUR_TASK" \
 --examples ./examples.jsonl \
 --teacher anthropic/claude-opus-4-7 \
 --base qwen2.5-7b

# what you get back:
# - .kolm artifact
# - K-score over your held-out set
# - Ed25519 signature + per-call _kolm metadata
# - $0 per-token from this point on

The window

Frontier capability is going to keep commoditizing. Token bills are going to keep growing as agentic patterns scale. Regulators are not going to relax. Whatever the answer to "AI ownership at scale" looks like, this is the year it stops being theoretical.
