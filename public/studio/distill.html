Studio · distill wizard

Distill a teacher into a student in four steps.

Pick a teacher council — one model or a mix of frontier APIs. Pick a base student to train against. Pick a recipe (support, code, extraction, legal…). Confirm the preview, kick the run. Same backend as kolm distill; this is the browser studio.

Which teacher(s)?

A council of multiple teachers reduces single-model bias. Each teacher generates labels on your capture set; the student trains on the consensus. Pick one to start; mix later. Frontier API costs are paid by the keys you provide via /account/api-keys.

Tip: a 3-teacher council (e.g. Claude Opus + GPT-4o + DeepSeek V3) plus Gemini as judge is the Trinity recipe — see teacher council docs.

Which base student?

The student is what gets trained. Smaller = faster, cheaper, lower ceiling. Default to Qwen3-8B if unsure — it is the 2026 baseline. See the Studio matrix for VRAM and INT4 sizes.

Which recipe?

Recipes bundle the task framing, loss function, LoRA targets, and K-Score floor. Pick the closest fit; full catalog at /studio/recipes. Custom recipes live as spec.toml in your repo — see spec.toml docs.

Review the preview, then confirm.

Preview reads from your captures and estimates the cost based on token counts and teacher pricing. The number is conservative. Once you confirm, the run lands in /studio/distill-status.
