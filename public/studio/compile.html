Studio · compile wizard

Compile a .kolm in four steps.

Pick a namespace from your captures. Pick where the work runs — your local GPU, or a cloud partner if you don’t have one. Pick a base model. Get a quote. Confirm. Same compiler that powers the CLI; this is the browser compiler.

Which namespace?

A namespace is the bucket your captured calls land in. The wizard will pull rows from this namespace and use them to train the student model. If you haven’t captured any calls yet, follow the 5-minute quickstart first.

No captures yet? Start with the quickstart or browse what you already have.

Where should the compile run?

No local GPU? Pick a cloud partner — we run it on your behalf, you pay only for the seconds you use, the partner tears down the rental when the job finishes. Local is free if you already have hardware.

No card, no install? Open the Colab notebook — same flow, runs on Colab's free T4 GPU. Good for 0.5B–7B models.

Which base model?

The student model is what gets trained. Smaller = faster + cheaper but lower ceiling. The proven matrix in For teams shows the sweet spots. Default to Qwen2.5-3B if unsure — it covers most tasks at 1.9 GB INT4.

Who this is for

Persona A · solo + GPU
Pick Local GPU. The same flow runs kolm compile against your CUDA / MPS / ROCm card. Quote is $0.
Persona B · no GPU
Pick Modal or RunPod. We rent the GPU on your behalf, train, return the .kolm, tear down. You see the cost before you spend anything.
Persona C · team
Use this once per namespace from your team workspace; the resulting artifact lands in your shared registry at /account/artifacts.
Persona D · enterprise
Use the same wizard inside your BYOC deployment — quote + run hits your in-VPC kolm instance and your own GPU pool.
Persona E · free tier
Stay under 50k gateway calls and use a free Colab GPU via the Colab notebook.
Persona F · ML engineer
Skip the wizard — kolm compile --spec spec.json --cloud modal --confirm from your terminal is the same path.
