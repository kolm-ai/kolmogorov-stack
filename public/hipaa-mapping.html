Per-control mapping for 164.308 / .310 / .312 / .314 / .316

The kolm side of the HIPAA Security Rule, control by control. Administrative safeguards, physical safeguards, technical safeguards, organizational requirements, and policies & procedures. Direct about where the control is the customer's responsibility (most physical safeguards), where it is joint, and where kolm carries it on the customer's behalf. A compliance reviewer can read this alongside our SOC 2 report and the BAA Schedule 1.

How to read this page

Each row names the regulatory citation, the standard or implementation specification, who carries it, and how kolm helps in the default deployment. We use three responsibility tags so a reviewer can scan a section without misreading the boundary.

  • kolm carries it

    responsibility · kolm-side

    Implemented inside the hosted control plane or the CLI itself. Evidence lives in our SOC 2 report and is referenced in the BAA Schedule 1.

  • Joint with the customer

    responsibility · joint

    kolm provides primitives (signed receipts, retention controls, audit-log emit). The customer configures, operates, and reviews. Both sides leave evidence.

  • Customer responsibility

    responsibility · customer

    Outside the kolm boundary. Most physical safeguards, the customer's identity provider, OS-level disk encryption, facility access. We will not pretend we cover what we do not.

Administrative safeguards 45 CFR 164.308

The administrative side of the Security Rule. The biggest section, and the one where joint responsibility shows up most often: kolm operates the workforce, training, and incident response on its side of the boundary; the customer does the same on theirs.

CitationStandardWhoHow kolm helps in the default deployment
164.308(a)(1)(i) Security Management Process kolm Risk analysis and risk management run quarterly across the hosted control plane and the CLI release pipeline. Results feed the SOC 2 system description and the engineering roadmap.
164.308(a)(1)(ii)(A) Risk Analysis joint kolm publishes the threat model at /security#threat. The customer extends the risk analysis to their VPC deployment, hardware footprint, and identity provider.
164.308(a)(1)(ii)(B) Risk Management joint kolm-side mitigations land in the release pipeline (CVE patch SLAs, dependency review). Customer-side mitigations land in the customer's runbook (network egress allowlist, key custody, OS encryption).
164.308(a)(1)(ii)(C) Sanction Policy kolm Workforce sanction policy in place for the kolm team; documented in the SOC 2 system description. Customer applies their own policy to their workforce.
164.308(a)(1)(ii)(D) Information System Activity Review joint The CLI emits structured audit logs to ~/.kolm/audit.log; the hosted control plane writes to an immutable audit store. Customer ships the CLI log into their SIEM and reviews on their cadence.
164.308(a)(2) Assigned Security Responsibility kolm A named security officer accountable for the kolm side; identified to the customer on BAA execution. Customer designates their own security officer per the rule.
164.308(a)(3)(i) Workforce Security kolm Background checks, role-based access, quarterly access review for the kolm workforce. Documented in SOC 2 control descriptions.
164.308(a)(3)(ii)(A) Authorization and Supervision kolm Least-privilege access to the hosted control plane; production access logged to an immutable audit store. Reviewed quarterly by the security officer.
164.308(a)(3)(ii)(B) Workforce Clearance Procedure kolm Documented clearance procedure as part of onboarding; references in SOC 2 system description.
164.308(a)(3)(ii)(C) Termination Procedures kolm Same-day access revocation across SSO, signing keys, and production systems. Tombstone audit entry retained.
164.308(a)(4)(i) Information Access Management joint Tenant- and namespace-scoped access on the kolm side. Customer manages user provisioning into kolm via SSO and SCIM.
164.308(a)(4)(ii)(A) Isolating Healthcare Clearinghouse Functions customer Not applicable to kolm. If the customer operates a clearinghouse, the customer applies isolation in their environment.
164.308(a)(4)(ii)(B) Access Authorization joint Customer grants kolm access to a customer-controlled IdP. kolm consumes signed assertions; kolm does not provision PHI access on the customer's behalf.
164.308(a)(4)(ii)(C) Access Establishment and Modification joint SSO and SCIM on the kolm side flow user lifecycle events from the customer IdP. Customer drives provisioning and de-provisioning; kolm honors the assertions.
164.308(a)(5)(i) Security Awareness and Training kolm Annual security training for the kolm workforce, with tracked attestation. Customer trains their own workforce per the rule.
164.308(a)(5)(ii)(A) Security Reminders kolm Quarterly security reminders to the kolm workforce; topics calibrated to recent incidents and threat-intel updates.
164.308(a)(5)(ii)(B) Protection from Malicious Software joint kolm releases are signed; SBOM published; reproducible build script in repo. Customer runs the CLI on hardware they manage; OS-level AV / EDR is the customer's choice.
164.308(a)(5)(ii)(C) Log-in Monitoring joint Hosted control-plane sign-ins logged; anomaly alerts on the kolm side. Customer monitors their own SSO and IdP for the same.
164.308(a)(5)(ii)(D) Password Management joint MFA enforced on the kolm side; signing keys generated locally and rotatable with kolm keys rotate. Customer enforces password and MFA on their IdP.
164.308(a)(6)(i) Security Incident Procedures joint kolm-side incident response runbook with on-call rotation. Suspected breach on kolm side reported to affected customers within 30 days per BAA. Customer runs their own IR on their side.
164.308(a)(6)(ii) Response and Reporting kolm Post-incident reviews shared with affected enterprise customers within 14 days. Breach notice template available; coordinated with the covered entity's notice obligations.
164.308(a)(7)(i) Contingency Plan joint Disaster recovery for the hosted control plane operated by kolm. Customer's compile workloads run on customer hardware; the customer's BCP covers that path.
164.308(a)(7)(ii)(A) Data Backup Plan customer Captures and weights live on customer hardware in the default deployment. Customer backs them up to their storage tier. Receipts can be re-fetched from the registry.
164.308(a)(7)(ii)(B) Disaster Recovery Plan joint kolm DR for hosted services; customer DR for their compile environment. Receipts are recoverable from the public registry by CID for as long as the customer publishes them.
164.308(a)(7)(ii)(C) Emergency Mode Operation Plan customer For compile workloads in the customer environment. The CLI runs air-gapped if needed; the verifier is a static binary and works without network.
164.308(a)(7)(ii)(D) Testing and Revision Procedures joint kolm runs DR exercises annually for hosted services. Customer tests their own BCP for the compile environment on their cadence.
164.308(a)(7)(ii)(E) Applications and Data Criticality Analysis customer The customer classifies their own applications and data. kolm documents the criticality of the hosted control plane in our internal continuity plan.
164.308(a)(8) Evaluation kolm Periodic technical and non-technical evaluation, with SOC 2 Type II as the recurring evidence. Bridge letters available between observation windows.
164.308(b)(1) Business Associate Contracts kolm BAA available at /baa. Flow-down to kolm subprocessors documented at /soc2#subprocessors.

Physical safeguards 45 CFR 164.310

Most physical safeguards are the customer's responsibility because PHI lives on customer hardware in the default deployment. kolm-side facilities (hosted control plane) inherit the hyperscaler physical controls of AWS and GCP, documented in their SOC 2 reports.

CitationStandardWhoHow kolm helps in the default deployment
164.310(a)(1) Facility Access Controls customer For the compile environment, the customer's facility access controls apply. kolm-side hosted control plane inherits hyperscaler physical controls (AWS, GCP) documented in their SOC 2 reports.
164.310(a)(2)(i) Contingency Operations customer Customer maintains facility access procedures for emergency restoration. kolm-side equivalents are handled by the hosting subprocessor.
164.310(a)(2)(ii) Facility Security Plan customer Customer-side facility security plan applies to their compile environment. kolm has no physical facility holding PHI.
164.310(a)(2)(iii) Access Control and Validation Procedures customer Customer applies visitor and validation procedures at their facility.
164.310(a)(2)(iv) Maintenance Records customer Customer keeps maintenance records for their compile-environment hardware.
164.310(b) Workstation Use customer Workstation-use policy is the customer's. The CLI runs on whatever workstations the customer authorizes.
164.310(c) Workstation Security customer OS-level encryption (LUKS, BitLocker, FileVault), MDM, and physical security on workstations are customer-side.
164.310(d)(1) Device and Media Controls joint Customer applies device and media policies to compile-environment hardware. kolm publishes guidance on artifact disposal and key destruction at /security#retention.
164.310(d)(2)(i) Disposal joint Customer disposes of media on their side. On the kolm side, hosted-side disposal follows the hyperscaler's verified disposal procedures.
164.310(d)(2)(ii) Media Re-use customer Customer applies media-reuse procedures. kolm does not transfer customer media.
164.310(d)(2)(iii) Accountability joint Customer tracks media movement on their side. kolm signs artifacts so movement of a .kolm file across systems remains verifiable by CID.
164.310(d)(2)(iv) Data Backup and Storage customer Customer backups remain in the customer's storage tier. Receipts can be re-fetched from the registry by CID if the customer published them.

Technical safeguards 45 CFR 164.312

The technical controls map most directly to kolm primitives. Access controls live in the hosted control plane and the CLI; integrity is delivered by the signed receipt chain; transmission security is TLS plus the offline-verifiable artifact.

CitationStandardWhoHow kolm helps in the default deployment
164.312(a)(1) Access Control joint Tenant- and namespace-scoped access on the kolm side; per-tenant signing keys. Customer manages user-level access via SSO / SCIM and OS-level controls.
164.312(a)(2)(i) Unique User Identification joint Hosted control-plane users have unique IDs. Customer's IdP enforces uniqueness on the customer side; kolm honors the assertion.
164.312(a)(2)(ii) Emergency Access Procedure customer Customer's break-glass procedure applies to the compile environment. The CLI is a static binary and runs from any authorized workstation.
164.312(a)(2)(iii) Automatic Logoff joint Hosted control-plane sessions time out per policy; the CLI is process-scoped. OS-level idle locks are customer-side.
164.312(a)(2)(iv) Encryption and Decryption joint Hosted control plane encrypts at rest (AES-256). The customer applies disk encryption (LUKS, BitLocker, FileVault) to the compile environment. Signing keys live at ~/.kolm/keys or in the customer's KMS.
164.312(b) Audit Controls kolm The CLI emits structured audit logs to ~/.kolm/audit.log; the hosted control plane writes to an immutable audit store. Receipts are the durable record of compile events.
164.312(c)(1) Integrity kolm Every .kolm artifact carries an Ed25519 signature over a content-addressed receipt naming the teacher, base model, eval pack, and K-score. Tamper of weights or scores invalidates the signature. Verifier is a static binary and works offline.
164.312(c)(2) Mechanism to Authenticate Electronic PHI kolm The receipt CID is content-addressed; recomputing the hash of the artifact and comparing to the receipt reveals any tamper. Public verify endpoint at /verify-prod.
164.312(d) Person or Entity Authentication joint Hosted control plane authenticates via SSO / API key; CLI authenticates the operator via the local signing keypair. Customer authenticates their workforce via their IdP.
164.312(e)(1) Transmission Security joint All kolm endpoints are TLS-only. Customer manages internal network controls in the compile environment. The artifact itself is independently verifiable in flight (signed bytes), not only in transit.
164.312(e)(2)(i) Integrity Controls (in transit) kolm Signed receipt binds the artifact hash; any mutation in transit fails verification regardless of TLS termination.
164.312(e)(2)(ii) Encryption (in transit) joint TLS 1.2+ on all kolm endpoints. Customer enforces TLS on their internal compile-environment hops.

Organizational requirements 45 CFR 164.314

The contract-level controls: the BAA itself, subcontractor flow-down, and the rules for group health plans. The default deployment puts most of this in the BAA at /baa.

CitationStandardWhoHow kolm helps in the default deployment
164.314(a)(1) Business Associate Contracts kolm BAA executed before PHI flows. Template at /baa. Redlines accepted within standard markup; counter-signature within two business days.
164.314(a)(2)(i) Business Associate Contracts: Required Terms kolm Required terms (permitted uses, safeguards, reporting, subcontractor flow-down, access, amendment, accounting, return or destroy) all carried in the kolm BAA template. Itemized at /baa#terms.
164.314(a)(2)(ii) Other Arrangements joint For government customers and federal pilots where the BAA is replaced or supplemented by an interagency agreement, kolm executes the equivalent terms in the alternate instrument.
164.314(a)(2)(iii) Subcontractor Flow-Down kolm Subprocessors that touch PHI on the kolm side execute a written agreement with terms substantially similar to the kolm BAA. List at /soc2#subprocessors.
164.314(b)(1) Requirements for Group Health Plans customer Where applicable to the customer's plan documents. kolm is not a group health plan and does not act as a plan sponsor.
164.314(b)(2)(i) Plan Document Amendment (safeguards) customer Customer plan documents reference administrative, physical, and technical safeguards. kolm's role is referenced via the BAA, not via plan documents.
164.314(b)(2)(ii) Plan Document Amendment (workforce) customer Customer plan documents describe workforce access. kolm does not workforce-access PHI on the plan's behalf except per the BAA.
164.314(b)(2)(iii) Plan Document Amendment (security incident) joint Customer plan documents reference security-incident reporting. kolm reports kolm-side incidents within 30 days per the BAA; customer reports under the plan's own obligations.

Policies, procedures, and documentation 45 CFR 164.316

The recordkeeping requirements. kolm maintains and retains documentation of the controls covered by SOC 2 for the period the rule requires; the same documents are referenced in the BAA Schedule 1.

CitationStandardWhoHow kolm helps in the default deployment
164.316(a) Policies and Procedures kolm Documented policies and procedures for each in-scope control. Reviewed annually; change history retained.
164.316(b)(1)(i) Documentation (form) kolm Policies maintained in writing; electronic copies provided on request as part of audit support.
164.316(b)(1)(ii) Documentation (action records) kolm Action records for assessments, evaluations, and incidents retained per policy. Receipts themselves are durable, content-addressed records of compile events.
164.316(b)(2)(i) Time Limit (retention) kolm Documentation retained for 6 years from creation or the date last in effect, whichever is later, per the rule.
164.316(b)(2)(ii) Availability kolm Documentation available to those responsible for implementing the procedures, and to auditors as part of the SOC 2 engagement.
164.316(b)(2)(iii) Updates kolm Documentation reviewed periodically and updated as needed in response to environmental or operational changes.
