Health plans get audited differently from health systems. The map below covers what your compliance team will actually be asked.

CMS-4201-F
The 2024 Final Rule on prior authorization: kolm's PA workflow produces the structured response, the API-friendly format, and the per-criterion timestamp that the Final Rule requires for the 7-day standard and 72-hour expedited windows.
HIPAA 164.312(b)
Audit controls: every decision emits a structured audit record with the receipt CID, the artifact CID, the input hash, and the human reviewer's ID. Streams to your SIEM in JSON or CEF.
NAIC AI Bulletin

Honest limits

The eight things we tell every health-plan buyer up front.

No magic. No "AI-driven coverage decisions." Read this before the contracts conversation.

Limit
Kolm does not make coverage decisions

The distilled artifact drafts a recommended action with citations. A trained reviewer signs the decision. We will not sign a contract that has kolm sitting in the decision seat for an adverse benefit determination.

Limit
The model will be wrong on outliers

Distilled K-score is within 3 points of teacher after quantization. Your hardest 5% of cases are the ones the model is most likely to miss. The defer-on-low-confidence path is on by default; we do not let you disable it without a written explanation.

Limit
Distillation requires approved historical decisions

You cannot distill a behavior you have not yet performed. For new product lines or new regulatory regimes you need a few months of human-labeled adjudication before kolm has training data to distill from.

Limit
State-by-state variance is on you

Insurance law varies by state and by line of business. The artifact you ship in Massachusetts is not the artifact you ship in Texas; we make the rebuild fast (under an hour) but the regulatory mapping is your job.

Yes
We sign a BAA before any data touches our infrastructure

For SaaS-hosted: BAA, then access. For BYOC and on-prem: BAA, then we ship you the bundle and never see your data. No exceptions for pilots.

Yes
Bias test on every build

Every artifact build runs the bias-test harness against your protected-class slices. The report ships with the artifact. Disparate-impact thresholds are configurable; defaults are conservative.

Yes
Right-to-explanation

Every decision the artifact recommends carries the citations that supported it: contract section, benefit row, medical-necessity criterion, member-specific data points. Use the same chain for the appeal record.

Yes
The receipts are useful in deposition

The artifact CID + the per-decision receipt is a tamper-evident chain you can pull years later. Your defense counsel will not have to take our word that the model behaved as documented.

Reference architectures

Reference architectures that pass payor security review.

Each one comes with a Terraform module, a network diagram, a HIPAA control map, and a written deviation log. Pick whichever matches your existing posture.

Pattern A

VPC-resident, single tenant

Kolm control plane and data plane both deploy into a private VPC inside your AWS / Azure / GCP account. Egress to kolm.ai is signed-receipt telemetry only; PHI never crosses the VPC boundary. Best fit for regional Blues and large IDN payors.

Boundary: your VPC
Keys: your KMS
Egress: attestations + metrics
Pattern B

On-prem with DMZ broker

Kolm runs entirely on-prem in your member-data DC. A read-only broker in the DMZ proxies the artifact registry and the verification endpoint. The broker holds no PHI, only signed CIDs. Best fit for payors with existing on-prem clinical data lakes.

Boundary: your DC
Keys: your HSM
Egress: none from data plane
Pattern C

FedRAMP / GovCloud

For Medicaid TPAs, Medicare Advantage plans, and state-program intermediaries that need FedRAMP Moderate. Deploys in AWS GovCloud or Azure Government. The same artifact, the same signed-receipts contract, just under the FedRAMP boundary.

Boundary: GovCloud
Keys: CMK in FIPS 140-2
Egress: FedRAMP-eligible only
Pattern D

TPA-of-TPAs, multi-payor

For third-party administrators that adjudicate for many self-funded employers. Each employer gets its own tenant, its own KMS key, its own artifact registry. Cross-tenant peeks are physically prevented at the storage layer, not just at the API.

Boundary: per-employer tenant
Keys: per-employer KMS
Egress: per-tenant scoped
Operational footprint

What it costs to run, what it costs to operate.

Honest numbers from production deployments. Your mileage will vary; these are the ranges we have actually observed.

Hardware
A single L40S or H100 node sustains 12-18M claims per day at 71% auto-route rates. Mid-sized payors typically run two nodes (one primary, one warm standby) per region. Per-decision compute cost lands at $0.0007 to $0.0021 at production volume.
Latency
P50 decision latency 180-420ms. P99 720ms. Cold-start of a new artifact under 4s. Real-time endpoints honor a 1.5s SLO; batch lanes are throughput-optimized at 800-1400 decisions per second per node.
Operations
One platform engineer can run a kolm deployment for a single-plan payor at 60-80% of one FTE. For TPAs running per-employer tenants, expect a small platform team (2-4 engineers) responsible for tenant lifecycle, evals, and drift response.
Failure modes
If the artifact returns no_match or low_confidence, the request falls back to the existing human queue. Kolm cannot increase your error rate; the worst case is parity with your current operation. We document this in writing.
Drift response
Every artifact ships with a K-score drift baseline. The runtime emits a drift alert when production K diverges by 3 points or more. The standard response is a re-distill from the most recent 90 days of approved cases. Cycle time: 6-18 hours.
Audit cadence
Quarterly evals against your own benchmark set are recommended. Annual external audit using the same artifact CIDs you ran in production. Auditors get a read-only registry account; they pull receipts directly.
Procurement path

How a payor actually buys this.

The path from first call to production for a regulated payor. We have done this; the order matters.

  1. Step 1
    Discovery + use-case ranking (2 weeks)

    30-minute calls with your claims, UM, SIU, and security leads. We rank candidate workflows by signal density, regulatory risk, and time-to-value. Output: a written ranked use-case memo.

  2. Step 2
    BAA + security review (3-6 weeks)

    Standard payor BAA; we accept yours as the base, redline known items. Security review includes the SOC 2 report, HIPAA Security Rule mapping, FedRAMP attestation where applicable, and a tenant-isolation walkthrough.

  3. Step 3
    Pilot in your VPC (30 days)

    Kolm installs into a sandbox VPC. We distill an artifact from a 50K-case sample of your approved decisions. Day-30 scorecard: K-score against your held-out set, FPR/FNR on your priority slices, latency, deviation log.

  4. Step 4
    Production cutover (4-8 weeks)

    Shadow mode first: kolm decides in parallel with your existing process; no decision reaches a member. After two weeks of clean agreement, switch to assist mode (human signs every decision). After two weeks of that, the auto-route bands turn on at agreed confidence thresholds.

  5. Ongoing
    Quarterly evals + drift response (continuous)

    Quarterly K-score evals, drift alerts in the platform, written change-log for every artifact rebuild. Your compliance team has a read-only seat in the registry and can pull a receipt for any decision, any time, for the artifact retention window.
