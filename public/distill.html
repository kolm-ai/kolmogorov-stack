Distillation

In kolm.ai, distillation compresses captured prompt and response pairs into a smaller specialist model, frozen at a K-score threshold. It is one pass inside the broader compile pipeline, not a synonym for it. This page explains what it is, when to reach for it, what the 7-step pipeline does, and the one command that runs it end to end.

What it is

Distillation in kolm.ai is the act of compressing a population of captured prompt and response pairs from a larger upstream model into a smaller specialist model that produces matching outputs on the prompts you actually see. The smaller model is "frozen" at the point where its K-score on a held-out slice of your captures crosses the configured threshold (default 0.85). Until that threshold is met, the artifact is not promotable; once met, it ships as a signed .kolm with a receipt chain back to the original captures.

Distillation is not the same as fine-tuning. Fine-tuning re-weights an existing base model on new labels and keeps the same architecture and parameter count. Distillation is also not the same as compilation. Compilation is the umbrella verb that includes distillation as one of its passes, alongside quantization, gating, signing, and packaging. A kolm compile run on a 1000-pair namespace will typically include a distillation pass; a compile on a 50-pair recipe-template namespace typically will not.

When to distill (vs. just compile a recipe)

  • You have at least 1,000 captured pairs in one namespace. Below that floor, a recipe template is cheaper, ships faster, and holds about as well. The threshold is a heuristic, not a wall; adjust with --min-pairs if your prompts are unusually narrow.
  • The upstream model's cost or latency exceeds the K-score gain you actually need. If you are paying gpt-4o-class rates for a job a 7B specialist can match within 2 K-score points, distillation pays back inside a week of traffic.
  • You need to ship to a smaller device. A 3090, an M3 Ultra, an NVIDIA Spark, or an air-gapped server cannot host a frontier upstream; a distilled specialist fits in 8 to 24 GB of VRAM.
  • The prompts are repetitive enough that a specialist will hold. The mining step in the pipeline groups captures by template hash; if the largest cluster is under 200 rows, the specialist generalizes worse than the upstream and the K-score gate will not let it ship.

The 7-step pipeline

1. MiningCluster captures by template hash, pick the dominant cluster as the training spine.
2. AugmentGenerate paraphrases and edge cases to widen the cluster without changing its semantics.
3. Active selectDown-sample to the rows that best cover the cluster's decision surface.
4. SanitizeStrip PHI, secrets, and other class-banned tokens via the configured redactor.
5. SeedsMaterialize the sanitized rows as a deterministic seeds.jsonl.
6. TrainRun the trainer bridge (LoRA or full fine-tune) against the base model on the seeds.
7. ScoreEvaluate the trained specialist on a frozen holdout. Ship only if K-score crosses the threshold.

See /docs/distill for the per-step CLI flags, the trainer-bridge contract, and the failure modes for each step. The seeds format is documented at /docs/datasets; the K-score gate at /k-score.

One command

Distillation does not require seven separate CLI invocations. One verb walks every step and ships the artifact if the gate clears.

kolm distill --from-captures <namespace> --min-pairs 1000

The verb reads captures from the named namespace, runs the 7-step pipeline above, and writes the resulting artifact under ~/.kolm/artifacts/ with its receipt chain. Below the --min-pairs floor (default 1000) the verb refuses with an exit code of 2, so you cannot accidentally distill on a thin namespace.

To preview without writing anything, use the dry-run flag.

kolm distill --preview <namespace>

The preview flag prints the cluster size, the template hash, the projected base model, the projected holdout K-score, and the estimated training time, then exits 0 without invoking the trainer. Use it to size a job before paying for the GPU hours.

Outputs

A successful distill run produces four artifacts. Each links to the spec or surface where it is documented.

OutputWhat it isWhere documented
<your-recipe>.kolmThe signed artifact. Single file, runnable on the target device with kolm run.RS-1 spec
seeds.jsonlThe deterministic seed dataset the trainer saw. Re-runnable; hash-pinned in the receipt./captures
Receipt chainEvery step (mine, augment, sanitize, train, score) writes a receipt. Walking the chain from the artifact gets you back to the source captures.RS-1 spec
K-score holdout reportA JSON report of the artifact's K-score on the frozen holdout, broken out per-axis (quality, latency, cost, safety, drift)./k-score
The receipt chain is the audit trail the compile pipeline gives you. If you ship a distilled artifact and the model later misbehaves, the chain points at the exact training rows, the exact upstream model, and the exact K-score gate that let it through. See /security for the verification path.
