Your hardware.

Every .kolm is one base model bound to one device. Pick from the frontier 2026 catalog or pin a stable default. Override either at the CLI; both ride the receipt chain.

Hardware tiers.

Pass kolm compile --tier=<slug> and the compiler picks the matching base + quant + context. kolm doctor --detect-hw reads nvidia-smi or system_profiler and recommends one.

  • 3090

    Single RTX 3090, 24 GB VRAM, consumer ceiling for Q4 27B-class.

  • 5090

    Single RTX 5090, 32 GB VRAM, fits 36B Q4 with comfortable context.

  • a100-80

    A100-80GB, datacenter workhorse.

  • h100-80

    H100-80GB, datacenter standard 2024-2026.

  • h200-141

    H200-141GB, 235B-A22B Q4 with room to spare.

  • dgx-spark

    DGX Spark workstation, 128 GB unified.

  • m3-ultra-512

    Apple M3 Ultra, 512 GB unified, runs Q2 1T-class.

  • cpu-server

    x86_64 fallback, edge models only.

Pre-cached weights.

The CLI ships a manifest of HuggingFace GGUF repos for every catalog row. Run kolm models prefetch --tier=edge once and the small models live on disk; kolm models pull <id> grabs one on demand. Served at /v1/models/manifest; a 302 redirect to the file lives at /v1/models/pull?id=&variant=.
