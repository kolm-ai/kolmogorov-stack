Regional and PPP-adjusted pricing.

A single global price quietly excludes most of the world. kolm adjusts list prices by local purchasing power, with a separate 50% non-profit row on top. Rates are indicative, refreshed quarterly, and applied at checkout based on a verified billing region.

rodneyyesep@gmail.com -> See standard pricing

Why regional pricing

If kolm Pro costs $49 / month in San Francisco and the same $49 / month in Lagos, the price is silently saying "this product is not for Lagos developers." That is not what we want to ship. The workbench is open-source under Apache-2.0; the hosted control plane is the only thing customers pay for, and there is no reason that fee should be priced for the highest-income market.

The adjustment factors below are derived from World Bank PPP conversion ratios, rounded for stability. They are not promotional discounts -- they are the actual list price in that region. The same kolm Pro in India is $49 * 0.27 = $13.23 / mo, list. No coupons, no expiry.

Indicative, not real-time. Factors are refreshed quarterly, not on every checkout. The number you see at signup is the number you pay for the full billing period -- we will not silently re-tier you mid-quarter. If the World Bank PPP series moves materially against you, we adjust the factor down (never up) at the next quarter boundary.

Non-profit row

Registered non-profits get an additional 50% off all tiers, stackable with the regional factor above. So a recognised 501(c)(3) in India pays $13.23 * 0.5 = $6.62 / mo for Pro, not $24.50.

  • United States: 501(c)(3) IRS determination letter.
  • United Kingdom: HMRC-registered charity number.
  • European Union: equivalent national non-profit status (e.g., e.V. in Germany, ASBL in Belgium).
  • Other regions: equivalent government-issued non-profit registration plus a brief description of the cause.

The non-profit row is also written up on the standalone non-profit pricing page; this row is here so the regional+non-profit math is visible in one place.

Verification process

Region-based discounting is honest only if it is verified. Three checks; none are intrusive.

  1. Billing address. The address on your card or invoice has to match the region you are claiming. We use that as the canonical signal.
  2. Region detection. We cross-check against the IP region at signup and the ASN. A mismatch does not auto-fail -- it just flags the account for the third check.
  3. Appeal. If a mismatch flag fires, you get an email with a one-page form: "where do you live and work, and why does the signal disagree?" A human reads it. We grant most appeals -- the goal is to catch arbitrage by US-based buyers, not to block honest customers travelling or using a VPN.

Anti-arbitrage. If the billing address is United States but the work is being done elsewhere, the United States rate applies. If a tenant is owned by a US-domiciled entity but operating a subsidiary in a PPP-adjusted region, the subsidiary qualifies for the adjusted rate on its own tenant -- not on the parent. We do not police this in real time; we audit it on renewal.

Apply

PPP-adjusted pricing applies automatically at signup once your billing region is verified -- there is nothing to claim if your card billing address is in one of the listed regions. If you are in a region not yet listed or you think your rate is wrong, write in.
