Non-profit pricing.

Not Kolm therapeutics, Kolm band, or other unrelated organizations using similar names.

50% off every kolm tier for registered 501(c)(3) charities and local-equivalent non-profits. Stackable with the regional PPP rate. Yearly re-verification. No "non-profit edition" -- the full workbench, the full registry, the full receipt chain.

rodneyyesep@gmail.com -> See standard pricing

Eligibility

Any organization that is legally constituted as a non-profit and uses kolm in service of its mission, not as a revenue line of an affiliated for-profit.

  • United States: 501(c)(3) IRS determination letter, or 501(c)(4) for civic non-profits operating in the public interest.
  • United Kingdom: HMRC-registered charity or CIO with a charity number.
  • European Union: equivalent national registration (e.V. in Germany, ASBL in Belgium, fondation reconnue d'utilite publique in France, ONLUS in Italy, etc.).
  • Canada: registered charity number on the CRA list.
  • Australia: ACNC-registered charity.
  • Elsewhere: government-issued non-profit registration plus a brief description of the cause. We approve most.

Discount

50% off the list price of every paid tier. Stacks with the PPP regional rate -- the regional factor applies first, then the 50% non-profit cut.

Re-verify yearly

The 50% discount is granted for one year at a time. Two weeks before the anniversary we email a one-question form: "is your non-profit status still active?" One reply, one check, one renewal.

  • If your status lapses, the account converts to the standard tier at the next billing cycle -- never silently.
  • If your status changes (e.g., you spin out a for-profit subsidiary), the for-profit subsidiary is billed at standard rates on its own tenant; the parent non-profit retains the discount on its own tenant.
  • If you forget to reply, we send a second reminder two weeks later before converting to standard rates. We default to the non-profit benefit-of-the-doubt, not against it.

Honest boundary. The non-profit discount applies to mission work, not to commercial side ventures of a non-profit. If your org runs a paid SaaS that happens to be operated by a 501(c)(3), the SaaS tenant pays standard rates. We do not police this in real time; we audit it on the yearly re-verification.
