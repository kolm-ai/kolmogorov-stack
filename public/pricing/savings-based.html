Savings-based pricing.

Charge 10% of documented API-spend reduction. Aligns kolm's success to yours. We only win when your monthly inference bill actually goes down -- measured against your own 30-day pre-kolm baseline, not a vendor estimate.

How it works

Four steps, no surprises. The whole loop is auditable end-to-end -- every saved dollar traces back to a captured request, a routed reply, and a signed receipt.

  1. Baseline. We observe 30 days of your current AI API spend untouched -- same provider, same prompts, same volume. That number becomes the contract baseline. No bill yet.
  2. Deploy .kolm. Once a signed artifact is in the runtime, we route the traffic through it. Per-call savings start landing in the ledger immediately, but billing still has not started.
  3. Measure. A monthly statement compares post-deploy spend to the baseline. We compute the reduction in absolute dollars; you see the same numbers we do, sourced from the same captures.
  4. Bill. Invoice equals 10% of the measured reduction. If the reduction is zero or negative, the invoice is zero. No floor, no minimum, no rollover.

Honest baseline contract

Vendors love savings-based pricing when they can pick the baseline. We do not pick it. The rules:

  • Baseline window is exactly 30 days of pre-kolm spend, measured at the same granularity (per-call cost) you will be measured against post-deploy.
  • Baseline is computed from your own provider invoices plus the gateway capture ledger -- both sides have to agree before the contract clock starts.
  • If you have seasonal traffic, we take the rolling 90-day average and divide by three. You pick which 90 days.
  • If your traffic grows 2x post-deploy, baseline scales with it. The 10% is on the savings-per-unit-of-work, not on absolute dollars -- otherwise we would punish growth.
  • No billing happens until the 30-day baseline window closes and a signed contract is in place. You see every number first.

Honesty contract. We will not bill against a baseline we did not observe directly. If you cannot share a full 30 days of provider invoices and capture-ledger data, savings-based pricing is not available -- fall back to a flat-rate plan. The honesty here is one-way: we are not allowed to estimate your savings, only to measure them.

Audit trail

Every micro-saving is traceable. The savings ledger is built on the same signed-receipt chain that backs kolm verify, so the savings number on your invoice is not a vendor claim -- it is a sum of per-call deltas you can replay.

  • Per-call rows record: request_id, baseline_cost_micro_usd, post_kolm_cost_micro_usd, delta_micro_usd, receipt_cid.
  • You can export the entire month as JSONL, hash it, and reconcile against your provider invoices line by line.
  • Any disputed row can be replayed in capture-replay mode and re-priced -- the ledger is append-only, so corrections show as new rows, never as silent edits.
  • The auditor view is read-only and tenant-fenced. We never see your raw prompts; we only see the cost deltas they produced.

Cap

The savings-based fee is capped: it will never exceed the equivalent Pro flat-rate for the same usage tier. If 10% of your reduction would invoice higher than Pro at the matching call volume, we bill Pro instead and the delta accrues as credit toward the next month.

Cap rationale. If kolm has done such a good job that the savings fee would exceed the flat plan, you should be on the flat plan -- it is cheaper for you and stabler for us. The cap auto-converts you. We tell you on the next invoice, not after the fact.

Enroll
