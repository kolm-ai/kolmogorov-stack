Data Processing Agreement

Effective version: 2026-05. Aligned with GDPR Art. 28 and the EU Standard Contractual Clauses 2021/914.

Plain-language TLDRKolm.ai processes only the data you send through the gateway/capture surfaces. We are a processor; you are the controller. Captures and artifacts stay tenant-isolated. We notify you in writing within 72 hours of any confirmed breach. Sub-processors are listed below and we give 30 days' written notice before adding any new one.

1. Definitions

Read full definitions

Controller, Processor, Data Subject, Personal Data, and Processing have the meanings given in Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR").

Customer Data means all data, including Personal Data, that Customer or its end users submit to the kolm.ai services.

Services means the kolm.ai gateway, capture, distill, compile, registry, and runtime control-plane services made available at kolm.ai.

2. Scope and roles

Customer acts as Controller. Kolm.ai acts as Processor. Kolm.ai will process Customer Data only on Customer's documented instructions, including with regard to transfers, unless required by Union or Member State law.

3. Subject matter and duration

Processing is for the duration of the Master Services Agreement (MSA) or successor contract, plus a wind-down period not exceeding 30 days.

4. Nature and purpose of processing

  • Routing API calls through the gateway to the model the Customer selects.
  • Storing, hashing, and serving captured request/response rows that the Customer explicitly approves.
  • Distilling and compiling artifacts from approved captures.
  • Operating the signed-artifact registry and providing offline verification receipts.

7. Security measures

  • TLS 1.3 in transit; AES-256 at rest for capture rows and artifacts.
  • Tenant isolation: every row is fenced by tenant_id and a defense-in-depth filter is applied at every store boundary.
  • Signed-artifact chain: Ed25519 over manifest.sha256 for every .kolm emitted by the compile surface.
  • Least-privilege RBAC, MFA-required admin access, audited via the AUDIT_OPS pipeline.
  • See /security for the full posture and the threat model.

8. International transfers

Where Personal Data is transferred outside the EEA, Kolm.ai relies on the EU Standard Contractual Clauses 2021/914 Module 2 (Controller-to-Processor). The clauses are incorporated by reference and govern in the event of conflict.

9. Data subject rights

Kolm.ai will assist Customer in responding to data-subject requests (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection) within 10 business days. Use kolm export --tenant <id> or the /v1/data-export route for self-serve exports.

10. Breach notification

Kolm.ai will notify Customer in writing without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware of any confirmed Personal Data breach. Notice will include facts, likely consequences, and remediation steps. Send disclosure reports to security@kolm.ai.

11. Return or deletion

On termination of the MSA, Kolm.ai will, at Customer's option, return or delete all Customer Data (including captures and built artifacts) within 30 days, save for backups that will be purged on the next backup-rotation cycle (which does not exceed 90 days).

12. Audit rights

Customer may audit Kolm.ai's compliance with this DPA once per calendar year, with at least 30 days' written notice, conducted remotely and at Customer's expense. The annual SOC 2 Type I report (and any successor SOC 2 Type II report once issued) satisfies Customer's audit rights when made available under NDA.

13. Liability

The liability of each party under this DPA is subject to the limits set out in the MSA.

14. Governing law

This DPA is governed by the law specified in the MSA. If no choice is made, the law of Delaware, USA applies, without prejudice to mandatory data-protection law of the data subject's home jurisdiction.
