For the developers, engineers, and compliance leads who care · A short manifesto

The model that decides should be the model that gets audited.

We wrote kolm because the gap between "a model that works in a demo" and "a model that you can defend in a deposition" is the entire job, and almost nobody is doing it.

For the past four years, a quiet thing has been true and almost no one has said it out loud. The model behind every consequential AI decision in production today is opaque to the people whose decisions it makes. A radiologist trusts a triage recommendation she cannot inspect. A claims examiner approves a denial whose rationale is a vendor's commercial secret. A bank's compliance team writes "we use AI" in its annual report and hopes the regulator does not ask which one, on which date, with which weights, against which evals.

This is fine until it is not, and then it is the whole story.

Every developer who has tried to ship a serious AI feature into a regulated environment has hit the same wall. The model you call is not the model you can sign. The artifact that runs in production is not the artifact your compliance team reviewed. The eval pack you ran in the demo is not the eval pack the regulator will ask about. The chain of custody from your captures, through your teacher, into your distilled student, through your runtime, into your decision, is a chain of trust nobody is keeping.

Kolm exists to keep the chain.

"The artifact that runs the decision should be the artifact a compliance officer can verify, years later, from a CID alone." The single sentence that the rest of this manifesto unpacks.
Six principles

What we will not compromise on.

Written down so we can be held to them. Each one named the wave that shipped it; each one is testable in the open source.

01

The artifact is the unit of trust.

Not the model architecture. Not the training run. Not the dataset name. The thing you sign, ship, and load is a single content-addressed .kolm file with a verifiable receipt and an embedded K-score. If you cannot point at a CID, you cannot claim provenance.

Shipped: Ed25519 receipt + content-addressed artifact + signed verifier · verify a .kolm
02

The gate is honest.

One number per artifact, computed against held-out evals the artifact never saw, normalized against the teacher, composed across five axes with a weight vector that is part of the receipt. K-score is the same number on your laptop, in your CI, and in your auditor's sandbox. No leaderboard hacking; the eval pack is content-addressed too.

Shipped: K-score composite + per-axis receipts + eval-pack hash · read the gate spec
03

Your data stays where you put it.

Distillation runs in your VPC. Captures land in your data plane. The teacher is whatever teacher you choose, including teachers behind your own firewall. We do not look at your captures, we do not look at your artifact contents, and we do not need to. Telemetry from your runtime is signed and contains hashes and metrics; never raw PHI.

Shipped: BYOC + VPC patterns + HIPAA/SOC 2 mappings · see deployment patterns
04

The compiler is open source.

Apache-2.0. Every line of the compiler, the verifier, the K-score math, the eval pack schema, and the runtime adapter is readable in public. We do not sell a black box that decides what your decisions mean. If we are wrong about something, it is in a file you can read.

Shipped: github.com/sneaky-hippo/kolmogorov-stack · read the source
05

Honest about what we do not measure.

K-score does not measure jailbreak robustness, multi-turn coherence past your eval shape, or alignment to values not in your eval pack. The receipt lists every axis in scope; everything else is out. We will tell you in writing what kolm is the wrong tool for. The matrix on the compare page is the public version of that conversation.

Shipped: honest-scope tokens across the surface + an explicit "skip kolm if" section on every product page
06

The chain is verifiable years later.

Every artifact and every eval pack has a content-addressed CID. Every per-decision receipt embeds the artifact CID, the input hash, the decision, and the confidence; all signed. As long as you retain the CIDs under your record-keeping policy, an auditor can pull, verify, and re-score a decision seven years after the fact. We have customers running this for HIPAA seven-year horizons today.

Shipped: per-call signed receipts + verifier + registry retention · SOC 2 mapping

What we will not do.

We will not ship a feature whose receipt would be a lie. We will not gate the verifier behind a paywall. We will not "deprecate" an older eval-pack version in a way that makes a regulator's old receipt unverifiable. We will not store your captures or your artifacts in our cloud unless you explicitly route them there, and we will not add a feature that requires us to.

We will not, in marketing copy, claim a benchmark our compiler did not compute under the exact protocol we publish. We will not use the kind of throat-clearing superlatives that pad a hero and carry zero information; you know the words, we will not type them. We will not write fluff to fill a hero. We will not put an emoji on a feature that does not yet exist.

And we will not, ever, ship a model into production whose decision was opaque to the person whose decision it made. That is the floor.

What we are betting on.

The frontier model labs will keep getting better, faster than we can keep up. That is fine. We are not building a frontier lab. We are building the layer that makes those frontier models deployable into the environments where the decisions actually carry weight. The labs ship intelligence; we ship the chain of custody around that intelligence.

In ten years, the model behind every consequential AI decision will be a distilled specialist, signed, scored, runnable on commodity hardware, with a receipt that travels with the decision. The frontier model will be the teacher, not the runtime. The runtime will be yours. The receipt will be the contract between you and the person whose decision was made.

That is the bet. The compiler that makes it true is open source. The five axes are written down. The receipt format is published. The verifier is a hundred lines of Python. The whole stack is yours to audit, fork, run, and improve.

Compute your first artifact. Read your first receipt. Decide for yourself.

Signed. The kolm.ai engineering team.
