Public training datasets per task, with licenses and citations.

Every row is a {task, dataset_name, size, license, source_url, citation, recommended_use} tuple. Use it to seed a fresh kolm seeds new brief, import directly into seeds.jsonl, or pin a row as the external holdout your verifier compares against. kolm does not redistribute any of these; your customer-hosted compile bridge pulls them in your environment.

How to use this registry.

Three paths in. (1) Seed-brief mode: paste a dataset row into the prompt for kolm seeds new "<task>" and the scaffolder will produce a spec.json that names the dataset as seed_source.public_dataset. (2) Direct import: download a slice of the dataset, transform to {input, target} JSONL, drop into your repo as seeds.jsonl. (3) External holdout: pin one of these as your residency-free correctness reference; the holdout hash bound into artifact_hash_input.external_holdout_hash means the verifier can re-run it against any later artifact and detect drift. Every row carries a license string a tenant can hand to procurement, and an author + year that a third-party auditor can chase to a primary source.

Caveats guard: licenses change. Verify the dataset's current license text on the source page before you ship; the values below were correct at the time of this review and are tagged with the review date in the page stamp. The corpus-licensing-gate (wave 194 roadmap) will enforce license compatibility at compile time so a tenant cannot accidentally pin a research-only corpus as the external holdout of a commercial product.

General-purpose instruction and preference datasets.

The Tulu-3 / LIMA / UltraFeedback triumvirate is the de-facto open baseline for the SFT then preference distillation pipeline (per §5). LIMA is the anchor for "quality beats volume." UltraFeedback is the open preference corpus most papers cite. Tulu-3 ships the on-policy DPO mix that outperforms UltraFeedback-alone at 70B scale.

datasetsizelicensecitationrecommended use
LIMA ~1,000 examples CC-BY-NC-SA-4.0 Zhou et al., arXiv:2305.11206, NeurIPS 2023 High-quality fine-tune anchor. Demonstrates that 1k curated SFT examples on a 65B base produce responses preferred or equivalent to GPT-4 in 43 percent of pairs. Use as the "quality over volume" reference when sizing a distilled_model recipe. Non-commercial license.
UltraFeedback ~64,000 prompts MIT Cui et al., arXiv:2310.01377, 2023 Preference distillation (SimPO / KTO / ORPO / DPO). 4-aspect annotations across UltraChat, ShareGPT, Evol-Instruct, TruthfulQA, FalseQA, FLAN. The de-facto open preference baseline.
Tulu-3 SFT mix ~939,000 examples ODC-BY-1.0 Lambert et al., Allen AI, arXiv:2411.15124, 2024 Large-scale general-purpose instruction tuning. Drawn from WildChat, Persona-IF, math, code, safety mixes. ODC-BY permits commercial reuse with attribution.
OpenHermes-2.5 ~1,000,000 entries mixed (per-source) Teknium / Nous Research, HF: teknium/OpenHermes-2.5, 2024 General instruction tuning. Aggregate of GPT-4 multi-turn conversations. License is per-source; some shards are non-commercial. Read the dataset card before pinning.
Magpie-Llama-3.1-Pro-1M ~1,000,000 conversations Llama-3.1 community license Xu et al., arXiv:2406.08464, ICLR 2025 Self-instruct extension; seed-free synthesis from Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct. Inherits the Llama-3.1 community license; verify the acceptable-use policy applies to your downstream artifact before redistribution.
HelpSteer2 ~10,681 examples CC-BY-4.0 Wang et al., NVIDIA, arXiv:2406.08673, 2024 Helpfulness scoring across multiple axes (helpfulness, correctness, coherence, complexity, verbosity). Good companion for any preference recipe that wants graded labels rather than binary pairs.
Alpaca ~52,000 instructions CC-BY-NC-4.0 Taori et al., Stanford, tatsu-lab/stanford_alpaca, 2023 Self-Instruct anchor (175 seeds expanded via text-davinci-003). Use as the canonical Self-Instruct reproduction. Non-commercial.
Dolly-15k ~15,000 examples CC-BY-SA-3.0 Databricks, HF: databricks/databricks-dolly-15k, 2023 Human-authored instruction-response pairs across 7 task categories. Commercial-friendly; smaller than UltraFeedback but cleaner provenance.

Code, math, and reasoning datasets.

Code and math have crisp success criteria, which means they double as evaluations. Pin one of these as the external holdout and the verifier can re-derive a numeric score on the same byte-stable input across any later artifact.

datasetsizelicensecitationrecommended use
MATH ~12,500 problems MIT Hendrycks et al., arXiv:2103.03874, NeurIPS 2021 Competition-level math word problems with step-by-step solutions. Use as a generalist math reasoning eval and as a seed source for chain-of-thought distillation.
GSM8K ~8,500 problems MIT Cobbe et al., OpenAI, arXiv:2110.14168, 2021 Grade-school multi-step arithmetic word problems. The most-cited small-model math eval; pair with MATH for breadth vs depth.
HumanEval 164 problems MIT Chen et al., OpenAI, arXiv:2107.03374, 2021 Canonical code-generation eval (Python function completion). Small enough to run as a CI smoke gate on every artifact build.
MBPP 974 problems CC-BY-4.0 Austin et al., Google, arXiv:2108.07732, 2021 Basic Python programming problems with crowd-sourced descriptions. Broader than HumanEval; complementary holdout.
OpenCodeReasoning ~735,000 samples CC-BY-4.0 NVIDIA, HF: nvidia/OpenCodeReasoning, 2024 Code reasoning traces for distillation into smaller code-reasoning students. Source competitive-programming problems with synthetic R1-style traces.
APPS ~10,000 problems MIT Hendrycks et al., arXiv:2105.09938, NeurIPS 2021 Coding problems across difficulty tiers (introductory, interview, competition). Use for graded difficulty reporting on code-recipe students.

Finance · FinGPT multiple corpora MIT (code) verify Yang et al., arXiv:2306.06031, 2023 Open-source financial LLM datasets (sentiment, news, filings). Code is MIT but underlying news / filings inherit upstream source licenses; verify per shard.

Why amber on every row. Domain corpora are the most license-heterogeneous data class. A dataset that ships "Apache-2.0 code" almost always pulls non-Apache source data underneath: case law, EHRs, filings, news. The plain framing for any regulated tenant is "verify the full license stack before pinning as your external holdout or distilling into your shipped artifact." kolm gives you the citation; your counsel gives you the green light.

Synthetic data generation methods.

Sometimes the right answer is "make the data." Four open methods from the 2026-Q2 sweep, each linking back to the primary citation block in §5.

method 01

Self-Instruct

175 hand-written seeds expanded via N-shot prompting (N approx 8), Rouge-L diversity filter, iterate. Stanford Alpaca scaled this recipe to 52k.

Wang et al., 2022 · arXiv:2212.10560 method 02

Evol-Instruct

Iteratively evolves instructions via five In-depth ops (add constraints, deepen, concretize, increase reasoning steps, complicate input) plus In-breadth. WizardLM beat ChatGPT by +7.9 pts win-rate on hard prompts.

Xu et al., ICLR 2024 · arXiv:2304.12244 method 03

Magpie

Seed-free synthesis. Feeds the pre-fill chat template alone; the aligned LLM hallucinates both a user query and a response. Llama-3-8B-Base fine-tuned on Magpie outperforms Evol-Instruct- and UltraChat-derived datasets.

Xu et al., ICLR 2025 · arXiv:2406.08464 method 04

Persona-Hub

1B personas via Text-to-Persona and Persona-to-Persona expansion. Inject one persona per generation prompt to diversify outputs (math, instructions, knowledge-rich text, tool calls).

Chan et al., Tencent AI Lab, 2024 · arXiv:2406.20094

Composability: Magpie or Persona-Hub for diversity, Evol-Instruct for difficulty range, Self-Instruct as the baseline reproduction. Combine to cover topic breadth and difficulty depth simultaneously. The modal kolm narrow-task recipe is 20-100 hand seeds plus Evol-Instruct or Magpie expanding to 1k-10k pairs (per §5.3 practitioner range).

kolm-shipped seed examples.

The Wave 176-184 health-insurance template batch each ships 8-12 anonymized seed examples per task (EDI 837 / 835 / 834 / 270 / 271 / 278, FHIR USCDI, HEDIS measures, denial and prior-auth letters, MLR, Star ratings, NPI registry, ICD to CPT crosswalks). These are not public datasets; they are template seeds packaged with the spec.json so a tenant can run kolm seeds new health-insurance/denial-letter --template=denial-codeset-2026 and get a working scaffold immediately. See the full template grid at /health-insurance. The seed examples are intentionally small (8-12); the registry above is where you go when you need volume.

Plain scope: licensing and redistribution.

Not every "open" dataset is commercial-redistributable. Read the license per dataset before pinning as your external holdout or distilling into your shipped artifact. CC-BY-NC means no commercial use; CC-BY-SA means derivative works inherit the share-alike clause; ODC-BY is commercial-friendly but requires attribution; research-only licenses (MIMIC, i2b2, n2c2) require credentialing and data-use agreements per institution. License strings drift; verify the dataset's current license text on the source page on the day you ship.

kolm does not redistribute any of the datasets named on this page. Your customer-hosted compile bridge pulls them in your environment under your credentials. The kolm artifact records the external_holdout_hash of the byte-stable slice you used; the verifier re-derives that hash on demand. No raw corpus data is bundled into the .kolm file.

The Wave 194 corpus-licensing-gate wave 194 roadmap will enforce license compatibility at compile time. When the gate ships, kolm compile will require a dataset_license declaration per pinned external corpus, cross-reference against a tenant-supplied allow-list of commercially-compatible licenses, and reject the build at gate time if a research-only corpus is named as the external holdout of a commercially-shipping artifact. Until then, the responsibility sits with the tenant operator and counsel.
