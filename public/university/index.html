kolm.ai / university

Open-source AI workbench

kolm university. Frontier AI on your own infrastructure.

Short, opinionated lessons on the discipline behind owning your own frontier AI — distillation, evaluation, multimodal compilation, and shipping signed artifacts to your own hardware. Every lesson is free and grounded in the same open-source tooling you can install today.

kolm university exists because most AI education today is written either by hosted-API vendors who want you to stay on their bill, or by academics whose notebooks never make it to production. The lessons here are written by the people who built the compiler. They assume you want to leave the API, run on hardware you control, and audit every step. The first lesson is live. More are coming.

Curriculum

Each lesson is a single page you can read end-to-end in under twenty minutes. Code blocks are runnable against the open-source CLI (npm i -g github:kolm-ai/kolmogorov-stack or pip install git+https://github.com/kolm-ai/kolmogorov-stack@main#subdirectory=sdk/python). Numbers are reproducible — we show the exact hardware, the exact dataset size, and the exact K-score range.

lesson 01 . foundations

Distillation 101

Available

What knowledge distillation is, why you would do it, and how kolm turns 5,000 captured prompt-response pairs into a 7B specialist that runs on a single RTX 5090. Worked example with plain numbers.

lesson 02 . evaluation

Distillation 201: held-out evals and K-score

Planned

How to split a capture set into train, validation, and hidden held-out folds; how K-score combines accuracy, calibration, refusal rate, and tail-risk into one decision number; common evaluation traps.

lesson 03 . scoring

K-Score deep dive

Planned

Open-spec walkthrough of the K-score: what each component measures, the math behind the weighting, why we publish raw component scores alongside the headline number, and how to read a K-score report critically.

lesson 04 . multimodal

Multimodal distillation

Planned

Distilling vision-and-language and vision-and-audio teachers into a single artifact. Capture replay across image, audio, video, and PDF modalities. The bake-off harness for multimodal artifacts.

Certification program (planned)

kolm certified distillation engineer — not yet available

We are scoping a paid, time-boxed certification that ends with a graded project: you ship a real .kolm artifact that meets a published K-score floor on a real held-out dataset, and we issue a signed credential you can show in a portfolio. The certificate is not yet available. There is no cohort. There is no exam. Anyone claiming a current kolm certification is mistaken.

If you would be interested when it launches, the lowest-friction way to hear about it is the newsletter on the marketing site. We will announce dates, price, and curriculum there first.

Hands-on cookbook

Recipes you can copy, paste, and run

The university lessons are theory plus one worked example. The cookbook in the main docs is the practice side: dozens of short recipes for distilling a coding assistant, a support bot, a document extractor, a PII redactor, and more — each one comes with a runnable spec, a seed set, and a target K-score so you know when you have hit the bar.

How to use this

If you have never compiled a model before, start with the five-minute first-artifact path, then come back here for the theory. If you are already shipping artifacts, skip straight to Distillation 101 for the model behind why it works. If you are evaluating kolm for a team, the manifesto covers the philosophy and the pricing page covers the cost model.

All lessons are released under the same Apache-2.0 license as the codebase. The certification program is in planning and is not yet open for enrollment. We will not retroactively grant certifications, and there is no path to "test out" of a program that has not yet been built.
