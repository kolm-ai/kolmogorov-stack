2. What kolm is

A short product definition so the rest of the document has a clear referent. Each row maps to a paid or unpaid surface.

compiler

Open-source kolm CLI and SDKs

The compiler, the Node, Python, MCP, C, and Rust SDKs, and the runtime adapters are licensed under Apache-2.0. They run on your hardware, your VPC, or your laptop. No account is required.

hosted

Hosted gateway and registry

An optional managed endpoint at https://kolm.ai that routes requests to upstream providers, mirrors public registry entries, and serves verification at /verify/<cid>. These features require an account and these terms.

artifact

.kolm artifact format

A signed zip that contains weights, a manifest, the eval pack and its scores, and the receipt chain. The format spec is open. Artifacts you compile are owned by you.

enterprise

Enterprise plan

SSO, SCIM, role-based access, BAA, regional deployment, single-tenant control plane, named support. Terms here are baseline, the enterprise order form is the controlling document for the enterprise plan.

4. Hosted gateway terms

The terms that apply when you route traffic through https://kolm.ai, publish to the registry, or use any paid plan. The compiler is unaffected by these.

Account creation

You create an account by signing up with an email. We send a verification link. The first request you make produces an API key. Treat the key like a password, rotate it through kolm logout and kolm login if you suspect leak.

Authentication and identity

Free and pro accounts authenticate by API key. The business and enterprise plans add SSO through SAML 2.0 and SCIM 2.0. The hosted gateway will reject any request that does not present a valid identity.

Rate limits

Per-plan limits are published on the pricing page and returned in the X-RateLimit-* headers on every response. When a limit is hit, the gateway returns HTTP 429 with a Retry-After header and a JSON envelope that names the limit you tripped.

Service availability

The hosted gateway is a multi-region, multi-provider router. Uptime targets and credit terms are published per plan. Status incidents are posted at /security with a real timeline, no fluff.

6. Your data, your boundary

A summary of the data clauses. The privacy policy and the security overview are the canonical documents and they are linked at the bottom.

You own your captures

Prompts, completions, tool calls, retrieval contexts, and trace bodies are yours. The default install writes them to ~/.kolm/events on your filesystem. We do not ingest them.

You own your models

Distilled weights, INT4 and INT8 quantizations, eval-pack scores. Compile output is yours. We do not have a license to your weights and we do not need one.

Hosted metadata stays minimal

The hosted gateway records timestamp, route, token count, latency, status, and key fingerprint. Bodies are not stored. The full list is in the privacy policy under "What we collect".

For deletion, residency, BAA, DPA, and a list of subprocessors, see the linked pages at the bottom of this document.
