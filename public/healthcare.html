How PHI stays inside

What makes this PHI-safe.

No vendor-speak. The exact mechanism for each property, with the file and line you can verify yourself if you run kolm in-house.

01

The data plane runs in your VPC

On-prem or BYOC. PHI is processed by code running in your AWS / GCP / Azure account or your data center. No outbound calls to kolm.ai except optional metering (which can be turned off and replaced with a local meter).

02

Distillation is local

The teacher model and student model both run in your perimeter on your hardware. Captures never leave. The compile step is a worker that runs in your VPC, not a hosted service.

03

Encryption at rest and in transit

TLS 1.3 in transit. AES-256-GCM at rest with a per-tenant key in your KMS (AWS KMS, GCP KMS, Azure Key Vault). Keys rotate quarterly by default; you can force rotation any time.

04

PHI redaction at the boundary

kolm capture log --redact phi strips 18 HIPAA identifiers using Presidio + a clinical-NER overlay before the capture is persisted. The redactor is a worker pod; you can swap it for your own redactor without changing the rest of the system.

05

Audit trail to your SIEM

Every read, write, model invocation, and capture is in the audit log with member-MRN-hash, route, IP, and the receipt CID. Streams to Splunk, Datadog, or Elastic in JSON or CEF. Append-only with a signed chain.

06

Signed artifact + signed inference

The .kolm artifact is signed with your tenant's Ed25519 key. Optionally, each inference call returns a per-call receipt linking the input hash, the output hash, and the artifact CID. That receipt is your chain of custody if a clinical decision is ever audited.

07

Break-glass and right-to-erasure

Break-glass access uses dual-control activation with forced rotation. Right-to-erasure: kolm erase --member member_id removes the member from captures, eval sets, and the audit-log searchable index (the immutable log retains the redaction receipt, not the data).

Honest scope

What kolm does, and what we tell every clinical buyer up front.

No diagnostic claims, no FDA marketing language. Read this before the rest of the page becomes interesting.

A

Kolm is not a clinical decision support device

The CLI does not output diagnoses. Our distilled artifacts route, summarize, structure, draft, and prioritize. Anything that crosses into a clinical decision is your application logic, your label, and your regulatory path.

B

We do not file your 510(k)

If you are building a Class II device on top of kolm, you own the 510(k). What kolm gives you: the signed receipts you need for your software-of-unknown-provenance (SOUP) documentation and the audit trail your QMS requires.

C

Models will be wrong sometimes

Our distilled models drop K-score by under 3 points after quantization. They still hallucinate, miss edge cases, and exhibit bias. Every kolm workflow ships with a defer-on-low-confidence path and a human-in-the-loop gate by default; turning that off is a choice you have to make on purpose.

D

We will not let you skip the BAA

If your usage touches PHI we will not sign a contract without a BAA. Period. Even for a pilot. The BAA is one page of legal you can read in five minutes; ask and we will send it the same day.

Reference architectures

How health teams actually deploy kolm.

Same product, several placements. Pick the one that matches your IT estate and your compliance posture.

A. EHR adjacent

Inside the EHR cloud account

Helm chart installed in the same AWS / GCP / Azure account that runs your Epic / Cerner / Athena instance. PHI flows through your existing VPC peering; kolm never sees an external network.

helm install kolm kolm/health \
--set residency=us-east-1 \
--set kms.arn=arn:aws:kms:... \
--set egress=none
B. On-prem with DMZ

Bare metal, with an outbound DMZ

kolm runs in your data center on Kubernetes or bare metal. The only outbound path is the audit-log stream to your SIEM (Splunk, Elastic, Sentinel). No vendor cloud is in the path.

$ kolm install --on-prem \
--bundle kolm-2026-05.tar.zst \
--signature kolm-2026-05.sig \
--egress audit-only
C. Research enclave

Air-gap appliance in a HIPAA enclave

Signed OVA appliance that boots offline in a research enclave. Pre-loaded model registry. Upgrades arrive on a signed USB or a one-way data diode; the appliance verifies signatures before applying.

appliance: kolm-2026-05.ova
signature: kolm-2026-05.sig
verify: gpg --verify (offline)
boot: zero outbound
D. EHR vendor SaaS

Dedicated SaaS, per-customer tenancy

You run an EHR add-on and your customers each need a BAA. We give you a dedicated single-tenant environment per customer; you sign a master BAA, your customers sign a downstream BAA with you.

tenancy: dedicated, per-customer
BAA: master + downstream
residency: per-customer region
audit: per-customer SIEM stream

Operational footprint

What the operations team needs to know.

Day-2 facts. Capacity, paging, change management, the boring numbers that decide whether this is something you can actually run.

i

Footprint per 100k members

Reference deployment: three application nodes (8 vCPU, 32 GB RAM each), one inference node with a single A10G or RTX 4000 Ada (24 GB VRAM), one Postgres instance, one S3-compatible bucket. Handles ~2,400 inferences per hour on a 7B INT4 specialist with headroom.

ii

Change management

Upgrades arrive as signed bundles every two weeks (security) or monthly (features). The bundle includes a release note, a signature, and a rollback receipt. You apply on your cadence; we do not push to your cluster.

iii

Paging

Optional vendor-side paging (we get woken up if your kolm-operated bits page). For BYOC and on-prem your own oncall pages first; we are available 24/7 under enterprise contracts with a 1-hour response on Sev-1.

iv

Disaster recovery

RPO 15 min, RTO 4 hours on hosted. BYOC and on-prem inherit your DR posture; we ship runbooks for restoring captures, registry artifacts, and signing keys from cold storage.

v

Telemetry

Prometheus-format metrics on /metrics (opt-in). OpenTelemetry traces (opt-in). Structured logs on stdout. Zero PHI in any telemetry stream by default; opt-in flags add only structural identifiers (artifact CID, namespace name).

vi

Cost

For BYOC the cost is your cloud bill plus our flat enterprise license. For SaaS-hosted, the public Enterprise tier starts at $4,000/mo for 5M gateway calls. Talk to us if you have more than 50M/month; we have not blown anyone's budget yet.
