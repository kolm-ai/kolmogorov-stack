When to pick each one

No stack wins every column. Pick the column that matters.

Pick kolm if

You ship into regulated environments

You need a signed artifact a compliance officer can verify years from now, a per-call receipt your auditor can pull, and a deploy pattern that keeps PHI inside your own infrastructure. The K-score gate stops bad artifacts from ever reaching your runtime.

Decisive when: HIPAA, SOC 2, FedRAMP, or an internal compliance review is on the path to production.
Pick Fireworks AI if

You want the fastest hosted inference for open weights

Fireworks runs an excellent serverless inference layer for open models with low-latency adapter swapping. Pair it with kolm for the build-time artifact pipeline and the signed receipts, then serve the result on Fireworks.

Decisive when: latency-critical workloads; you do not need build-time compliance or a self-hosted runtime.
Pick Together AI if

You want a model-cloud with fine-tune SaaS

Together is a competent fine-tune-and-host across 100+ open models. If you do not need the artifact to leave their cloud and per-token vendor cost is fine, the managed experience is fast to start.

Decisive when: no regulatory boundary, no need to run offline, value managed UX over open weights.
Pick Baseten if

You want polished deploys with autoscaling

Baseten's Truss and Chains are a clean serving stack with autoscaling, A/B routing, and a strong ops surface. If a hosted runtime is acceptable, it is a fast on-ramp.

Decisive when: simple deployment surface required, hosted runtime acceptable, no per-call receipt needed.
Pick DIY if

You have a deep ML platform team

If you already operate Triton, Axolotl, and a custom eval harness in production and your team enjoys the stack, you do not need a higher-level compiler. Use kolm verifier as a drop-in trust layer instead.

Decisive when: 5+ engineers focused on the ML platform; existing tooling investment.
Pick hyperscaler if

You are buying single-vendor everything

If your procurement reality is "the same vendor owns the data warehouse, the IAM, and the foundation model", the hyperscaler fine-tune is the path of least resistance, even if it is the most expensive per token long-term.

Decisive when: the strategic vendor decision is already made; ML is one workload among many.
Where we are the wrong fit

Where kolm is not the right fit.

Cases where we will tell you to pick a different stack. Save yourself a pilot.

Skip kolm if

You need a hosted base model with no build step

If your workload is "call a frontier LLM and pass the output to a user", you do not need a compiler. Hit the model provider directly. We are useful when the answer is "distill a specialist that can replace the call in production"; if you are not doing that, the build step is overhead.

Better fit: direct API call to Anthropic, OpenAI, or a hyperscaler endpoint.
Skip kolm if

You serve 10,000+ adapters multiplexed

Our inference path supports adapter swapping but it is not where we put first-quintile engineering effort. If your dominant cost is "I have ten thousand customer-specific adapters and I need them all warm on one node", Fireworks AI or Together AI is the better serving fit and we will tell you so.

Better fit: Fireworks AI, or kolm-for-build → Fireworks-for-serve combo.
Skip kolm if

Your team will not adopt a CI gate

The K-score gate is the heart of the value proposition. If your team will not adopt a "fail the build if the artifact does not score above floor X" workflow, you are paying for a feature you are not using and we will be miserable for each other.

Better fit: hosted fine-tune SaaS where the eval is decoupled from deploy.
Migration paths

From each stack into kolm, in the order most teams take it.

From Fireworks

Build with kolm, serve with Fireworks

The cleanest hybrid. Use kolm to capture, distill, and sign the artifact; export the LoRA + GGUF that Fireworks already serves; keep your inference layer. The artifact arrives in your runtime with a verifiable receipt and a K-score baked in.

From Together

Export fine-tune, sign with kolm

Export your existing Together fine-tune as a base, point kolm distill at your captures for the next iteration. The first kolm artifact is a parity check; the second is the one that ships under the new gate.

From Baseten

Re-import traffic, re-distill

Logged traffic transfers. Convert Baseten request logs with `kolm import --from json`; the same captures go into kolm distill. K-score against the same held-out cases is the apples-to-apples check.

From OpenPipe / Predibase / LoRAX

Migrate before the acquirer migrates you

OpenPipe wound down inside CoreWeave (Jul 2026), Predibase rolled into Rubrik, and LoRAX is maintained downstream of that move. Export the captures you still own, point `kolm import --from openpipe` (or `--from predibase`) at them, re-distill, and ship under your own signed artifact contract before the next pricing change lands.

From DIY

Adopt the verifier first

Lowest-disruption path: keep your training pipeline, adopt only `kolm verify` to sign and gate the artifacts your DIY stack produces. The receipt + K-score arrive without changing your training code.

From hyperscaler

Distill the hyperscaler endpoint as the teacher

Your existing vendor endpoint becomes the teacher in kolm distill. You build a specialist that lives in your VPC and the production traffic stops paying per-token to the hyperscaler. The hyperscaler stays the fallback.

From scratch

Quickstart, then bring captures

If you have no incumbent stack, run the quickstart, generate a toy artifact, then point capture at your real traffic for two weeks. After 5K captures, distill and score. The first real artifact is usually inside a month.
