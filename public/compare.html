07 / Scope and limits

What the report covers, and what it does not.

Stating the edges is part of the evidence. A reviewer trusts a report that is clear about what it did not test more than one that implies it tested everything.

In scope

Assessed from the agent's own logs

Permission and least-privilege posture, audit-trail integrity, data egress and redaction, model and dependency provenance, agent identity, memory and retrieval integrity, and multi-agent delegation. Direct and indirect injection are tested by a red-team battery and reported with reproductions.

Out of scope

Reported as not assessed, never implied

Improper output handling (OWASP LLM05), misinformation (LLM09), and consumption beyond the runaway-loop signal we flag (LLM10) are not assessed. Neither is poisoning of the base model's training data, nor model extraction. The report evidences posture from logs; it is not a substitute for a human-led penetration test where a buyer contractually requires one.

Scope is contractual. Permission posture, redaction and audit-trail integrity are assessed. Injection is tested and reported, not warranted.

08 / Questions

Common questions

Is kolm a replacement for Vanta or Drata?

No. GRC automation keeps your company SOC 2 and ISO evidence current, which you still need. kolm produces the agent-specific, buyer-verifiable evidence a reviewer asks for about the agent itself. Every finding maps back into the frameworks those platforms track, so the two sit side by side.

How is a signed report different from a SOC 2 or a trust portal?

A SOC 2 attests company controls over a period and usually lives behind an NDA in a portal. A kolm report is a single canonical object about one agent that your buyer verifies offline, in their own browser, against your public key, with no account and no kolm server in the trust path. Try the sample verifier.

Do runtime guardrails give my buyer what they need?

Guardrails defend at runtime, which matters in production, but runtime enforcement is not a procurement artifact. A reviewer cannot hand a firewall configuration to their team as signed evidence about your agent. A guardrail and kolm address different jobs, and you can run both.

What about a questionnaire or a pentest PDF?

Familiar and sometimes required, but unsigned and impossible to verify independently. The document goes stale on your next deploy and shifts the burden of proof onto the buyer. kolm re-attests on a schedule and the buyer verifies every byte against your key.

A signed record of the agent's identity, provenance and delegation, an append-only transparency log, an agent-specific red-team battery, and an evidence report the buyer verifies with no account, all mapped to SOC 2, ISO 42001, NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act, OWASP LLM Top 10 and MITRE ATLAS.
