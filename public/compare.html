01 / The landscape

Three categories, each good at its own job.

A fair read on what each tool is built for, and where it stops short of the evidence a procurement reviewer needs about your agent.

GRC / compliance automation

Vanta, Drata and the like

Strong at continuously collecting your company SOC 2 and ISO evidence and running a trust center. You should keep using one. But the evidence describes the company, lives in a portal, and is not a cryptographically signed artifact about a specific agent that a reviewer verifies themselves.

Gap: agent-specific, buyer-verifiable proof

AI security / guardrails

Runtime firewalls and filters

Strong at blocking prompt injection and policy violations in production, in the moment. A real defense layer. But runtime enforcement is not a procurement document. A reviewer cannot hand a firewall configuration to their team as the signed evidence the review asks for.

Gap: an evidence artifact for the reviewer

DIY questionnaire + pentest

Spreadsheets and PDF reports

Familiar, flexible, and sometimes required by the buyer. But a filled-out questionnaire and a pentest PDF are unsigned and cannot be checked independently. They go stale on your next deploy and shift the burden of proof back onto the buyer.

Gap: signed, current, verifiable

05 / Questions

Before you ask.

Is kolm a replacement for Vanta or Drata?

No. GRC automation keeps your company SOC 2 and ISO evidence current, which you still need. kolm produces the agent-specific, buyer-verifiable evidence a reviewer asks for about the agent itself. Every finding maps back into the SOC 2, ISO 42001 and NIST AI RMF work those platforms track, so the two sit side by side.

How is a signed report different from a SOC 2 or a trust portal?

A SOC 2 attests company controls over a period and usually lives behind an NDA in a portal. A kolm report is a single canonical object about one agent that your buyer verifies offline in their own browser, against your public key, with no account and no kolm server in the trust path. Try the sample verifier.

Do runtime AI guardrails give my buyer what they need?

Guardrails defend at runtime, which matters in production, but runtime enforcement is not a procurement artifact. A security reviewer cannot hand a firewall configuration to their team as signed evidence about your agent. A guardrail and kolm address different jobs, and you can run both.

What about a questionnaire or a pentest PDF?

They are familiar and sometimes required, but they are unsigned and cannot be verified independently. The document goes stale on your next deploy and shifts the burden of proof onto the buyer. kolm re-attests on a schedule and the buyer verifies every byte against your key.

What does kolm have that the others do not?

A signed record of the agent's identity, provenance, and delegation, an append-only transparency log, an agent-specific red-team battery, and an evidence report the buyer verifies with no account, all mapped to SOC 2, ISO 42001, NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act, OWASP LLM Top 10 and MITRE ATLAS.
