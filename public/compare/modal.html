kolm vs Modal

kolm vs Modal: which should you pick?

Modal rents you serverless GPUs by the second; kolm compiles your captured traffic into a signed model you own and run on any hardware. Pick Modal when you want pure on-demand compute with no model ownership. Pick kolm when you want a portable, verifiable artifact and a per-token bill that drops to zero after compile.

How they differ, axis by axis

What is the core difference between kolm and Modal?

Modal is a serverless GPU platform: you rent ephemeral GPU containers per second for training and inference jobs. The table below contrasts each on the axes that matter for ownership, cost, provenance, and portability, so you can see where kolm's compile-and-own model diverges from a hosted or rented offering and decide which fits your workload.

When to pick each

When is Modal the right answer, and when is kolm?

Neither tool is strictly better; they solve different problems. Choose Modal when your workload is pure ephemeral batch compute (rendering, ETL, one-off training) and you do not want to own or serve a model afterward. Modal's per-second autoscaling is excellent for spiky, stateless GPU jobs. The opposite case is summarized below, so you can match the choice to whether you want a hosted, rented capability or an owned, portable model with a verifiable build receipt.

When kolm wins. Choose kolm when you make repeated LLM calls you already pay per token for, and you want to turn that traffic into a model you own, quantize to your own hardware, and stop paying per token — with a signed receipt for every build.

FAQ

Can I move off Modal to kolm without rewriting my app?
Yes. kolm exposes an OpenAI-compatible gateway, so you change the base URL and keep your existing client. The gateway captures (input, output) pairs as you go, which become the training set for a compiled specialist model.
Does kolm replace my GPU provider?
No. kolm is the compiler, not the cloud. You can compile on a rented Modal GPU, on a local RTX 5090, or in your own VPC. The output is a portable artifact you run anywhere.
Do I keep the weights?
Yes. kolm's output is a single signed .kolm file containing your quantized weights plus a receipt. There is no runtime dependency on kolm and no per-token bill behind it.
