kolm vs LM Studio

kolm vs LM Studio: which should you pick?

LM Studio downloads and runs existing GGUF models on your desktop; kolm compiles a specialist trained on your own traffic and signs the build. Pick LM Studio to run off-the-shelf models locally. Pick kolm when you want to produce the GGUF — distilled to your workload and quantized with a verifiable receipt.

When to pick each

When is LM Studio the right answer, and when is kolm?

Neither tool is strictly better; they solve different problems. Choose LM Studio when you just want to chat with an existing open model locally with a friendly desktop UI and no build step. The opposite case is summarized below, so you can match the choice to whether you want a hosted, rented capability or an owned, portable model with a verifiable build receipt.

When kolm wins. Choose kolm when you want to produce the model in the first place — a specialist distilled from your traffic and quantized to a GGUF you can then open in LM Studio, with a signed receipt of how it was built.

FAQ

Can I run a kolm artifact in LM Studio?
Yes. kolm exports standard GGUF, which LM Studio loads directly. kolm is the compiler; LM Studio is one of several runtimes for the output.
Why compile instead of downloading a model?
A downloaded model is generic. A kolm-compiled specialist is distilled from your own captured traffic, so it is smaller and tuned to your task, with a receipt that proves how it was built.
Do I need a GPU?
Quantization benefits from a GPU, but the resulting GGUF runs on CPU offload in LM Studio. The verified matrix figures were measured on a single RTX 5090.
