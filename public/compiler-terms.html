Compiler workspace terms.

These terms govern use of the Kolm API wrapper, compiler workspace, signed artifacts, specialist composition, deployment tooling, account surfaces, and related hosted services. Audit report engagements have their own surface at audit.kolm.ai/terms.

01 Account And API Use

You may create a Kolm workspace, use API keys, route model requests, capture examples, manage provider credentials, and run compile or deployment workflows subject to these terms and any plan limits shown in the product or pricing page.

You are responsible for protecting API keys, configuring scopes, and ensuring that users under your workspace are authorized to access the data, models, devices, and providers connected to Kolm.

02 Customer Content

Customer Content includes prompts, responses, traces, tool calls, examples, files, model metadata, provider credentials, deployment targets, and other materials you submit or route through Kolm. You retain ownership of Customer Content.

You grant Kolm a limited license to process Customer Content only to provide the service: capture, normalize, redact where configured, compile, evaluate, package, serve, debug, secure, and support your workspace.

03 Artifacts And Outputs

Kolm may generate manifests, receipts, signed artifacts, eval outputs, routing decisions, device-fit recommendations, and deployment packages. You are responsible for reviewing outputs before relying on them in production.

Artifact signatures and manifests help verify integrity. They do not guarantee that a model is safe, lawful, compliant, accurate, or fit for a particular use case.

04 Provider And Device Responsibilities

If you connect third-party model providers, cloud accounts, local devices, or edge devices, you are responsible for the terms, credentials, permissions, costs, and security posture of those systems. Kolm may help route or deploy workloads, but it does not assume your obligations to upstream providers or device owners.

05 Acceptable Use

Do not use Kolm to violate law, bypass access controls, extract secrets, deploy malware, evade provider terms, attack third-party systems, or process data you do not have rights to process. Do not attempt to overload, probe, or disrupt the service.

06 Plans, Billing, And Limits

Plans may include gateway calls, compile credits, seats, storage, retention, devices, provider integrations, and support levels. Self-serve paid plans require successful payment. Enterprise terms may be governed by an order form.

07 Privacy And Security

Kolm applies tenant isolation and access controls to workspace data. You are responsible for choosing what to route, retain, redact, export, or delete. The privacy policy describes personal data handling; a data processing addendum may apply for business customers.

08 Audit Module

The audit module is a separate product surface for signed readiness reports and buyer-facing verification. Using audit.kolm.ai or entering a separate audit engagement may add audit-specific terms, scope statements, and deliverables.

09 No Warranty

The service is provided as available except where an order form states otherwise. Kolm does not warrant that compiled artifacts will satisfy every deployment target, that every workload can be shrunk to every device, or that outputs will be error-free.
