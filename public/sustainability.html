Sustainability and CO2 footprint.

Every distillation has a carbon cost. We measure it, stamp it into the artifact manifest, and let viewers render an honest "Saved X kg CO2 vs cloud inference" pill. No carbon-neutral marketing, no offset hand-waving -- just GPU TDP, run duration, and regional grid intensity with +/- 30 percent error bars.

Why we measure

Every distillation has a carbon cost.

Training a student model on a teacher's traces uses real electricity. Running a model in the cloud uses more. Running a compiled .kolm on your own GPU avoids the cloud-inference round-trips, which is usually the larger lifetime emission. We want both numbers visible so the comparison is honest.

  • Training emissions

    one-time, capped at compile

    One distill loop, one number. GPU TDP times wallclock duration times regional grid carbon intensity. Stamped into the artifact at compile time so the receipt does not drift later.

  • Cloud-inference round-trips

    recurring per call

    Every API call to a hosted frontier model is a transit and a datacenter inference. Multiply by your call rate and the recurring cost dominates within weeks. Running locally cuts this cost to your own GPU draw plus zero network.

  • Local inference draw

    your hardware, your grid

    A 5090 at full draw is roughly 575 watts. The "saved CO2" pill compares your local draw against the cloud equivalent. We do not subtract the embodied carbon of the GPU itself -- that is a separate accounting we do not claim to capture.

CO2 estimate model

GPU TDP times duration times grid intensity.

Plain inputs, plain math, plain output. We do not lifecycle-assess your datacenter. We do not factor PUE because we do not know your facility. The estimate is intentionally simple so it is auditable line-by-line.

co2_kg_estimate = (gpu_tdp_watts * duration_seconds / 3600 / 1000) * grid_kg_co2_per_kwh # example: RTX 5090 distill of Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct for 13.85 seconds on US-West grid # = (575 * 13.85 / 3600 / 1000) * 0.290 # = 0.000642 kg CO2 # = 0.642 grams CO2 (+/- 30 percent)

  • gpu_tdp_watts

    vendor datasheet

    NVIDIA, AMD, and Intel publish max TDP per SKU. We use the published nameplate, not measured draw, because measured draw varies with utilization and we want a conservative upper bound.

  • duration_seconds

    wallclock from your run

    Captured by the kolm CLI at the start and end of distill, quantize, and compile. No probabilistic estimates. The exact seconds you waited.

  • grid_kg_co2_per_kwh

    ElectricityMap public data

    Regional carbon intensity from ElectricityMap's open dataset. We snapshot the hourly value at the start of your run. If your tenant lacks a region setting, we fall back to the global average and flag estimate_quality:low in the manifest.

Sustainability badge spec

co2_kg_estimate in the manifest, pill in the viewer.

Every compiled .kolm carries the field. Any viewer that knows the field can render the pill. The pill text is computed from the artifact, not hard-coded server-side.

{ "artifact_id": "art_qwen3b_distill_v4", "co2_kg_estimate": 0.000642, "co2_estimate_quality": "high", "co2_grid_region": "US-WEST", "co2_grid_kg_per_kwh": 0.290, "co2_method": "tdp_times_duration_v1", "co2_error_bar_pct": 30 }

Viewers render the pill from these six fields. No server lookup, no opaque scoring.

Saved 14.2 kg CO2 vs cloud inference

The "saved vs cloud" pill multiplies your call rate by the cloud-inference baseline minus the local draw. Cloud baseline is a regional median; if we cannot determine it, the pill is suppressed and replaced with "CO2 estimate unavailable for this region".

Honest contract

+/- 30 percent error bars. No "carbon-neutral" without receipts.

Energy accounting is hard. The honest move is to publish error bars and refuse to claim offsets we cannot prove. We will not stamp "carbon-neutral" on any artifact unless the tenant has uploaded matching offset receipts that we have hash-bound to the artifact manifest.

  • What we claim

    conservative upper bounds

    Conservative upper-bound estimates of training and local-inference emissions, error-barred at +/- 30 percent, sourced from public TDP datasheets and ElectricityMap public data. We claim the math is auditable line-by-line.

  • What we never claim

    no carbon-neutral without proof

    We never stamp "carbon-neutral" on any artifact without matching offset receipts. We never claim PUE multipliers we cannot verify. We never subtract embodied carbon of GPUs or datacenters. We never use marketing-grade rounding that hides the error bar.

  • If we cannot estimate

    empty-state, never a guess

    If grid_region is unknown and the global-average fallback also fails, the manifest field is set to null and the viewer renders "CO2 estimate unavailable". We do not invent a number to fill space.

Honest framing. CO2 estimates have a +/- 30 percent error bar by design. They are not a lifecycle assessment. They do not include embodied GPU or datacenter carbon. We will not call a model "carbon-neutral" without uploaded offset receipts that hash-bind to the artifact manifest.
