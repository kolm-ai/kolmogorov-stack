kolm vs Together AI. Hosted GPU pool vs portable file.

Together AI runs a polished GPU pool that hosts 200+ open-weight base models and lets you fine-tune any of them, billed per token. kolm runs the same training step and ships you the file. Same end state, two different deliverables.

Together AI

A hosted inference + fine-tune service. Bring pairs, fine-tune any of 200+ open models, serve from Together's GPU pool. Per-token billing on inference; deploy and runtime are theirs.

vs

kolm

A capture -> distill -> compile workbench. Same training step on a curated set of open bases. The deliverable is a signed .kolm file you run on your own hardware. Flat per compile, $0 marginal inference.

Same training, different deployment

Both products do supervised fine-tuning on open-weight bases (Llama, Qwen, Mistral, Phi). The training step itself is similar - LoRA or full-parameter, configurable rank and epochs, eval-gated. Where they diverge is what comes out.

Together returns a hosted model endpoint with a per-token rate sheet. kolm returns a .kolm file: weights, recipes, manifest, signature. From the moment you have the file, your inference cost is "what you pay your hardware vendor," not "what you pay Together per token."

When kolm wins

  • You want to own the file. The deliverable is portable. Run it on your laptop, ship it to a customer, deploy it air-gapped, embed it in a phone app. None of that is possible when the model lives on Together's servers.
  • $0 marginal inference. Per-token billing scales linearly with traffic; flat-compile + own-hardware doesn't. For high-volume tasks the curves cross fast.
  • Verifier-gated training + signed receipts. Each compile runs captured pairs through a synthesized verifier and embeds a K-score. The artifact ships with an Ed25519 signature. Together has no equivalent.

When Together AI wins

  • Excellent hosted inference. If you don't want to operate inference servers, Together's GPU pool is mature - the autoscaling works, the rate-limiting is tight, the OpenAI-compatible API is one drop-in line of code.
  • Massive base-model breadth. Together supports 200+ base models on the inference side. kolm targets a smaller curated set (Phi-3, Qwen2.5, Llama-3.2 in the MIT/Apache footprint) - the bases we have validated end-to-end through verifier and distillation.
  • Faster eval-and-redeploy loop. Together's "tweak settings, redeploy" cycle is a few clicks. kolm asks you to re-compile, which is longer per cycle (though you get a fresh file out of it).

Can I use both?

Yes. Together is a reasonable hosted-inference backend for the frontier-grade tier of your stack while you compile high-volume sub-tasks into .kolm files for the local tier. The kolm capture gateway preserves the upstream call, so the Together endpoint keeps doing its job until the local artifact is ready.

Bottom line

Pick Together AI if you don't want to operate inference and you need broad base-model coverage. The serving pool is excellent and the breadth is real.

Pick kolm if you want the file, $0 marginal cost, on-device or air-gapped deploy, or a signed artifact a compliance officer can verify years later.
