Privacy

Your data, your boundary.

Kolm is an open-source compiler that runs in the customer environment. The default deployment never sends customer prompts, completions, model weights, or eval-pack runs to kolm.ai infrastructure. This page documents what we collect when you use the hosted gateway, what we never collect, how to delete what you have given us, and the developer commands that put data rights in your terminal.

What we collect

A short, complete list. If something is not on this list, we do not collect it. A security or compliance reviewer can map every row to a stored field on the hosted gateway.

accountCollected

Account email

The address you sign up with. Used for login, billing receipts, security notices, and any policy-change announcements you have not unsubscribed from. Stored under users.email.

billingCollected

Billing details

Card token from Stripe (never the PAN), billing address, and invoice line items: per-tenant request counts, plan tier, overages. No prompt or completion content is logged in billing rows.

registryCollected (only if you publish)

Public receipt CIDs

If you run kolm publish we store the content-addressed hash of the receipt so downstream verifiers can resolve it at /verify/<cid>. The published receipt is signed JSON listing K-score, eval-pack hash, runtime metadata, and deployment-specific model or index references – it never includes weights, captures, or completions.

gatewayCollected (only if you route through us)

Gateway request metadata

Timestamp, route, token counts, latency, HTTP status, and the API key fingerprint. Prompt and completion bodies are not stored on the hosted gateway. Captures live in ~/.kolm/events on your side.

supportCollected (if you write to us)

Support tickets you send

When you email privacy@kolm.ai or support@kolm.ai we keep the thread so the next engineer on rotation has context. Tickets are deleted on request.

securityCollected

Authentication audit log

Sign-in events, API key rotations, and admin actions on the hosted control plane. Retained for the regulated minimum needed to investigate incidents.

What we do not collect by default

Every item below stays on the customer side of the boundary. The compiler ships with no upstream pipe for these categories. A developer can confirm by running with --offline and watching there is no egress.

Stays inside your network

Captures
Prompts, tool calls, completions, and trace bodies land in ~/.kolm/events (or the path you point KOLM_DATA_DIR at). Tenant-scoped, namespace-scoped, never replicated to us.
Distill datasets
Teacher-student pairs from kolm distill are written next to the captures they came from. We do not see them, we do not index them, we do not score them.
Model weights
Base weights, distilled weights, INT4 and INT8 quantizations are written to your filesystem or your object store. The .kolm artifact is a zip you control.
Eval pack runs
K-score gates execute against the artifact locally. Scores and axes are computed in-process and stored next to the artifact.
Traces and logs
Trace IDs, tool outputs, retrieval contexts. Use the OTLP exporter if you want them in Datadog, Grafana, Honeycomb, or your SIEM.
SSO directory data
On the enterprise plan, SCIM user and group rows live in your IdP. We mirror the minimum needed to authorize requests and we drop on deprovision.

Cookies and analytics

A short policy because we collect a short list. Product pages and developer surfaces are cookie-free. Marketing pages carry a single first-party analytics cookie that you can opt out of.

productNo cookies

/docs/* and product pages

Reference docs, the gateway dashboard, capture viewers, distill control panels, and the verifier set no analytics or marketing cookies. A session cookie holds your auth token only when you are signed in.

marketingOpt-out cookie

Marketing pages only

Pages such as the homepage, pricing, and case studies carry a first-party analytics cookie. No third-party trackers, no ad pixels. Set kolm-no-track=1 in your browser to disable.

CLIOff by default

CLI telemetry

The CLI does not phone home unless you set KOLM_TELEMETRY=1. The opt-in counter records command name and exit code only. No prompt content, no flag values, no filenames.

Where your data lives

The hosted gateway and registry run in US-East by default. EU residency is available on the enterprise plan. The compiler does not care which region you run in; the regions below apply only to the hosted services.

defaultUS-East

Hosted gateway and registry

AWS us-east-1. Encrypted at rest with KMS-backed keys and in transit with TLS 1.3. Daily backups are encrypted and held in the same region. Cross-region replication is off unless you have purchased a multi-region SLA.

enterpriseEU residency

EU data residency

On the enterprise plan, the gateway and registry run from AWS eu-west-1 or eu-central-1. SCCs are baked into the DPA. Email privacy@kolm.ai to request the residency addendum.

your-sideWherever you run it

Captures and weights

Stored wherever KOLM_DATA_DIR points. Default is the user home directory. For team deployments, point it at an encrypted volume, S3-with-object-lock, or a CIFS share you control. No region constraint from us.

air-gappedNo region at all

Disconnected deployments

For regulated and classified environments, the CLI and runtime are static binaries with no outbound calls. See the air-gapped path on security for the verified install.

Subject rights

Export, delete, port, correct. For data we hold on the hosted gateway, every right is exercisable from the CLI without a ticket. For data on your side of the boundary, the same commands work because you own the filesystem.

exportLive

Right to access

Run kolm account export to receive a JSON bundle of every row we hold for you on the hosted gateway: account, billing summary, audit log, published CIDs. Captures and weights are already on your machine; nothing to export from us.

deleteLive

Right to erasure

Run kolm capture delete --subject <id> to remove rows for a data subject and write a tombstone receipt. For account closure, run kolm account delete; the hosted gateway purges billing history (after the regulated retention minimum) and audit rows.

portLive

Right to portability

The export bundle is plain JSON. Captures are JSON Lines. Artifacts are zip files. There is no proprietary container. Run kolm export --since 2026-01-01 --out ./bundle to package a date range.

# Exercise data rights from the CLI.
kolm account export # JSON bundle of hosted-side rows
kolm capture delete --subject "subject-42"
kolm capture delete --namespace "prod-eu" --before 2025-12-31
kolm account delete # closes account after grace window
kolm export --since 2026-01-01 --out ./bundle.zip

Sharing with third parties

Minimal by design. The only third parties that ever see your prompts or completions are the teacher endpoints you point at, and only if you choose the hosted teacher path. Everything else stays in your VPC. The full list is on subprocessors.

teacher pathIf you choose it

Anthropic, OpenAI, Google

Invoked only when you set KOLM_DISTILL_TEACHER to a hosted endpoint. Bring-your-own-key: when you supply your own provider API key (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, or any other upstream model provider), kolm routes your request directly to that provider without proxying or holding the response in transit, and kolm is not the contracting party with the provider for those calls. Free-tier exception: the free /v1/free/chat surface and the W-M Vercel teacher-chat fallback are different – these routes use kolm's own provider account, so for those specific paths kolm is the contracting party with the upstream provider, and the gateway metadata logging described above still applies. To avoid that path, supply your own provider key in /account/settings. Self-hosted vLLM, SGLang, and TGI teachers keep all traffic in your network.

infrastructureAlways

AWS, Cloudflare, Stripe, Resend

Compute, CDN, billing, transactional email for the hosted gateway. None of these see captures or weights. See subprocessors for purpose, data category, location, and the vendor DPA links.

SSOEnterprise plan only

WorkOS

Powers SAML, OIDC, and SCIM. The identity records we touch are limited to email, user ID, and group memberships needed to authorize requests. Disabled unless you have provisioned SSO.

neverAd networks, data brokers

Not now, not ever

We do not sell, rent, or license your data. We do not run ad pixels on product pages. We do not enrich your account with broker data. Customer Content is never used to train any model, including ours.

Children's data

A short section because we do not target children. The product is for engineers and operators, not consumers.

No accounts under 13

We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 13. If you believe a child has created an account, email privacy@kolm.ai and we will delete the account and any associated rows. For EU subjects, the threshold is the age set by the member state where the subject is resident.

Changes and notification

Privacy changes are public, dated, and diffable. No silent rewrites.

How we notify you

Material changes are announced by email to the account contact at least 30 days before they take effect. A dated diff is posted to changelog. Non-material changes (clarifications, typo fixes, vendor address updates) are posted to the changelog without email.

Version history

Every effective date in this document corresponds to a tagged commit in the public site repo. To read a prior version, walk back through the changelog. The current effective date is at the top of this page.

How to reach us

For privacy requests, deletion confirmations, residency questions, or to flag a concern. We do not use a generic contact form for privacy.

Privacy desk

Email privacy@kolm.ai. Acknowledged within 2 business days. Substantive response within 30 days for subject-rights requests, faster for GDPR Article 15-22 when statutory windows apply.

Data Protection Officer

Required for EU subjects. Email dpo@kolm.ai. The DPO is independent of the product and engineering teams and can escalate to the company officers.
