Severity ladder

Four tiers. Each has its own paging cadence and disclosure clock.

The classification is set at first triage and may be raised (never lowered) as the incident unfolds.

Sev 1
Customer data exposure or full service outage

Page on-call <15 min. Status page banner <30 min. Email-affected customers <4h. Public post-mortem <14 days.

Sev 2
Partial outage or degraded latency > 2x baseline

Page on-call <30 min. Status page banner <1h. Email customers if mitigation requires user action. Post-mortem <30 days.

Sev 3
Minor degradation, single-region or single-namespace

On-call ticket <1h. Status page note <2h. No proactive email. Post-mortem on request.

Sev 4
Internal only, no customer impact

Triaged async. No external disclosure. Logged for SOC 2 audit trail.

The clock

From detect to disclose. Six checkpoints.

These targets apply to Sev 1. Sev 2-4 slide on the same shape with longer windows.

T+0

Detect

Alert from monitoring or external report

Source: Sentry, internal probes, customer ticket, or HackerOne. All sources page the same rotation.

T+15m

On-call ack

Engineer joins the incident bridge

Classification set. Incident commander assigned. Comms lead assigned.

T+30m

Public banner

status.kolm.ai posted

Initial banner: what is broken, who is affected, what we are doing. No internal jargon, no speculation.

T+4h

Affected-customer email

If customer data may be exposed

Direct email to namespace admins. Identifies which namespaces, what data type, what the recommended action is.

T+resolve

All-clear

Banner cleared + status RGB returns green

Status page lists root cause one-liner. Detailed RCA waits for the post-mortem.

T+14d

Public post-mortem

Published at kolm.ai/security/post-mortems

What broke, why, the fix, what we changed structurally so this class of incident does not recur. No blame on individuals.
