Scope

In scope, out of scope.

Anything not on the in-scope list is out of scope. When in doubt, ask before testing.

In scope
*.kolm.ai · api.kolm.ai · @kolm/cli npm

Production gateway endpoints, account UI, CLI npm package, and the signed-receipt verifier. Self-hosted artifacts only in scope if reachable via the public account.

In scope (priority)
Receipt forgery · signature bypass · capture leakage

Bugs that let an attacker forge a kolm-audit-1 receipt, bypass Ed25519 verification, or read captures across tenants. These hit max payout.

Out of scope
3rd-party services · social eng · DoS

AWS, Vercel, Cloudflare, Stripe, Postmark, Sentry, HackerOne itself. No social engineering of staff. No volumetric DoS. No physical attacks.

Out of scope
Self-XSS, CSRF on logout, version disclosure, missing security headers without exploitation

Findings of these types may earn reputation but are not paid.

Payout

Severity ladder + payment guideline.

Severity follows CVSS 3.1 with kolm-specific adjustments for receipt-system bugs.

Critical (CVSS 9.0+)
up to $15,000

Receipt forgery, full tenant takeover, capture lake read across tenants, signing-key exfiltration.

High (CVSS 7.0-8.9)
up to $5,000

Single-tenant takeover, namespace data leak, auth bypass on a sensitive endpoint.

Medium (CVSS 4.0-6.9)
up to $1,000

Stored XSS, CSRF on a sensitive action, billing-tier bypass that gains paid features.

Low (CVSS 0.1-3.9)
$100 - $500

Information disclosure, rate-limit bypass without amplification, cosmetic issues that have security implications.

Process

Triage in 3 business days. Fix-or-explain in 30 days. Disclosure at 90.

You retain the right to publish your finding 90 days after the initial report regardless of fix status. We will not legal-threaten good-faith research.

1

Report via HackerOne

in: PoC + impact · out: H1 ticket

Include a minimal PoC and the impact you observed. We do not need video; reproducible steps + curl is ideal.

2

Triage

in: report · out: severity + bounty estimate

3 business days to ack. Severity set with you in the loop; if we disagree we explain on-ticket.

3

Fix or explain

in: triaged report · out: patch + commit SHA

30 days to fix or to explain why we are not fixing. Either way you get the bounty on triage.

4

Disclosure

in: fix shipped · out: CVE + public credit

90 days after report (or immediately if you prefer to wait for the fix). Credit on the patch commit + on the HackerOne hall of fame.

Safe harbor

Good-faith research is safe-harbored.

If you act in good faith and within scope, we will not pursue legal action under CFAA, DMCA, or equivalent. We will defend you against third-party legal action you face as a direct result of your research. Full safe-harbor text on the HackerOne program page.
