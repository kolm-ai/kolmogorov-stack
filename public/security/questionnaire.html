Security questionnaire

50 pre-answered procurement questions. Linkable per-question.

v2026.05.27 · refreshed quarterly · if a question you need is missing, mail security@kolm.ai

What this is. Most enterprise vendor reviews ask the same 50 questions. Rather than answer them one redline at a time, every answer is here and linkable. Send your security team this URL plus the SOC 2 Type II report (NDA at /security/soc2-type2) and most reviews close in one round.

Constraints. Where the answer is "we are working on this" or "this is a customer-side responsibility" we say so. The point is to be linkable, not to oversell.

1.Data & residency

Q1 · #q-1

Where is customer data stored?

SaaS: us-east-1 (AWS, Vercel, Railway) with an EU mirror (eu-west-1) for customers on the Business tier. Self-hosted: in your cloud account or air-gapped data center. We never see the data.

see: /security/posture

Q2 · #q-2

Is data ever transferred outside the chosen region?

No, with one exception: frontier-model fallback. If you route a call through the Wrapper to (e.g.) anthropic, that call hits Anthropic's US endpoint. Disable cross-region fallback in gateway.toml with region_lock = true and the gateway will refuse to fall back to a non-region-pinned provider.

see: /docs/gateway-region-lock

Q3 · #q-3

Do you support EU data residency / GDPR Article 28?

Yes. EU mirror is on the Business tier (us-east-1 → eu-west-1). DPA available at /legal/dpa. Article 28 sub-processor list at /security/sub-processors.

Q4 · #q-4

What customer data do you hold by default?

Authentication metadata (email, hashed API key), namespace metadata, capture envelopes you ship to the Wrapper, and compiled artifact manifests. Prompts / completions are captured only when the namespace has capture enabled — default is off.

Q5 · #q-5

Can you process PHI / HIPAA-regulated data?

A BAA is available on the Business tier. The SaaS path is configured for HIPAA-compatible storage. Self-hosted in your VPC is the simpler answer for regulated workloads.

2.Encryption & keys

Q6 · #q-6

Encryption at rest?

AES-256-GCM for all object storage (S3, Postgres column-level for high-sensitivity columns). AWS-managed KMS by default; BYOK (Bring Your Own Key) on the Business tier via AWS KMS or HashiCorp Vault transit secrets.

Q7 · #q-7

Encryption in transit?

TLS 1.3 only. HSTS with preload (max-age 63072000). Certificates issued by AWS ACM and rotated automatically. No TLS 1.0 / 1.1 endpoints; TLS 1.2 fallback is disabled.

Q8 · #q-8

How are API keys stored?

Argon2id (memory-hard) hash with per-key salt. Never logged in plaintext. The plaintext key is shown once at creation; if lost, rotate.

Q9 · #q-9

Receipt signing key management?

Per-tenant Ed25519 keypair. Private key encrypted at rest under the tenant master key (KMS). Public key surfaces at /v1/keys/pub for offline receipt verification. kolm receipts rotate-key for periodic rotation.

Q10 · #q-10

Do you support customer-managed encryption keys (CMEK / BYOK)?

Yes, on the Business tier (AWS KMS or Vault transit). Self-hosted: you bring your own KMS.

3.Identity & access

Q11 · #q-11

Single sign-on (SSO / SAML)?

SAML 2.0 IdP-initiated and SP-initiated. Metadata at /v1/sso/saml/metadata. Tested with Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, Auth0.

Q12 · #q-12

SCIM provisioning?

SCIM 2.0 endpoint at /v1/sso/scim for user / group lifecycle. ServiceProviderConfig exposes capabilities per RFC 7644.

Q13 · #q-13

Multi-factor authentication?

TOTP and WebAuthn / passkey for the web console. SSO-mandated MFA flows through unchanged when SAML is enabled. Required for users in the admin role on Business plans.

Q14 · #q-14

Role-based access control?

Roles: owner, admin, maintainer, contributor, viewer. Per-namespace ACLs override tenant-level role. Audit log records every role change.

Q15 · #q-15

Session timeouts and idle policy?

12-hour absolute session lifetime, 30-minute idle timeout. Configurable down to 1-hour / 5-minute on Business tier. Sessions invalidated on password change, key rotation, or admin revoke.

4.Tenancy & isolation

Q16 · #q-16

How are tenants isolated?

Logical isolation at every layer: row-level tenant_id on Postgres, S3 key prefixes per tenant, and a tenant-fence enforced in src/auth.js on every request. Cross-tenant reads are a high-severity bug-class — we have lock-in tests that fail the build if a query is added without a tenant predicate.

Q17 · #q-17

Do you offer dedicated infrastructure?

Yes — Business tier offers single-tenant deployments (dedicated VPC + Postgres + S3 bucket). Self-hosted in your account is the strongest isolation.

Q18 · #q-18

Is customer data ever co-mingled in caches?

No. The capture lake and artifact store are partitioned by tenant_id all the way to the storage backend. CDN caching applies only to public marketing pages and the docs site, never to authenticated routes (the Vercel rewrite explicitly excludes /v1/* from cache).

5.Network & perimeter

Q19 · #q-19

What ports are open at the perimeter?

443 only. The API server has no inbound port other than HTTPS. Self-hosted defaults are the same; kolm serve binds to an explicit port (default 8765) and you choose your reverse proxy.

Q20 · #q-20

IP allow-list?

Yes, configurable per-namespace under access_control.allowed_ip_cidrs. Requests from outside the allow-list return 403 with an audit-logged reason.

Q21 · #q-21

Web application firewall?

Cloudflare WAF in front of kolm.ai. Standard OWASP rule set + bot management. Rate-limit rules also enforced at the application layer in case the WAF is bypassed.

Q22 · #q-22

DDoS protection?

Cloudflare L3/4 and L7 DDoS protection on all public endpoints. Self-hosted: bring your own (Cloudflare, AWS Shield, etc.) — the deployment guide includes recommended configs.

6.Audit & logging

Q23 · #q-23

What gets audit-logged?

Every authenticated request (who, when, route, response code), every role change, every key rotation, every compile / deploy / serve event, every capture-approval action. Logs are append-only with an HMAC chain so deletion is detectable.

Q24 · #q-24

How long are audit logs retained?

365 days hot, 7 years cold (S3 Glacier). Business tier customers can export to their own SIEM (Splunk, Datadog, Sumo Logic) via a streaming connector.

Q25 · #q-25

Can the audit log be tampered with?

Each log entry includes prev_chain_hash = HMAC(secret, sorted-key JSON of prior entry). A deletion or modification breaks the chain and is detected on read via kolm audit verify-chain.

Q26 · #q-26

Do you offer audit log streaming?

Yes. Webhook (HTTPS POST + HMAC signature) or S3 sink. Streaming on Business tier; Pro tier uses pull-based export.

7.Incident response

Q27 · #q-27

Do you have a documented incident response plan?

Yes — available under NDA. Public summary at /security/incident-response. Severity 1 paging targets <15 minutes; root-cause public post-mortem within 14 days for any incident that affects customer data.

Q28 · #q-28

Breach notification SLA?

72 hours for incidents involving customer data, regardless of regulatory minimum. DPA codifies this commitment.

Q29 · #q-29

Status page?

status.kolm.ai — uptime, latency, and active incidents. Updated automatically from the same Sentry / uptime probes the engineering team uses.

Q30 · #q-30

Disaster recovery RTO / RPO?

RTO 4 hours, RPO 15 minutes. Postgres point-in-time recovery + cross-region read replicas. Quarterly DR drill; the most recent drill report is available under NDA.

8.Deletion & retention

Q31 · #q-31

How do I delete my data?

Self-serve: Settings → Account → Delete in the web console, or kolm account delete --confirm. Soft-delete for 30 days, then hard-delete from primary + replicas + cold storage. Backups expire on rolling 90-day cycle.

Q32 · #q-32

Per-record deletion (GDPR Article 17)?

Yes — kolm captures delete --capture-id <id> or the equivalent web action. Deletion is propagated to the hash chain via a tombstone entry so the chain stays verifiable while the body is gone.

Q33 · #q-33

Data export?

Capture lake: JSONL, Parquet, Hugging Face datasets format. Receipts: CSV, JSONL. Artifacts: native .kolm (portable, signed). All exports stream from /v1/captures/export and /v1/receipts/export; no email-me-a-zip flow.

Q34 · #q-34

Do you use customer data to train models?

No. Captures stay in the customer's tenant; only that tenant can compile against them. We do not aggregate, anonymize, or include any customer data in our own training. The trinity-500 student card calls out every source dataset; none are customer-derived.

9.Sub-processors

Q35 · #q-35

Who are your sub-processors?

Full list at /security/sub-processors. Core: AWS (us-east-1, eu-west-1), Vercel (CDN + frontend), Railway (API), Cloudflare (WAF + DNS), Stripe (billing), Postmark (transactional email), Sentry (error monitoring).

Q36 · #q-36

How are sub-processors vetted?

Each one ships under our DPA template; SOC 2 Type II or ISO 27001 evidence reviewed on add and on annual re-cert.

Q37 · #q-37

Will you notify me before adding a sub-processor?

Yes — 30 days advance notice to the address listed in your contract. You can object in writing; if we cannot accommodate, you have a 30-day termination right.

11.AI-specific risk

Q43 · #q-43

How do you prevent prompt injection?

Defense in depth: structured input parsing, system-prompt isolation, untrusted-content tagging, and the kolm-audit-1 receipt schema that records the exact prompt + completion so injections are auditable after the fact. Full posture at /security/prompt-extraction.

Q44 · #q-44

Training data poisoning?

Five-signal poison detector on every capture before it enters the compile pool: low-entropy floods, gradient-attack patterns, repeated-substring attacks, content-policy violations, and one-shot-jailbreak templates. Quarantined samples never enter training. See /security/model-poisoning.

Q45 · #q-45

Membership inference?

Compile pipeline includes a membership-inference probe and gates the artifact if the probe AUC exceeds a tenant-configurable threshold (default 0.55). Detail at /security/membership-inference.

Q46 · #q-46

Red-team / safety evaluation?

Every compiled artifact runs through a 200-prompt red-team battery (jailbreaks, PII extraction, self-harm, hate, copyright). Failures gate the artifact. Battery + leaderboard at /security/red-team.

Q47 · #q-47

Model card / passport?

Every artifact ships a passport.json with full provenance: teacher models, recipe versions, training pair counts, eval results, K-Score, hallucination rate, source datasets. kolm passport export --format compliance produces a model-risk-review-ready PDF.

12.Deployment models

Q48 · #q-48

Self-hosted / bring-your-own-cloud?

Yes. Docker Compose, Kubernetes (Helm chart shipped), VPC reference Terraform. kolm deploy bundle --airgap produces a self-contained tarball for offline installation. Full guide at /docs/self-hosted-deploy-complete.

Q49 · #q-49

Air-gapped deployment?

Supported. Air-gap bundle includes the API server, the compile pipeline, all model weights you license, and a local certificate authority. License and signature verification works offline; receipts continue to be Ed25519-signed against your local key.

Q50 · #q-50

FedRAMP / IL5 / classified environments?

FedRAMP Moderate is on the roadmap (target 2027). For IL5 / classified, the air-gap bundle is the supported path today — the entire stack runs in your environment with no outbound calls. See /government.
