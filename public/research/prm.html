Process Reward Models (PRMs)

A process reward model scores each step of a chain-of-thought, not just the final answer. The signal is denser, which lets you select better solutions at inference time and train better policies with RL. The cost is the dense step-level supervision the PRM needs to learn from.

What it is

The standard reward model in RLHF emits a single scalar over the entire response. For tasks where the final answer follows from many intermediate steps (mathematics, multi-hop reasoning, code synthesis with tools) this aggregates information loosely; a wrong step compensated by a lucky guess at the end reads the same as a fully correct trajectory. Lightman et al. (2023) introduced the term process reward model in Let's Verify Step by Step: train a model to score the correctness of each step in a math solution, using the PRM800K dataset of step-level human labels on GSM8K and MATH solutions. They show step-level reward beats outcome-only reward on MATH by 11 points when used to re-rank candidate solutions at test time. Follow-up work (Wang et al. 2024, Math-Shepherd) replaces the expensive human labels with automatic step labels derived from Monte Carlo rollouts: a step is "good" if continuing from it eventually reaches the correct answer with high probability. The trade-off is noise; automatic labels are cheaper but noisier than human ones.

How kolm uses it

In kolm v0.2.6 the gate scores the final response only. PRM integration ships in v0.3, paired naturally with GRPO (/research/grpo) for multi-step recipes. The implementation starts from the Math-Shepherd automatic-labeling pipeline because manual step labels do not scale to the typical kolm tenant. The PRM trains alongside the student, with the per-step scores feeding both the inference-time best-of-N selector and the RL stage's reward. The receipt records the PRM checkpoint hash, the labelling method, and the per-step score distribution on the holdout.
