Supervised fine-tuning

Supervised fine-tuning is cross-entropy on labelled prompt-response pairs. It is the simplest and most reliable post-training method, the workhorse behind response distillation, and the baseline every preference-optimization method (DPO, KTO, ORPO, GRPO) is measured against.

What it is

Supervised fine-tuning takes a base language model and continues training it with the standard next-token cross-entropy objective on a curated set of (prompt, response) pairs. The model's parameters update to maximize the log-likelihood of each target response conditioned on its prompt. The method was named in Ouyang et al. (2022, Training Language Models to Follow Instructions) as the first stage of InstructGPT, and predates the term itself by decades in the form of plain supervised learning. SFT is the dominant fine-tuning method in production because the loss is convex per token, gradients are well-behaved, and the training loop is identical to pretraining. What makes SFT good: a clean prompt format, a response set with low intra-cluster variance on the answer field, and a learning rate small enough to preserve base-model capability outside the training distribution. What makes SFT bad: distribution shift on rare prompts, overfitting to verbose response patterns, and the inability to express preferences (the model learns what to say, not what not to say). PEFT methods (LoRA, QLoRA; Hu et al. 2021, Dettmers et al. 2023) make SFT cheap by training a low-rank adapter rather than full weights; for the 1B to 14B student range typical of kolm, a rank-32 LoRA on attention and MLP projections recovers within 1 percent of full fine-tune K-score at a tenth of the compute.

How kolm uses it

SFT is the actual training loop behind every distillation mode in kolm v0.2.6. Response distillation (/research/response-distill) is SFT on teacher-sampled responses. Rationale distillation (shipping in v0.3) is SFT on rationale-then-answer sequences. The trainer is a thin wrapper over Hugging Face TRL's SFTTrainer with kolm-specific extensions: per-row receipt linkage, LoRA-by-default with rank chosen from the recipe size class (rank 16 for 1B students, rank 32 for 3B to 8B, rank 64 for 14B), gradient checkpointing on by default, and the K-score gate run on holdout after each epoch with early stopping when the curve plateaus. The receipt records the trainer git revision, the LoRA config, the per-epoch holdout K-scores, and the final selected checkpoint hash. Full fine-tune (no LoRA) is opt-in per recipe and is required for any artifact that will be quantized below 4-bit, where adapter weights merge poorly.
