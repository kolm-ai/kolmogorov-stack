02 - What Guards What You Read

Every line on this site has to pass before it ships.

We run our own copy through the same discipline we built the product on. If a page loads, it earned it - routing, fresh copy, working links, claims tied to proof, and real screenshots all checked first.

Site tests

You land on the page you clicked

Direct route fetches confirm titles and current copy for trust, security, enterprise, contact, platform, capabilities, research, and changelog - no wrong turns.

Rewrite tests

No page can quietly serve the wrong thing

Rewrite checks keep every product page on its own content, so a stale alias can't take its place.

Stale-copy tests

Old messaging can't sneak back in

Automated searches block outdated copy and broken characters from returning to a rebuilt page after we've moved on.

Reference lint

Every link and asset resolves

A reference audit checks internal links, images, manifests, docs paths, and route targets across the whole site - so nothing you click goes nowhere.

Claim scope

The copy can't outrun the proof

Claim checks keep certification, benchmark, partner, and adoption statements tied to evidence you can verify - or off the page entirely.

UI screenshots

Readable on the device you're on

Full-page screenshots check spacing, stacking, and layout across desktop and mobile, so the page reads clean wherever you open it.

03 - What We Won't Claim Yet

The claims we're holding back - and why.

You deserve to know what we haven't earned the right to say. These stay off the page until there's evidence you can check for yourself.

Awaiting partner proof

We won't name partners or third-party validation until there's named, checkable evidence behind it. No borrowed credibility.

partner
Awaiting certification

We won't make certification claims until there's live, reviewable evidence you can pull straight from the trust page.

cert
Awaiting a shipped package

We won't advertise a package, SDK, or CLI until you can install a real version and run a real verification command against it.

package
Awaiting public benchmarks

We won't post performance or "better than" claims without public data, method, sample, and limitations you can rerun yourself.
