kolm.ai · legal

Acceptable Use Policy

Applies to the kolm.ai gateway, capture, distill, compile, registry, and runtime surfaces. Effective 2026-05.

Plain-language TLDRBuild things people will be glad you built. Don't use kolm.ai to make CSAM, plan a real-world attack, dox someone, run unlicensed credit/health/legal advice at scale, or evade a sanction. Compiled artifacts you run on your own hardware are governed by your own AUP — we have no visibility there. Report abuse to abuse@kolm.ai.

Permitted — expected even

  • Building shipping products on the gateway.
  • Distilling teacher behavior into smaller specialists.
  • Compiling on-device or air-gapped runtimes.
  • Red-teaming your own systems with our consent.
  • Academic research on AI safety, robustness, and evaluation.

Prohibited — will get you suspended

  • CSAM, non-consensual sexual content, terror content.
  • Operational planning of physical attacks or weapons of mass destruction.
  • Doxxing, harassment, targeted political manipulation at scale.
  • Mass automated impersonation, spam, or social-engineering credential theft.
  • Practicing medicine, law, or credit underwriting at scale without a licensed human review loop.
  • Evading US/EU/UK sanctions or sanctioned-party screening obligations.
  • Reverse-engineering kolm.ai infra, attacking other tenants, or load-testing without prior written notice.

1. Scope

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) governs your access to the kolm.ai cloud-hosted control plane and its API. It does not govern artifacts you have compiled and run on your own infrastructure — those are governed by your own AUP and applicable law. See /security for the boundary.

2. Account hygiene

  • Keep API keys secret. Rotate compromised keys immediately via the dashboard or kolm key rotate.
  • One account per legal entity. Share access via teams, not shared credentials.
  • You are responsible for usage incurred under your API keys, including by automated agents.

3. Capture content responsibility

Captures stored on kolm.ai are content you have approved. You must have a lawful basis to send any Personal Data, regulated content, or third-party IP through the gateway, and you indemnify kolm.ai for claims arising from your captures.

4. Distill and compile responsibility

  • If the teacher model is third-party (Anthropic, OpenAI), distillation must comply with that provider's terms of service.
  • Compiled artifacts inherit the license of the base model weights. Many open-weight licenses (e.g. Llama Community License) carry restrictions; you are responsible for compliance.
  • Do not market a distilled artifact in a way that misrepresents its provenance or the K-Score it actually achieved.

5. Rate limits and fair use

The hosted plane has soft rate limits per plan. Sustained load that materially exceeds those limits without prior coordination may be throttled or temporarily suspended. We will reach out before disabling anything.

6. Security testing

Authorised security research against your own tenant is welcome. Send scope and contact to security@kolm.ai before starting. Unauthorised testing of other tenants, the control plane, or shared infrastructure is prohibited.

7. Reporting abuse

If a kolm.ai-issued artifact or a customer's gateway-hosted product is being used to violate this AUP, report it to abuse@kolm.ai with the URL, the artifact id (if known), and any supporting evidence. We acknowledge within one business day and respond within five.

8. Enforcement

We issue warnings for unclear cases and immediate suspension for clear violations. Suspended accounts can appeal in writing to appeals@kolm.ai; we keep a written reasoning log so appeals can be evaluated by a second reviewer.

9. Updates

We may update this AUP. Material changes are announced at /changelog at least 30 days before they take effect.
