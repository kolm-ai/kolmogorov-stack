Teacher source · per-vendor stance · honest

Distillation and provider terms.

Every frontier-model API has a clause that limits what you can do with its outputs. Here is exactly how kolm reads those clauses, how kolm's defaults handle them, and the open-weight teacher mode that removes the question entirely.

01The risk

Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google all reserve the right to update their API terms. Common clauses today restrict using their outputs to "train, fine-tune, or develop a competing model." The plain reading is broad and the vendors have not always been precise about what counts as "competing." Anyone building distillation on top of those APIs has to think about three failure modes.

First, a clause change tightens enforcement and your existing artifacts become contractually awkward. Second, a vendor declines to renew your account because your account history shows fine-tune-shaped traffic. Third, your downstream customer's counsel reads the clause more strictly than you do and pulls a procurement requirement.

02Kolm's default stance

By default, kolm treats teacher-API outputs as inputs to the user's own training corpus, not as redistributable model weights. The captures land in your ~/.kolm/captures.jsonl, on your disk, under your account. You own them. Kolm does not aggregate them. Kolm does not publish them. Kolm does not use them to improve a hosted model.

The distilled artifact is a separately compiled object. It carries a teacher-source stamp in its manifest (teacher-source disclosure) so downstream consumers can read the provenance and apply their own policy.

03The mitigation: open-weight teacher mode

Open-weight teachers (Llama-3, Qwen, Mistral, DeepSeek) carry no API-side restriction on what you do with their outputs. Their license governs the weights themselves; the inference outputs ride the same license. For most production distillation, this is the path with zero TOS surface area.

# Force open-weight teacher mode (refuses proprietary teachers)
export KOLM_TEACHER_SOURCE=open-weights
kolm distill --teacher qwen2.5 --corpus support-tickets.jsonl

# Or pick a specific open-weight teacher on the command line
kolm distill --teacher llama-3.1-70b-instruct --corpus support-tickets.jsonl

# Verify the artifact records the source
kolm verify dist/support-redactor.kolm | grep teacher_source
# teacher_source: open-weights

When KOLM_TEACHER_SOURCE=open-weights is set, the distill pipeline refuses any teacher that is not on the open-weight allowlist. You get an explicit error instead of an artifact that quietly carries proprietary lineage.

05What we will not do

Kolm will not encourage you to use a proprietary teacher in a way that the teacher's TOS plainly prohibits. The CLI defaults to refusing proprietary teachers when KOLM_TEACHER_SOURCE=open-weights is set. The default registry entries for hosted distillation are open-weight teachers. The honest path is the easy path.
