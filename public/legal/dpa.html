What the DPA covers

Six things, every Article 28 DPA needs to specify.

Each row links to the binding clause in the PDF and the operative section of the Terms of Service.

Subject matter
Provision of the kolm Gateway + Compiler

Processing on behalf of the controller (you), in the scope of API calls made through the gateway and artifacts compiled from captured data.

Duration
For the term of the subscription + 30 days

30 days after termination, captures + receipts are exported to you on request and then deleted. kolm captures export + kolm receipts export for self-service.

Nature + purpose
Inference, training, and audit retention

We process to (a) route inference calls, (b) train distilled models when you trigger compile, and (c) retain signed receipts for the audit-trail TTL you configure.

Type of data
Whatever you send through the gateway

By default, prompts and completions. PII redactor (gateway.toml redact_pii=true) strips email/phone/SSN before storage. We have no view of your downstream data otherwise.

Categories of data subject
Your end users + your employees

You are the controller. You are responsible for legal basis and notice to data subjects.

Sub-processors
Pre-authorized + 30-day objection window

Current list at /security/sub-processors. We notify by email + on this page when a new sub-processor is added; you have 30 days to object before they go live.

SCC

Standard Contractual Clauses (2021/914) Module Two.

For transfers from the EU/EEA to the US (where our headquarters and some sub-processors are located), the DPA includes the European Commission's 2021 SCC, Module Two (controller-to-processor). Annexes I, II, III are pre-filled with the parties, processing details, and security measures listed on /security/posture.
