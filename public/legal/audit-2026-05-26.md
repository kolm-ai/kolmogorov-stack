   billing token (Stripe), public receipt CIDs, gateway request metadata

   authentication audit log. Each row has its own card.
2. [PASS] Lists what is NOT collected (lines 176-186): captures, distill datasets,
   model weights, eval-pack runs, traces/logs, SCIM directory data. Each item names
   the on-disk path (`~/.kolm/events`, `KOLM_DATA_DIR`).
3. [GAP] Retention policy is referenced but not quantified per-category. The TOS
   (terms.html line 381) says captures purged within 30 days of termination, backups
   within 90 days, "account email retained only for legal and tax record retention
   windows" - the windows themselves are not stated. The privacy page itself does
   not break retention per category at all.
4. [PARTIAL PASS] Third-party processors enumerated on privacy.html lines 282-300
   (Anthropic/OpenAI/Google, AWS, Cloudflare, Stripe, Resend, WorkOS) but the canonical
   full list lives on `/subprocessors`. The DPA (dpa.html lines 128-133) lists a
   different set: Vercel, Railway, Anthropic+OpenAI, Stripe. Cloudflare, Resend,
   AWS, and WorkOS appear on the privacy page but NOT in the DPA table. Vercel and
   Railway appear in the DPA but NOT on the privacy page. **Subprocessor list is
   inconsistent across documents.**
5. [PASS] GDPR coverage: dpa.html section 2 (controller/processor roles), section 8
   (SCCs Module 2 2021/914), section 9 (Art 15-22 rights in 10 business days),
   section 10 (72-hour breach). privacy.html names a DPO at `dpo@kolm.ai` (line 344).
6. [GAP] California CCPA / CPRA not addressed by name anywhere. No "Do Not Sell or
   Share My Personal Information" link, no Notice at Collection, no California
   resident-specific rights section. "We do not sell, rent, or license your data"
   (line 299) is the closest statement and is good but does not satisfy CPRA notice
   requirements on its own.
7. [PASS] Contact section present: `privacy@kolm.ai`, `dpo@kolm.ai` (lines 339-344),
   plus `support@kolm.ai`, `security@kolm.ai` elsewhere.
8. [GAP] Children's section (line 311) says "we do not knowingly collect ... under
   13" but does not address COPPA verifiable parental consent flow if a child does
   sign up. Acceptable as worded for a B2B developer tool but flag for counsel.

Privacy pass/gap: 5 pass / 3 gap.

## CRITICAL: Distillation clause

### Current state

The product covers the question in **three** places, with three different shapes:

- **terms.html section 5 (line 254)**: "For the self-hosted compiler, acceptable
  use is governed by the laws that apply to you and the upstream model providers
  you choose. Kolm has no enforcement role over a self-hosted install."
- **tos.html section 3 (line 105)**: "No re-publication of frontier teacher
  outputs in a way that violates the upstream teacher provider terms (each teacher
  vendor's terms are linked from the artifact manifest)."
- **legal/distillation-tos-clarity.html**: a long per-vendor table classifying
  Anthropic/OpenAI/Google as `warn` (restricted; "consult counsel") and Llama,
  Qwen, Mistral, DeepSeek as `ok` (open weights, no API restriction). Closes with
  "Not legal advice."

A `KOLM_TEACHER_SOURCE=open-weights` env var refuses proprietary teachers at
distill time, and every `.kolm` artifact stamps `manifest.teacher_source` as
`open-weights | proprietary | mixed`.

### Risk level: HIGH

Reasons:

1. **The hosted gateway proxies Anthropic with kolm's own server-side key.**
   `api/teacher-chat.js` line 244 emits `proxy_key_source`; `src/router.js` line
   8510 wires `POST /v1/free/chat` as a public, IP-rate-limited (20/IP/day) chat
   surface; `src/capture.js` uses `vercel-teacher-chat` as a Vercel-hosted
   fallback that holds the upstream key. This means kolm.ai itself - not the
   end user - is the contracting party with Anthropic for any free-tier traffic,
   AND for any traffic on the Pro/Team/Business tiers that does not use BYOK. If
   a Pro-tier user runs `kolm distill` and the teacher route goes through the
   kolm gateway with kolm's Anthropic key, **kolm is the one violating Anthropic's
   Acceptable Use Policy clause prohibiting training competing models on Claude
   outputs**, not the user.
2. **The TOS does NOT contain a BYOK carve-out clause.** terms.html section 5
   only releases kolm from enforcement for *self-hosted* installs. It does not
   address the much more common case where the user is signed in to the hosted
   gateway, supplies their own provider key (or doesn't), and runs distill. There
   is no clause stating "when you supply your own provider key, you are the
   contracting party with that provider and you bear sole responsibility for
   provider TOS compliance."
3. **The TOS does NOT shift compliance responsibility to the user for the
   gateway-routed case.** The closest language is tos.html line 105 ("no
   re-publication ... in violation of upstream terms") - this is a user-side
   prohibition on REPUBLICATION, not a flow-down of distillation-training
   compliance. It is also in the short TOS only, not the canonical terms.html.
4. **The privacy page line 284 disclaims proxying** ("Bring-your-own-key. We
   do not proxy these calls and we do not see the traffic"). This is contradicted
   by the live code path in `src/capture.js` / `api/teacher-chat.js` and by the
   W-M wave note in the conversation memory ("Vercel teacher-chat proxy fallback,
   post-W-M prod benchmark gateway 2478ms mean 10/10 upstream 2xx"). **The
   privacy claim that kolm never proxies provider calls is currently false for
   the free tier and may be false for paid tiers depending on routing.**
5. **Anthropic Usage Policy 2026** (as cited industry-wide) restricts using
   Claude outputs to "train, fine-tune, or otherwise create or improve a
   competing AI model or service." A distilled student model trained on Claude
   outputs that ships under the kolm registry is, under a strict reading, a
   competing model. The legal/distillation-tos-clarity page acknowledges this
   risk ("warn") but the TOS does not flow the obligation through to the user.
6. **The distill-tos-clarity page disclaims itself** ("Not legal advice. Read
   your provider's terms"). Disclaimers in a marketing page do not substitute
   for an enforceable contractual term in the TOS.

### Recommended language (SUGGESTION ONLY - send to counsel; do NOT paste verbatim)

Add a new section between sections 7 and 8 of `terms.html`, titled
**"7A. Upstream model provider terms and BYOK"**:

> **(a) Hosted gateway provider keys.** When the hosted gateway routes a request
> to a third-party model provider (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, or any other
> hosted provider listed at /subprocessors) using kolm's own provider account or
> key, that route is subject to the upstream provider's terms as applied to kolm.
> Kolm reserves the right to block, throttle, or refuse any request that, in
> kolm's reasonable judgment, would violate the upstream provider's acceptable
> use policy, output-use restriction, or terms governing model training. This
> includes, without limitation, requests that appear directed at building a
> dataset for model training, fine-tuning, or distillation from a provider
> whose terms prohibit such use.
>
> **(b) Bring-your-own-key (BYOK).** Where you configure the gateway, the
> Studio, or any kolm surface to call an upstream model provider using a key,
> token, or credential issued to YOU by that provider (for example by setting
> `KOLM_TEACHER_SOURCE`, `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY`, `OPENAI_API_KEY`, `KOLM_DISTILL_TEACHER`,
> or any equivalent), you are the contracting party with that provider for the
> resulting request. You represent that your use, including any downstream
> training, fine-tuning, distillation, or redistribution of provider outputs,
> complies with that provider's then-current terms. Kolm acts solely as
> technical conduit and disclaims any liability for your compliance posture
> with the upstream provider.
>
> **(c) Distillation and "competing model" clauses.** You acknowledge that
> some upstream provider terms (including, as of the effective date of these
> terms, the Anthropic Usage Policy and the OpenAI Terms of Use) restrict use
> of provider outputs to train or fine-tune a model that competes with the
> provider. You are solely responsible for evaluating whether your intended
> distillation, fine-tuning, or training use is permitted under the upstream
> terms in effect at the time of capture and at the time of training. Kolm
> publishes a teacher-source stamp on every `.kolm` artifact (see
> /legal/teacher-source-disclosure) and an open-weight teacher mode (see
> /legal/distillation-tos-clarity) as engineering aids; neither is a legal
> opinion or a safe harbor.
>
> **(d) Indemnification flow-through.** Section 11 (Indemnification) shall be
> read to include, within your defense obligation to kolm, any third-party
> claim by an upstream model provider arising from your distillation, training,
> or downstream redistribution of that provider's outputs, except to the
> extent the claim arises from kolm's failure to honor your routing
> instruction or kolm's own breach of the upstream terms.

Additionally:

- Add an `effective_date` field at the top of every legal page (acceptable-use,

- Reconcile `tos.html` and `terms.html` to a single canonical TOS. Either delete

  superseded plain-language summary that does not control. The liability-cap

## Top blockers for launch

1. **DISTILLATION + BYOK clause missing from canonical TOS.** This is THE legal
   risk core. The current TOS does not flow Anthropic/OpenAI/Google output-use
   restrictions through to the user, does not contain a BYOK carve-out, and
   does not give kolm a contractual basis to refuse training-shaped requests
   on its own provider key. Combined with the privacy page falsely stating
   kolm does not proxy provider calls (line 284), this creates a meaningful
   exposure when the first frontier provider audits a kolm-derived artifact.
   See "Recommended language" above. **Counsel review required before public
   billed launch.**
2. **Two parallel TOS documents with conflicting liability caps** ($100 vs
   $1,000) and conflicting contact addresses (`dev@kolm.ai` vs
   `legal@kolm.ai`). Pick one.
3. **Privacy page line 284 is materially false** for the free-tier
   `/v1/free/chat` route and the Vercel `teacher-chat` proxy fallback. Either
   rewrite the line ("We proxy free-tier chat through kolm's own provider key;
   the BYOK path bypasses kolm and goes direct") or stop proxying.
4. **CCPA/CPRA section missing.** Add a California-specific section to
   privacy.html before selling to any California-resident consumer; lower urgency
   for B2B-only but still a checkbox most procurement reviewers look for.
5. **Subprocessor list inconsistent** between privacy.html, dpa.html, and
   subprocessors.html. Make one canonical (recommend `/subprocessors`) and have
   the other two pages link instead of duplicating.
6. **Personal Gmail in legal contact line.** Replace `dev@kolm.ai`
   in tos.html section 10 and liability.html bug-bounty line with `legal@kolm.ai`
   and `security@kolm.ai`.
