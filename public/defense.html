How the air-gap is honest

What makes this SCIF-deployable.

No vendor-speak. The exact mechanism for each property, with the CLI verb or install flag you can verify yourself the first time you bring up the appliance.

01

Compile runs inside your enclave

The self-hosted compile server kit ships as a signed Docker image and Helm chart. Deploy into your IL5 or IL6 enclave, point your build pipeline at it, and never reach back across the diode. Inbound only, signed bundles, gpg-verifiable.

02

ITAR boundary is enforced at the boundary

Training corpus, captured prompts, base-model weights, and resulting artifact weights all stay inside the export-control boundary you already maintain. There are no third-party API calls in the build path; the boundary you draw is the boundary kolm respects.

03

Receipts verify offline

Every .kolm carries a signed manifest (spec, seeds, split, train, evals, signatures) that re-verifies offline. A different machine on the air-gap side computes the same artifact_hash bit-for-bit. The receipt chain is byte-exact reproducible.

04

FIPS 140-3 crypto modules

Ed25519 signing and SHA-256 hashing route through FIPS-validated modules. The crypto operator is logged; key custodian and dual-control activation are mandatory in the IL6 profile. KMS-resident keys; no plaintext private material on disk.

05

One-way ingress, audit-only egress

Updates arrive on a signed USB or a one-way data diode; the appliance verifies signatures before applying. The only outbound path is the audit-log stream to the unit's SIEM, gated by your accreditor. No vendor cloud is ever in the path.

06

Signed bundles, signed runtime

The OVA, the Helm chart, every kolm container, and every model weight ship with detached signatures. kolm verify-bundle re-checks the chain against the pinned public key bundle on every boot. Tampering breaks the chain; the appliance refuses to start.

07

Classification markings preserved end-to-end

Capture metadata carries the classification banner from intake through artifact compile. The artifact manifest pins the maximum-allowed classification; an attempt to run a higher-marked input against a lower-cleared artifact fails closed at the gateway.

Honest scope

What kolm does, and what we tell every defense buyer up front.

No "AI weapon" claims. No autonomous-targeting marketing. Read this before the rest of the page becomes interesting.

A

Kolm is not a weapon system

The CLI does not output a kinetic decision. Our distilled artifacts route, summarize, structure, draft, and triage. Anything that crosses into a targeting decision is your application logic, your label, your DoDD 3000.09 review, and your commander's call.

B

We do not file your ATO

If you are deploying kolm in an enclave, the AO owns the ATO. What kolm gives you: the artifact manifests, the signed receipts, the SBOM, the build-provenance attestations your authorization package will need. The eMASS form is yours to file.

C

Models will be wrong sometimes

Our distilled models drop K-score by under 3 points after quantization. They still hallucinate, miss edge cases, and exhibit bias. Every kolm workflow ships with a defer-on-low-confidence path and a human-in-the-loop gate by default; the override is logged with the operator's name on it.

D

We will not skip the export-control review

If you are bringing training data that touches ITAR-controlled technical data, we will ask for the export determination before we engage. Period. The boundary you draw is the boundary kolm respects, and we will not sign a contract that papers over a determination you have not made.

Reference architectures

How defense teams actually deploy kolm.

Same product, several placements. Pick the one that matches your enclave, your network, and your AO's risk tolerance.

A. GovCloud BYOC

Inside your IL5 enclave

Helm chart installed in your AWS GovCloud or Azure Government account. Kolm runs in the same enclave as your existing mission systems. Residency US East-Gov or US West-Gov. Audit-only egress to your SIEM.

helm install kolm kolm/defense \
--set residency=us-gov-east-1 \
--set kms.arn=arn:aws-us-gov:kms:... \
--set egress=audit-only
B. On-prem SCIF

Signed bare-metal install

kolm runs inside your SCIF on Kubernetes or bare metal. Inbound updates only, via signed bundle and gpg verification. Audit-only egress is gated by the AO. No vendor cloud is ever in the path.

$ kolm install --on-prem \
--bundle kolm-2026-05.tar.zst \
--signature kolm-2026-05.sig \
--egress audit-only
C. Air-gap appliance

OVA boot, zero outbound

Signed OVA appliance that boots offline. Pre-loaded model registry. Upgrades arrive on a signed USB or a one-way data diode; the appliance verifies signatures before applying. Receipts verify offline; the appliance never reaches a network.

appliance: kolm-2026-05.ova
signature: kolm-2026-05.sig
verify: gpg --verify (offline)
boot: zero outbound
D. Tactical edge

Ruggedized laptop or comms kit

Single signed .kolm file deploys to a tactical laptop or comms kit. The artifact runs locally with zero network calls. P95 latency 90 ms on a Dell 7400 Rugged. Audit log syncs when the kit next reaches a friendly network.

$ kolm run osint-summary.kolm \
--in feed.utf8 \
--out summary.json \
--offline

Operational footprint

What the program office needs to know.

Day-2 facts. Capacity, support tiers, change management, the boring numbers that decide whether this is something your AO will actually accredit.

i

Footprint per unit or detachment

Reference deployment: three application nodes (8 vCPU, 32 GB RAM each), one inference node with a single A10G or equivalent ruggedized GPU (24 GB VRAM), one Postgres instance, one S3-compatible bucket. Handles ~3,000 inferences per hour on a 7B INT4 specialist.

ii

Change management

Updates arrive as signed bundles every two weeks (security) or monthly (features). The bundle includes a release note, an SBOM, a signature, and a rollback receipt. Air-gap customers ingest via signed USB or diode; you apply on your cadence.

iii

Support tier

Cleared-personnel support tier available for IL5 and IL6 customers. 24/7 paging with a 1-hour Sev-1 response under enterprise contracts. Phone-and-key authentication for all support sessions.

iv

Disaster recovery

Air-gap appliances inherit your DR posture (we ship runbooks for cold-storage restore of artifacts, signing keys, and audit logs). GovCloud BYOC: RPO 15 min, RTO 4 hours with cross-region replication.

v

Telemetry

Prometheus-format metrics on /metrics (opt-in). OpenTelemetry traces (opt-in). Structured logs on stdout. Zero mission content in any telemetry stream by default; opt-in flags add only structural identifiers (artifact CID, namespace name).

vi

Pricing

BYOC: your cloud bill plus our flat enterprise license. Air-gap appliance: per-appliance license with a multi-year support tier. Tactical-edge: per-device license at scale. Talk to us; we have not blown a program office's budget yet.
