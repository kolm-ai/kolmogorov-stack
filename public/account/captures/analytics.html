Capture analytics.

W811 capture analytics for one namespace. Volume + topic clusters (bag-of-bigrams nearest-centroid), per-cluster K-Score breakdown when bakeoff scores exist, and an IDR (Important-Distinct-Recent) staleness gauge that summarizes how fresh the corpus is. Every cluster emits a per-cluster gap signal to the active-learning loop (W815).

IDR staleness gauge.

Important-Distinct-Recent. 0 means every captured row is recent (last 7d). 1 means the corpus is fully stale relative to the 30d comparison window. A high gauge in a production namespace is the signal that your training data is drifting from your real traffic.

Cluster bubbles.

Each bubble is one topic cluster. Bubble size scales with capture volume; the warning border highlights clusters with below-mean K-Score (where the student is weakest). Hover for top tokens; the table below shows the per-cluster K-Score breakdown.

Per-cluster K-Score breakdown.

K-Score statistics roll up only the rows in each cluster that carry a recorded score (W812 bakeoff results or W720 self-improvement runs). Clusters with no scored rows show no_samples rather than a fabricated zero.
