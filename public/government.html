FedRAMP · NIST 800-53 posture

What we actually claim about authorization.

Stated plainly so your ATO writer knows what to inherit and what to own.

Plain statement

kolm OSS is not itself FedRAMP-authorized. It is open-source software (Apache 2.0). Open-source software is not a FedRAMP service offering, so it cannot be on the marketplace as one.

What kolm does instead: it runs on your authorized infrastructure — AWS GovCloud (FedRAMP High), Azure Government, on-prem racks under your ATO. The package you authorize is your stack, not ours.

The audit story kolm gives you is the receipt: every inference produces an Ed25519-signed record of what model ran on what input at what time. That record is what your AU-2, AU-3, and AU-12 control narratives reference.

If it helps your team, we can sit with your ISSO and draft SSP language that maps kolm components (CLI, distillation worker, inference runtime, receipt store) to NIST 800-53 Rev 5 control families. That is a working session, not a certification.

Reference architectures

Three shapes that fit.

Patterns we have walked partners through. Pick the one closest to your boundary.

Federal civilian · AWS GovCloud
Air-gapped VPC inside GovCloud. Distillation runs on GPU capacity inside that VPC (Modal-in-VPC or equivalent). Inference deploys to a Bedrock-equivalent on-prem runtime backed by vLLM or TGI. The pipeline never reaches a commercial region.
DoD IL5 · on-prem
Fully on-prem, no cloud edge. Local teacher (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B INT4) on a single L40S. Distilled student deploys to classified hardware. Receipts written to a write-once volume inside the enclave.
State / local · Azure Gov or GovCloud
Single-tenant managed deploy. kolm components run in your subscription / account, your KMS keys, your VNet / VPC. We help you stand it up; ongoing operation is yours.
What we don’t claim

Limitations.

Stated up front so procurement does not get a surprise on call two.

Constraints

kolm OSS is not certified by any government. It is a tool you can deploy in environments that need certification — the certification belongs to the environment, not the tool.

  • We do not store your training data. The pipeline reads from your storage and writes back to your storage.
  • We do not have access to your inference traffic. Inference runs in your process, on your hardware.
  • You audit us by reading the source. Apache 2.0 on GitHub — every file, every commit, every release.
  • kolm.ai (the company) does not have a FedRAMP authorization. The kolm runtime, distilled on your data and run on your hardware, inherits your authorization — not ours.
  • Any claim a vendor (including this page) makes about a control is just a claim until your assessor reviews the artifact. The receipts are the artifact.
