03 / Data handling

We see less than you think, and keep it for less time.

The audit reads redacted activity logs, not your production secrets. You redact before upload, the credentials we hold are scoped and revocable, and what you send is used for the engagement and not retained beyond it.

Redaction

Secrets stay out at the source

You redact before anything reaches us. The scan reads the structure of agent activity, the scopes, the destinations, the trail, not the payloads underneath. Bring redacted logs, not raw data.

ASR-3 · data egress

Scoped keys

Least privilege, revocable

Where a connector is used, it holds read-only, narrowly scoped credentials, and you can revoke them the moment the audit ends. We ask for the minimum that lets the check run.

ASR-1 · least privilege

Retention

Kept for the engagement, not after

Redacted logs are used to produce the signed report and are not retained beyond the engagement. Subprocessors are listed in full, and the data terms are written down, not implied.

DPA · Subprocessors

Scope is contractual. Permission posture, redaction and audit-trail integrity are assessed. Injection is tested and reported, not warranted.

04 / Transparency log

Every report is logged where anyone can watch.

Each issuance is appended to a public, tamper-evident log, so a reviewer can confirm the report you handed them is the one we signed and that we have not quietly rewritten history. It is an append-only Merkle log in the RFC 6962 style, built on Ed25519 and SHA-256. It is not a blockchain.

schemerfc6962-style merkle log
leafsha-256(canonical_report_bytes)
signed25519(issuer_sk, leaf)
treeappend-only leaves never edited
rootsha-256 merkle root signed checkpoint
proofinclusion proof per report
monitorconsistency proofs public, watch for rewrites
chainnone no tokens, no consensus network

Append-only, by construction

New leaves are added, old ones are never changed. Altering a past entry changes the Merkle root, which breaks the signed checkpoint and is visible to anyone watching.

An inclusion proof per report

Each report ships a proof that it sits in the tree at a fixed position. Your buyer checks the proof offline and confirms the report is the exact one we logged.

A Merkle log, plainly

Ed25519 and SHA-256 over an append-only tree. No tokens, no consensus network, no chain in the trust path. The same primitives the verifier already checks.
