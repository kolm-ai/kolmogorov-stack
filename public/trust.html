03 / Data handling

We see less than you think, and keep it for less time.

The audit reads redacted activity logs, not your production secrets. You redact before upload, the credentials we hold are scoped and revocable, and nothing is retained beyond the engagement.

Redaction

Secrets stay out at the source

You redact before anything reaches us. The scan reads the structure of agent activity: the scopes, the destinations, the trail, not the payloads underneath.

04 / Transparency log

Every report is logged where anyone can watch.

Each issuance is appended to a public, tamper-evident Merkle log in the RFC 6962 style, built on Ed25519 and SHA-256. A reviewer confirms the report you handed them is the one we signed, and that history was never rewritten. It is not a blockchain.

schemerfc6962-style merkle log
leafsha-256(canonical_report_bytes)
signed25519(issuer_sk, leaf)
treeappend-only leaves never edited
rootsha-256 merkle root signed checkpoint
proofinclusion proof per report
monitorconsistency proofs public, watch for rewrites
chainnone no tokens, no consensus network

Append-only, by construction

New leaves are added, old ones are never changed. Altering a past entry changes the Merkle root, breaks the signed checkpoint, and is visible to anyone watching.

An inclusion proof per report

Each report ships a proof that it sits in the tree at a fixed position. Your buyer checks it offline and confirms the report is the exact one we logged.

A Merkle log, plainly

Ed25519 and SHA-256 over an append-only tree. No tokens, no consensus network, no chain in the trust path.
