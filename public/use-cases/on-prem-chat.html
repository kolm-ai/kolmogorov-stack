On-prem internal chat assistant

How does kolm handle on-prem internal chat assistant?

kolm distills an internal-chat specialist from your captured Q&A and quantizes it to run fully on-prem — so a regulated team gets a tuned assistant inside its own boundary, with PII redacted on capture and a signed receipt for every build. The result is a model you own and run on your own hardware, trained on your real workload, with a signed build receipt instead of a recurring per-call API bill.

The problem

What makes on-prem internal chat assistant expensive today?

Regulated teams cannot send internal questions to a public LLM API, yet hosting a generic open model gives weak, untuned answers and still carries operational cost. That cost compounds with volume and ties a sensitive workload to an external API, which is exactly the situation an owned, compiled specialist is designed to replace once the traffic pattern is well understood.

The kolm flow

How does the kolm pipeline solve it?

Run the kolm gateway inside your network. Capture internal (question, answer) pairs with redaction, distill a specialist, gate it on the K-score, and compile a quantized artifact that serves with no outbound network. Each stage runs inside your own environment, so capture, distillation, and compilation happen without handing the workload to a third party, and the final artifact is a portable, signed model.

The outcome

What do you end up with?

The outcome is an internal assistant that runs air-gapped on your own hardware, tuned to your knowledge, with a verifiable receipt and no per-token bill. Because the artifact is portable and signed, you can move it between a laptop, a VPC, or an air-gapped network, and prove at any point exactly which weights and method produced it.

FAQ

Can this run with no internet?
Yes. Once compiled, the artifact serves locally with no network dependency at inference time.
Is it tuned to our content?
Distillation trains on your captured pairs, so the specialist reflects your internal knowledge and tone rather than a generic base model.
How do we audit a build?
Every compile emits an Ed25519-signed receipt with weight hashes and the K-score, replayable at /verify-prod.
