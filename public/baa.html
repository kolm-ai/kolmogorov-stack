Who needs the BAA

If protected health information (PHI) touches the kolm CLI, the hosted control plane, or the optional hosted teacher path, you need a BAA in place before production traffic flows. A few common paths so a compliance reviewer can locate themselves.

  • Provider, payor, or clearinghouse

    covered entity · BAA required

    Hospital systems, health plans, dental and behavioral practices, claims clearinghouses. Any time you put PHI into the compiler (captures, prompts, training pairs), the BAA must be executed first.

  • Downstream business associate

    business associate · BAA required

    A SaaS vendor, ML platform, or contract research organization that receives PHI from a covered entity and routes any of it through kolm. The customer signs a BAA with kolm; kolm becomes a subcontractor business associate.

  • Clinical research with PHI

    research · BAA available

    IRB-approved studies that compile models against de-identified or limited-data-set inputs. The BAA covers the kolm side; the IRB and the data-use agreement cover the upstream side.

  • Healthcare workloads without PHI

    no PHI · BAA optional

    If your captures are de-identified to the HIPAA Safe Harbor standard, or you are compiling on synthetic data only, the BAA is optional. We will still sign one on request so a procurement reviewer has the paperwork on file.

Scope of coverage

The BAA covers the components where PHI may transit or be processed on the customer's behalf. Most of the stack runs on customer hardware; the BAA is precise about which surface area on the kolm side is in scope.

What the BAA covers

Compiler in customer VPC
The CLI, distill workers, and quantization runtimes installed by the customer. PHI processed here never leaves customer infrastructure in the default deployment.
Captures with PHI
Tenant-scoped capture rows stored at ~/.kolm/events or wherever KOLM_DATA_DIR points. We do not touch these in the default deployment; if the customer opts into the hosted control plane for retention reporting only, the metadata is in scope.
Signed receipts and artifacts
Receipts may reference PHI-derived inputs by content hash; the BAA covers the integrity, retention, and disclosure of those references on the kolm side of the boundary.
Hosted teacher path (opt-in)
If the customer routes PHI-bearing prompts through the optional hosted teacher path, both kolm and the teacher subprocessor (Anthropic or OpenAI) handle the traffic under their own BAAs.
Hosted registry metadata
If the customer publishes a receipt CID to the public registry, the BAA scope covers the registry's handling of that metadata. The receipt body itself does not include raw PHI; only content-addressed hashes.

What the BAA does not cover

Self-hosted teacher endpoints
If the customer brings their own teacher (self-hosted vLLM, SGLang, TGI, or a private endpoint), traffic does not flow through kolm and is governed by the customer's own controls.
Customer's storage backend
S3, GCS, on-prem object stores. The customer's existing BAAs with those providers govern those tiers; kolm does not interpose.
Workstation OS and disk encryption
FileVault, BitLocker, LUKS, and OS-level access controls are the customer's responsibility, even when the CLI is installed locally.
Identity provider
SSO and MFA are operated by the customer's IdP. kolm consumes signed assertions; the IdP's own BAA covers that tier.

What kolm does and does not do under the BAA

A direct list. The plain version of "shared responsibility" rather than the vague version.

What kolm does

  • Sign artifacts and receipts. Every .kolm artifact ships with an Ed25519 signature over a content-addressed receipt naming the teacher, base model, eval pack, and K-score verdict. Verifiable offline by an auditor.
  • Score with K-score and gate. The compiler runs the eval pack and records the verdict in the signed receipt. A real verdict, not a marketing claim.
  • Honor retention controls. Per-namespace retention you set with kolm namespace set-retention. Subject-deletion via kolm capture delete --subject with a tombstone receipt for the auditor.
  • Notify on suspected breach. Within 30 days of discovery on the kolm side of the boundary, per the BAA. Forensic timeline shared with the affected covered entity.
  • Return or destroy data at termination. On termination, customer data on the hosted side is returned in a verifiable export or destroyed; kolm signs a destruction certificate.
  • Operate a trust mailbox. trust@kolm.ai for BAA requests, breach reports, and audit support questions. Acknowledged within one business day.

What kolm does not do

  • Store PHI in our infrastructure. The default deployment keeps captures and weights on customer hardware. We do not replicate them upstream.
  • Route PHI through our network. Unless the customer opts into the hosted teacher path, no PHI-bearing prompt transits kolm.ai.
  • Sell or share PHI for marketing. The BAA prohibits secondary use; we do not have an internal use case for customer PHI to begin with.
  • Use PHI to train shared models. Distilled student weights derived from customer captures live in the customer's namespace and do not feed any kolm-side model.
  • Act as a covered entity. Kolm is not a provider, payor, or clearinghouse. We are a business associate when handling PHI on customer behalf; this page does not promise something we are not.
  • Sub-contract without notice. Subprocessors are listed publicly at /soc2. Any new subprocessor that would touch PHI requires prior notice.

How to execute the BAA

A real process. We do not gate the BAA behind a sales call; a compliance engineer can drive it from request to counter-signature without ever touching a calendar.

  1. Email trust@kolm.ai with the legal entity name, the covered-entity or business-associate role, and whether you want the kolm template or want us to redline yours. Use the contact form if you prefer.
  2. Template delivery · we send the kolm BAA template the same business day. The template references the HIPAA Security Rule mapping at /hipaa-mapping and the SOC 2 report as Schedule 1.
  3. Redlines accepted · within standard markup. We will accept your standard BAA template; healthcare procurement teams routinely send AHA, NIST, or institution-specific variants. Material redlines come back within five business days.
  4. Counter-signature · counter-signed PDF returned within two business days of agreed text. We do not require a sales call to sign.
  5. Subprocessor list refresh · once the BAA is in place, you are added to the notice list at /soc2 so any future subprocessor change reaches you with the prior notice the BAA requires.
  6. Audit support · for OCR audits, IRB reviews, or downstream covered-entity audits, a security engineer responds to evidence requests within five business days. The receipt chain itself is verifiable offline; most audit questions resolve there.

Scope

What we will say to your auditor that we also say on this page. No marketing rewrite at execution time.

postureBusiness associate

We sign the BAA; we are not a covered entity

Kolm is not a provider, payor, or clearinghouse. When the customer puts PHI through the compiler, we become a business associate to the customer; we are not regulated as a covered entity ourselves and this page does not claim we are.

postureDefault keeps PHI inside

The default deployment keeps PHI on customer hardware

Captures, weights, and eval-pack runs live on the customer side. The hosted control plane handles signup, registry, and the verify endpoint; the BAA scope on the kolm side is narrow because the data path is narrow.

postureVerifiable, not promised

The receipt chain replaces "trust us"

Every artifact is signed. An auditor can verify the chain offline against the eval pack, the K-score axes, and the teacher / base-model identifiers in the receipt. The BAA references this; the receipt does the work.

postureWhere we will say no

What we will not put into the BAA

We will not promise zero subprocessors (we list them publicly), will not waive prior notice on subprocessor changes, will not accept unbounded indemnification, and will not represent that we are a covered entity. Real boundary; real agreement.
