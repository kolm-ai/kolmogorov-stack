Capture, compile, sign, deploy

Four steps from your API traffic to a model on your own hardware.

No rewrite to get here, no lock-in once you have it. Point your live calls at Kolm, let it compile the behavior you already use into a signed model, keep only what passes your evals, and run it on the hardware that fits. Each step leaves you something you can open, check, and keep.

01 / Capture

Point your calls at Kolm.

Keep your SDK exactly as it is — drop Kolm in front of your OpenAI- or Anthropic-compatible calls. REST, streaming, webhooks, OTEL, MCP, A2A, GraphQL/RPC, queues, warehouse drops and logs all flow in, with secrets stripped before anything is written to disk.

02 / Compile

Get a model in one file.

Examples, recipes, evals, tokenizer metadata, hashes and a verifier receipt compile into one portable .kolm — signed with Ed25519, reproducible, and inspectable layer by layer. One file you own and can move anywhere.

03 / Compose

Ship only what passes.

Your evals decide what makes the cut: human labels, a failure taxonomy, protected slices, drift checks and regression replay. The model is built from the behavior that actually passed — nothing else.

04 / Deploy

Run it where it fits.

Laptop, your private cloud, the edge, or a server — pick the smallest runtime that fits, with the real limits shown for each. Every target ships with a runtime recipe and the receipts to prove what you deployed.
