What's a use case page actually for?

Why a vertical page if the compilation flow is the same?

Because the regulator, the data residency rule, and the seed corpus are different. Healthcare has 21 PHI classes and HIPAA. Finance has SR 11-7 and MNPI. Defense has IL5 / IL6 and ITAR. Education has FERPA. Each vertical page mirrors the language the buyer's auditor uses so legal and procurement do not have to translate.

Are these compiled artifacts available, or just architectures?

Healthcare ships a working claims-redactor.kolm at 98.5 percent (K=0.985) on a 60-seed gold set with 21 PHI classes. Legal ships legal-clause-extract.kolm at K=0.84. Finance ships finance-disclosure-redact.kolm for MNPI scrubbing. Defense ships the self-hosted compile-server kit for IL5 / IL6 enclaves. The four newer verticals (Government, Education, Fintech, SaaS) ship recipes and seed schemas; you compile against your own gold set.

Can I bring my own vertical?

Yes. Run kolm init <task>, point it at 60 to 200 captured prompt-and-output pairs from the capture inbox, and compile. The receipt chain (spec, seeds, split, train, evals, signatures) is identical regardless of vertical. If you want a custom landing page authored for an industry that's not listed here, mail founders at kolm.ai.
