    Introducing kolm v1: Wrapper, Studio, Run and Govern

    V1 ships three surfaces that share one disk format. The Wrapper routes calls to OpenAI, Anthropic, and Together while signing a receipt for each turn. Studio captures those turns, distills a smaller local model from them, and compiles it for the target hardware. Run and Govern deploys the artifact, watches behavior, and exports the evidence DAG when an auditor asks. The three surfaces look like separate products but they touch the same JSONL receipt schema, the same Ed25519 keypair, and the same artifact format on disk.

    The wrapper tax, decomposed: where 703 milliseconds went

    We measured the kolm gateway against direct OpenAI on 10 paired prompts. The wrapper adds 703 ms of mean latency, 40 percent end-to-end. This post breaks that number down phase by phase: pipeline overhead splits into routing, fingerprinting, receipt signing, and write-ahead log; transport overhead splits into the Vercel hop, the Railway leg, and the SSL handshake. The Vercel proxy alone accounts for 400 to 500 ms. Railway-direct is faster but costs you receipt-on-edge and the public domain.

    Trinity-500: distilling three frontier teachers into one 7B student

    We trained a Qwen2.5-7B student on 410 council pairs sampled from Claude 3.5 Sonnet, GPT-4o, and DeepSeek-R1-32B. LoRA r=16 alpha=32 dropout=0.05, 79.18 seconds on an RTX 5090. Benchmarked at n=57 across four behaviors: asks-one-question 96.5 percent, judge-clarify 100 percent, judge-on-policy 100 percent, 1.24 second mean latency at 210 character mean response. Beats Claude Haiku 4.5 and base Qwen 7B; ties GPT-4o-mini at roughly half the character count. This post walks the recipe: which teacher generates which slice, how we filter for diversity, and the traps we hit on bf16 plus gradient checkpointing.

    Receipts as compliance evidence: kolm-audit-1, Ed25519, and the four frameworks

    Every kolm call writes a JSONL receipt with 19 canonical fields signed by an Ed25519 keypair scoped to the tenant. The schema is named kolm-audit-1. This post walks the field list, shows a verified example, and maps each field to a control in SOC 2 (CC6.1, CC7.2, PI1.1, C1.1), HIPAA 164.312(b) and (c)(1), and EU AI Act Article 12, 13, and 26. The point is not that one schema satisfies four regimes; it is that one signed log line gives auditors the seven things they always ask for.
