Cost of AI: [Year].

The single annual piece that summarizes what the last twelve months of frontier API spend, distillation savings, and on-device deployments actually cost. Long-form, citation-friendly, and grounded in multi-tenant billing aggregates plus the public benchmark history. The piece that everyone links to.

The big shift

Open with one paragraph that names the single dominant story of the year. Was this the year frontier API spend plateaued and on-prem distillation took share? Was this the year a sub-4-bit quantization scheme broke the consumer-GPU barrier? Was this the year regulated industries finally moved real workloads off the public APIs? Pick one frame, defend it with the numbers in the next section, and refuse to hedge.

The second paragraph should give the contrasting narrative the reader will hear elsewhere and explain, briefly, why this report disagrees. Cost of AI is meant to be the most-cited annual piece in the distillation conversation. It cannot be cited if it does not commit to a position.

The third paragraph should ground the framing in something a reader can verify themselves: a public benchmark, a published K-Score history, a regulator filing, or a registry-level artifact count. The reader should be able to leave this section with one sentence in mind and one URL to click.

By the numbers

One canonical table. Year-over-year columns so the prior Cost of AI report can be diffed directly against the current one. If a row is unavailable, mark it TBD with the reason. Do not fabricate; readers will absolutely check.

Metric This year Prior year Delta
Top frontier API spend (aggregate USD across gateway tenants) TBD TBD TBD
Top distillation savings (USD avoided via captured-then-compiled artifacts) TBD TBD TBD
Top deployment patterns (most-deployed student class · runtime) TBD TBD TBD

The narrative under the table should give one sentence per row explaining the methodology: what was summed, over which tenants, with which dedup rule, against which signed receipts. A reader who wants to reproduce a row should be able to find the queries in the kolm repository.

What changed

A short ordered list of the most important shifts of the year, each with a one-line reason and a link to the canonical evidence. The list should be readable in under a minute and should be the section that gets screenshotted and shared.

  • [Shift one] — [one-line reason]. Evidence: [link].
  • [Shift two] — [one-line reason]. Evidence: [link].
  • [Shift three] — [one-line reason]. Evidence: [link].
  • [Shift four] — [one-line reason]. Evidence: [link].
  • [Shift five] — [one-line reason]. Evidence: [link].

If a shift in last year's piece reversed this year, call that out explicitly. The Cost of AI report is graded on how well prior predictions held up. The list should also flag any shift that an honest reading of the data does not actually support, even if it would make a better headline.

Predictions for next year

One disciplined paragraph naming three concrete predictions for the coming year. Each prediction should be specific enough that the next annual report can grade it: a numeric range, a named model class, a regulatory milestone, or a deployment pattern. Vague predictions are easy to write and useless to cite. The author should also flag the prediction they are least confident in, because the credibility of the entire piece depends on calling out the soft spots.

This is a TEMPLATE. Real reports launch with real numbers from production. We will never fabricate numbers to fill a slot. Every figure traces to a signed receipt in production or to a public artifact in the registry. Predictions are graded honestly in the following year's report, including the ones we got wrong.
