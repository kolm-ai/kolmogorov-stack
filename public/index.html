02 / How it works

Audit. Sign. Verify.

One lifecycle. The report is reproducible, scoped to what was tested, and checkable by anyone you hand it to.

01 · Audit We examine the agent

Onramp through a log import or a sidecar proxy. Permissions, audit trail, data egress and injection resistance, each mapped to the standards your buyer cites.

02 · Sign We seal the findings

The report is canonicalized and signed with Ed25519. The signature covers the exact bytes, so a downgraded finding or an inflated score breaks it. Each issuance enters an append-only log.

03 · Verify Your buyer checks it

They verify the signature offline, in their own browser, against your public key. No account, no upload, no kolm server in the trust path.

Trust moves from our word to the math. The same check runs in the verifier at the top of this page.

04 / Verification

They don't trust us. They check the math.

Security theater can't be falsified. This can. Two checks run in the buyer's own browser, against the key inside the report, with no kolm server in the path.

Tier 1 · signature

Edit one field, the seal breaks

The Ed25519 signature covers the canonical bytes. Change a finding or inflate the score and the match fails, in front of the reviewer.

Tier 2 · issuer

A rogue key signs, but doesn't match

The signing key is pinned to the keyring your buyer expects. Forge a fresh key and tier 1 clears, but tier 2 exposes it.
