kolm.ai / compliance / eu-ai-act

Open-source AI workbench

EU AI Act compliance, generated from your .kolm artifacts. Frontier AI on your own infrastructure.

Compile a model into a .kolm artifact and the toolkit auto-generates an Annex IV technical-documentation envelope, classifies the system's risk category, configures human-in-the-loop thresholds, and pulls tenant-fenced data governance reports.

Not Kolm therapeutics. This page describes kolm.ai, the open-source AI workbench.

Why this exists

Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (the EU AI Act) entered into force on 1 August 2024. The high-risk obligations kick in over a 24-to-36-month phase-in. Every system on the EU market in a high-risk category needs Annex IV technical documentation, a conformity assessment, declared post-market monitoring, and demonstrated human oversight per Article 14. Drafting Annex IV from scratch is a six-figure compliance project. Most of the fields, though, are already living in your .kolm manifest — the architecture, the training-data summary, the performance metrics, the cybersecurity attestations. We just need to extract them in the right shape.

W766 is the extractor. It reads a manifest, derives the Annex IV envelope, and stamps every absent field as not_yet_disclosed so the gaps are obvious to a regulator. The fields are never fabricated. A field labeled not_yet_disclosed is a TODO for the builder, not a paper-over.

Risk classification

W766-2 · risk scoring

Four risk categories. Manifest task_category maps to category per Annex III.

The catalog covers more than twenty task categories drawn from Annex III (high-risk list) and Article 5 (prohibited / unacceptable practices). Manifest field task_category wins; vertical is the fallback; intended_use goes through a regex classifier with confidence capped at 0.95. The floor when nothing matches is minimal — never null, never fabricated.

minimal

minimal risk

Spam filters, code completion, search ranking, recommendation. No specific obligations under the AI Act.

limited

limited risk

Chatbots, generative text and image, deepfakes, voice synthesis. Article 50 transparency obligations apply — disclose AI interaction, mark synthetic content.

high

high risk

Medical diagnosis, credit scoring, biometric ID, employment screening, critical infrastructure, law enforcement, admin of justice. Full Annex IV documentation, conformity assessment, human oversight per Article 14, registration in the EU database.

unacceptable

unacceptable risk

Social scoring of natural persons by public authorities, subliminal manipulation, real-time remote biometric ID in public spaces, emotion recognition in workplace. Article 5 prohibits these — no conformity path legalizes them.

{
  "manifest": {

  }
}

Technical documentation export

W766-1 · annex iv emitter

Nine Annex IV fields. JSON or markdown. Never fabricated.

The toolkit sources each Annex IV field from the manifest where present, and stamps not_yet_disclosed where it is not. A regulator reading the export can distinguish "the builder hasn't attested to X" from "X is empty by design". Both Node (src/ai-act-export.js) and Python (apps/export/ai_act_docs.py) implementations emit the same shape so a CI runner without Node can still produce docs.

intended_purpose system_architecture training_data_summary performance_metrics risk_management human_oversight_measures accuracy_metrics cybersecurity_measures postmarket_monitoring_plan

Human-in-the-loop configuration

W766-3 · human review threshold

Per-namespace confidence threshold. Below the threshold, route to a human reviewer.

Article 14 requires that high-risk systems "can be effectively overseen by natural persons". This subsystem is the runtime knob — for any (tenant, namespace), set a confidence threshold in nats. When the W709 runtime confidence router records a decision below the threshold, the row is tagged human_in_loop_triggered and surfaced for review. Threshold validated ∈ [0, 10] nats — out-of-range rejects loudly with an honest envelope.

POST /v1/compliance/ai-act/human-in-loop
{
  "namespace": "billing-agent",
  "threshold_nats": 1.5,
  "confirm": true
}
→
{
  "ok": true,
  "namespace": "billing-agent",
  "threshold_nats": 1.5,
  "persisted_event_id": "evt_...",
  "version": "w766-v1"
}

Data governance reports

W766-4 · tenant-fenced aggregation

Per-tenant capture counts, high-risk volume, human-in-loop trigger rate, confidence distribution.

The report pulls captures from the event store under a tenant-fenced query, then re-checks row.tenant_id on every row inside the aggregation loop (W411 defense-in-depth). A schema bug in future revisions cannot leak across tenants. Aggregates: count_total, count_high_risk, count_human_in_loop_triggered, average_confidence_at_decision, oldest_capture_at, newest_capture_at, plus a per-namespace breakdown.

GET /v1/compliance/ai-act/governance-report?namespace=billing-agent&from=2026-01-01&to=2026-06-01
→
{
  "ok": true,
  "report": {
    "tenant_id": "tenant_...",
    "namespace": "billing-agent",
    "count_total": 1287,
    "count_high_risk": 42,
    "count_human_in_loop_triggered": 8,
    "average_confidence_at_decision": 0.86,
    "oldest_capture_at": "2026-01-04T...",
    "newest_capture_at": "2026-05-23T...",
    "by_namespace": { "billing-agent": 1287 }
  },
  "version": "w766-v1"
}

Honesty contract

The toolkit never fabricates Annex IV fields. Every field the manifest does not carry is stamped not_yet_disclosed. A regulator reading the output sees the gaps. The builder sees the TODO. There is no silent fallback that fills in a plausible-looking placeholder — that would be the opposite of compliance evidence.

The risk classifier never returns null. The floor is minimal when nothing matches. The reasoning string explicitly says no_task_category_matched so an auditor can see why the floor was applied.

The human-in-the-loop threshold validates ∈ [0, 10] nats. An out-of-range value is rejected with an honest invalid_threshold envelope — never persisted.

This toolkit is not legal advice. The EU AI Act has phase-in deadlines through 2027, several Delegated Acts still in draft, and Member-State-level transposition that adds local detail. Treat the auto-generated Annex IV as a starting draft your legal team reviews, not a final filing. The toolkit version stamp w766-v1 appears in every envelope so reviewers can trace the generator version that produced a given doc.
