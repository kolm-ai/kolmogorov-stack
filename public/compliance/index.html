kolm.ai  /  compliance

The receipt is the evidence.

Every framework that governs AI in production asks for the same three things: what model ran, what it did, did it stay inside its lane. A .kolm answers all three by construction: artifact CID, input/output hashes, K-score and gate, Ed25519 receipt chain, replay months later. This page maps that to the frameworks themselves.

A.HIPAA Privacy & Security Rules — mapped to .kolm

45 CFR Part 164. For the Privacy Rule we cite Safe Harbor (§164.514(b)); for the Security Rule we cite the administrative, physical, and technical safeguards (§164.308–312). A signed BAA is required before any PHI flows through the gateway. Self-host puts the gateway inside your boundary; the artifact runs offline.

Rule / safeguard What it asks for kolm evidence
§164.308(a)(1)Security management process — risk analysis + risk management.kolm assurance export --format json; quarterly K-score drift report; receipt chain replay.
§164.308(a)(3)Workforce security — authorize, supervise, terminate access.SCIM provisioning; SSO via SAML 2.0; kolm audit-log entries per API key.
§164.308(a)(5)Security awareness — periodic training, log-in monitoring.Anomalous-key alerts; Sentry-piped failed-auth events; documented response runbook.
§164.308(a)(6)Security incident procedures.Status page incident timeline; receipt audit trail to scope blast radius (which CIDs, which artifacts, which tenants).
§164.308(a)(7)Contingency plan — backup, DR, emergency-mode operation.Air-gap bundle (kolm deploy bundle --airgap) is the offline DR mode; artifact runs without any kolm.ai call.
§164.310(d)Device and media controls — disposal, reuse, accountability.Device registry tracks deployed artifacts by CID; kolm devices remove emits a revocation receipt linked into the evidence DAG.
§164.312(a)Access control — unique user ID, automatic logoff, encryption at rest.Per-tenant hashed API keys; namespace ACLs; AES-256 at rest; TLS 1.3 in transit.
§164.312(b)Audit controls — record + examine activity in systems with PHI.Every inference produces a signed receipt: input_sha, output_sha, artifact_cid, chain_prev_hash, chain_hash, Ed25519 signature.
§164.312(c)Integrity — protect ePHI from improper alteration.Receipt hash chain: any tampering with a prior receipt breaks the chain. Verifier rejects breaks.
§164.312(e)Transmission security.TLS 1.3 for gateway; air-gap bundle never transmits (self-host eliminates the control entirely).
§164.514(b)Safe Harbor de-identification — remove 18 identifiers.kolm capture redact --policy hipaa-safe-harbor; 18-identifier verifier in the compliance pack.

Out of scope: §164.308(a)(2) (security official designation), §164.308(b) (BAAs with downstream) — both are governance you implement. We sign a BAA upstream; we don't manage your downstream BAAs.

B.SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria — mapped to .kolm

AICPA TSC 2017 (revised 2022). Common Criteria (CC) apply to every report; the relevant Availability + Confidentiality + Processing Integrity criteria add on top. Kolm holds SOC 2 Type I; Type II is in progress. This table maps the criteria your auditor reviews on top of ours.

Criterion What it asks for kolm evidence
CC1.1–CC1.5Control environment — ethics, board oversight, structure, accountability.Org-level; /security + /manifesto. We provide the documented engineering baseline; your CEO + board sign the rest.
CC2.1–CC2.3Information & communication — quality of information used in controls.Receipts and audit logs are the information stream; status page + changelog + security HoF cover external communication.
CC3.1–CC3.4Risk assessment — identify, analyze, assess risks; consider fraud + change.Threat model at /threat-model; model risk via assurance case + K-score gate; drift detector flags distribution shift.
CC4.1–CC4.2Monitoring — evaluations + communication of deficiencies.Sentry + status page + per-tenant usage alerts; security HoF for external reports.
CC5.1–CC5.3Control activities — selection + development of controls + technology.CI gate (kolm bench in PR) blocks deploys when K-score drops; ship-gate's 52 checks; release-verify's 7 gates.
CC6.1–CC6.8Logical & physical access — provisioning, deprovisioning, authentication.SSO/SAML 2.0; SCIM; hashed API keys; Ed25519 client-keypair registration at /v1/keys/public; per-tenant namespace isolation.
CC7.1–CC7.5System operations — detection + response + recovery from events.Receipt chain replay scopes the incident; rollback via artifact lifecycle (deployed → superseded → revoked); /status incident log.
CC8.1Change management — authorize + design + test + deploy.kolm compile requires K-score gate pass; artifact lifecycle records every state change with a receipt; evidence DAG ties artifact ↔ captures.
CC9.1–CC9.2Risk mitigation — vendor + business partner risk.Sub-processor list at /subprocessors; provider routing receipts identify which upstream served the call.
A1.1–A1.3Availability — capacity, monitoring, environmental protection, recovery.Status page uptime; multi-region BYOC for Enterprise; air-gap bundle for the local-only continuity case.
C1.1–C1.2Confidentiality — protect confidential information through its lifecycle.Tenant-fenced captures; namespace ACLs; capture redaction policies; export-then-delete supported via kolm captures export.
PI1.1–PI1.5Processing integrity — complete, valid, accurate, timely, authorized.The receipt is processing integrity: input_sha + output_sha + artifact_cid + chain_prev_hash + Ed25519 signature, verifiable months later with kolm verify.

Privacy criteria (P1–P8): mapped under /privacy + the data residency page at /compliance/data-residency.

C.SR 11-7 / OCC 2011-12 Model Risk Management — mapped to .kolm

Federal Reserve SR 11-7 + OCC Bulletin 2011-12. Three lines of defense: development & implementation (line 1), validation (line 2), audit (line 3). Receipts are the artifact every line ends up needing.

SR 11-7 element What it asks for kolm evidence
V.AConceptual soundness — design + theory + data quality.Passport: teacher council, distillation method, training data lineage. kolm passport export.
V.BOngoing monitoring — performance over time, benchmarking, sensitivity.K-score per-month; drift detector vs training distribution; kolm drift + kolm savings.
V.COutcomes analysis — back-testing, comparison to outcomes.Receipts are the back-test corpus: every input/output is hashed + signed + replayable.
VIGovernance, policies, controls — roles, model inventory, validation independence.Model inventory = artifact registry indexed by CID; validation-team API keys scoped to read-only on receipts + namespaces.
VIIExternal resources — vendor model usage requires same standards.Kolm's own passport covers vendor-side soundness; your model risk team adds the line-2 review.

D.ISO/IEC 42001:2023 AI Management System — mapped to .kolm

The 2023 standard for AI management systems. Clauses 4–10 mirror the structure of ISO 9001/27001: context → leadership → planning → support → operation → performance → improvement. Tied to the AI-specific Annex A controls.

Clause / Annex A What it asks for kolm evidence
Cl. 6Planning — AI risk + impact assessments.Assurance case template + kolm passport identify each model's intended use, capability bounds, and known failure modes.
Cl. 8Operation — operational planning, controls, and documented information.CI gate + ship-gate + receipts. Operations produce a continuous log of artifact behavior.
Cl. 9Performance evaluation — monitoring, internal audit, management review.K-score over time; drift & savings reports; kolm assurance export --format pdf for the management review pack.
A.6.2.6AI system verification + validation.Kolm benchmark harness with held-out eval + K-score; per-axis scores via kolm bench --axes.
A.6.2.8AI system recording of event logs.Receipts. The receipt chain is the event log; kolm receipts export --format csv for the audit trail.
A.7.3Acquisition of data — provenance, quality, fitness for purpose.Capture lake with poison detector + redactor; provenance fields on every capture; namespace-fenced.
A.8.2Information for interested parties (users, affected persons).Public model card per artifact (auto-generated by kolm export --format hf-model-card); user-facing transparency notice templates in /legal.

Out of scope: Cl. 5 (Leadership) is organizational; Cl. 7.1–7.5 (resources, competence, awareness, communication) is HR + change management. We provide the AI-specific artifacts that make Cl. 6–9 + Annex A auditable.

How the mapping works

Each mapping table answers the same shape for every control:

  • id, the control or subcategory identifier (GV-1.1, MS-2.3, §164.514(b)).
  • control text, what the framework asks for, abbreviated.
  • evidence in .kolm, the exact manifest field, receipt field, or CLI verb that satisfies it.

If a control is not covered by the artifact spine, we say so explicitly. Examples: human-in-the-loop oversight (MG-3.x) is governance you implement, not something a manifest field can satisfy. We do not claim otherwise.

What we are not

We do not certify your compliance. We do not write your policies. We do not stand between you and your auditor.

What we do: ship the artifacts that turn "did the model behave" from an asserted answer into a replayable one. Your compliance team and your auditor decide whether the evidence satisfies the framework. We give them something to point at that is not vibes.
