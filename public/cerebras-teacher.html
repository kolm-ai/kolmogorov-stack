Cerebras · Fast teacher

Distill from Llama-3.3-70B at Cerebras speeds. Train on your GPU. Run anywhere.

Cerebras Cloud serves Llama-3.3-70B and Qwen-3-32B at thousands of tokens per second. kolm uses Cerebras as a teacher for council distillation — your distill collection that used to take an hour now takes minutes. Wire one env var (CEREBRAS_API_KEY), name cerebras:llama-3.3-70b in your spec, and the rest of the recipe is unchanged.

What this unlocks

Why a Cerebras teacher matters.

Three concrete wins when you swap a slow hosted teacher for a Cerebras one inside an otherwise unchanged council distillation recipe.

Faster collection

2000-pair council distill in ~10 min instead of ~60 min.

When one of the teachers is Cerebras Llama-3.3-70B, the wall-clock for the collect step drops by an order of magnitude. Cerebras inference latency is small enough that the council step stops being the slow path of the pipeline.

3-teacher council

Claude + GPT-4o + Cerebras Llama for lineage diversity.

Council distillation works better when the teachers come from different families. Adding Cerebras Llama gives you signal diversity from three different model lineages — Anthropic, OpenAI, and Meta — without changing your existing council weights.

Cost-aware

Cerebras $/1k often beats Anthropic and OpenAI at scale.

For high-volume capture corpora, swapping the heaviest teacher slot to Cerebras can pull the council cost below the single-teacher Anthropic baseline. The bench section below shows actual numbers from your own key.

Setup

Three lines, no SDK to install.

Export the key, name the teacher in your spec, run the collect. The Cerebras adapter ships inside the kolm CLI — nothing extra to wire.

terminal · bash Copy & run 
# 1. Export your Cerebras Cloud key.
$ export CEREBRAS_API_KEY=...

# 2. Name Cerebras as the teacher in the distill spec.
$ kolm distill spec --teacher cerebras:llama-3.3-70b

# 3. Collect 800 council pairs (~minutes, not the usual ~hour).
$ kolm distill collect --spec spec.json --rows 800
Benchmark

Reproducible against your own key.

We do not ship synthetic numbers on this page. The benchmark runs against the real Cerebras Cloud, lands in a versioned fixture, and ships with every release.

Reproducible: node scripts/cerebras-bench.mjs against your own key. Results land in data/eval-fixtures/cerebras-bench.json. We update the published numbers per release.

The bench measures four things that matter for distillation: tokens per second, first-token latency, $/1k tokens, and end-to-end wall-clock for an 800-pair collect step. Every cell is signed and traces back to a CI run.

Once the bench has been run with a real key, the fixture is written to data/eval-fixtures/cerebras-bench.json in your local checkout. The reproduce-it-yourself path is in the Cerebras council distill recipe — post a PR if your numbers diverge.
