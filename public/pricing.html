Metered compile credits

A compile is one full forge cycle.

Gateway calls and compile credits are separate meters. One credit covers a distill, quantize, sign, and benchmark pass on a single namespace. Re-running a verified recipe with the same spec is free - you already paid for the work, the receipt proves it, the registry serves the result.

  • Free · 5 / mo · learn the loop
  • Pro · 50 / mo · weekly iteration + ablations
  • Team · 250 / mo · parallel queue
  • Enterprise · custom · reserved GPU + multi-region runners
The economics

Why this looks different from every other LLM bill.

Most "AI platform" pricing scales with how often you call your model. Ours scales with how often you decide which model to use. After compile, the model is yours and inference is your hardware bill, not ours.

  • What you pay for. Gateway routing, capture storage, compile compute, registry hosting, and the verify path. The control plane.
  • What you don't. Per-token inference on your .kolm. Self-host runtime, source code, six SDKs, the CLI, and the docs.
  • Why the gateway costs anything. Cross-provider router, capture ledger, policy enforcer, doctor, registry. Real money to keep them up.
  • Why inference is free. It runs on your hardware. We never touch the request. The .kolm is signed at build time and verified at load. We can't bill what we can't see.

Try the stack. Ship a side project.

Pro $49 / mo · most teams start here

Ship a real product. Compile to spec.

Pro turns on the compile path. Once you have 100+ captures in a namespace, kolm compile "your job description" distills them into a student model, runs a bake-off against the teacher, scores it on a frozen eval, and ships a signed .kolm artifact. From that point on, you can run the artifact anywhere: cloud, laptop, browser, phone, VPC: and pay zero per-call.

500k calls is a lot of product surface. If each user interaction is 1 prompt, that's 16,000 daily-active users running a chat-style feature, or ~500 daily extracts on a 1000-doc/day pipeline. Most Pro accounts use 80–120k calls/month and never come close to the cap.

  • 500k calls / month: ~16k DAU on a chat feature, no overage at typical use
  • 5 namespaces: prod / staging / dev / two side projects
  • Compile + distill + quantize: turn captures into a signed .kolm
  • K-score + frozen eval: "the model is still good" is a number, pinned over time
  • Private registry: your artifacts stay in your tenant
  • Drift detection: gateway noticed a 2.3pt K drop since last build, alerts you
  • Verify-anywhere: anyone with your public key can confirm the .kolm is exactly what shipped
  • Email support, 24h response: an actual engineer, not a chatbot

Upgrade path

When to move up.

Every upgrade is one click in the dashboard or one CLI command. No re-onboarding, no migration, no data move - same API keys, same registry, same artifacts. The signal to move is usually a person, not a meter.

Free to Pro happens when the side project starts collecting users and you want kolm compile, a private registry, drift detection, or email support from an actual engineer. Pro to Team happens when a second person needs login - audit log, approve/reject flows, per-namespace cost breakdown for finance, or the air-gap bundle. Team to Enterprise happens when procurement enters the chat: BYOC, SAML/SCIM, BAA, EU/APAC residency, custom runtime targets, a named CSM with an SLA.

FAQ

Common questions about billing.

What counts as a "gateway call"?
Every request that hits api.kolm.ai/v1/* and gets routed to a model. Verify, capture-replay, registry queries, doctor checks, and dashboard activity do not count.
Do I keep paying after I compile?
No. Once a .kolm exists and you're running it on your own hardware (laptop, server, phone, browser, VPC, air-gap), there's no per-call charge. You only pay your kolm plan + your own hardware + your own electricity. The gateway plan covers ongoing capture, drift detection, and registry hosting.
What if I go over the call limit?
Overage at $0.50 / 1,000 calls, billed on the next invoice. We email you at 50%, 80%, and 100% so there are no surprises. You can also set a hard cap that returns HTTP 429 to your code: useful if you want to enforce a budget at the infra layer.
Can I self-host the whole stack?
Yes, on every plan, including Free. Apache-2.0 source, Docker Compose, Kubernetes Helm chart, plus an air-gap bundle on Team and Enterprise. The hosted gateway is a convenience: the portable runtime is the lock-in escape hatch. See /self-host.
Is there a startup or nonprofit discount?
Yes. 75% off Pro for funded startups under $5M ARR for 12 months. Free Team for registered 501(c)(3) nonprofits. Enterprise research programs are reviewed case by case. Email hello@kolm.ai with proof.
Can I bring my own API keys (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.)?
Yes, on every plan. The gateway can route to your provider keys instead of its own pool. The gateway-fee still applies, but the inference cost lands on your provider invoice. Annual pricing is 17% off Pro and Team via invoice; Enterprise is annual by default.
Where does my data live? Can it be moved?
By default, in our US-East tenant, encrypted at rest. EU/APAC residency and BYOC are Enterprise-scoped. Captures and artifacts are tenant-fenced, and you can export captures and registry state at any time. See /security.
Do you sell my data or train on my captures?
No. Captures are tenant-fenced and only used to compile artifacts that you own. We don't train any shared model on customer data. Our subprocessors are listed at /subprocessors: they're infrastructure (AWS, Vercel, Stripe) not data buyers. MSA + SCC-based DPA available on Enterprise.
What happens if I cancel?
Your gateway access stops at the end of the billing period. Your published .kolm artifacts keep running on your own hardware (we have no way to disable them: that's the whole point). You can download all captures + artifacts before cancelling. 30-day no-questions refund on first invoice; after 90 days post-cancellation, captures are purged from our side.
Is there a per-seat charge?
No. Team is flat-rate. Enterprise seat, SCIM, and SAML terms are scoped in the contract. We price the platform boundary, not individual readers. Foundation-model pretraining, unscoped bare-metal deployment, and unbacked certification claims are not included by default on Enterprise; the architecture review prices the actual build.
