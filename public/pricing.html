03 / Speed

Minutes for the scan. Days for a signature with a name on it.

A security review stalls on two different clocks. We compress both, and we are candid about which one needs a person.

The machine clock

No human waits here

Permission reads, audit-trail and egress checks, and the prompt-injection battery are compute. They run in minutes to hours and re-run on every deploy. The free scan, the $750 report, and both continuous plans need nobody in the loop.

The human clock

A name behind the result

Enterprise reviewers still want a person to stand behind the finding, not just a machine that approved a machine. A named co-signer reviews and signs alongside the math. That is days, bounded by an SLA, against the four to eight weeks a from-scratch review takes.

The automated tiers need no human. The Reviewed Attestation adds one, on purpose.

06 / Questions

Before you ask.

Why are the prices just listed?

Because you should not sit through a call to find out whether you can afford us. Every fee is flat and final. You pick a plan and start.

Is the free scan really free?

Yes. Upload redacted logs and get a signed snapshot in minutes. It is watermarked and scoped to what you sent, and it is yours to verify offline. Most teams use it to see a finding before they commit.

Why pay monthly when a report is one-time?

A point-in-time report goes stale on your next deploy. A permission you granted in January can still fire in August. Continuous re-attests on a schedule, so the evidence your buyer checks is never older than your last release.

What does the named co-signer add over the crypto?

The signature proves the bytes are intact. The name proves a person reviewed them. Enterprise reviewers ask for both, so the Reviewed Attestation puts a named, accredited reviewer beside the math.

Do you warrant our agent is secure?

No, and distrust anyone who does. We test and report with reproductions, scoped to exactly what we examined. The scope statement is part of the signed report. Try the sample verifier.

Is there a contingency or success fee?

No. Every price on this page is the whole price. No percentage of your deal, no surprise true-up. Enterprise terms cover an MSA and custom scope.

How is this different from a cheap automated scan?

Every report is generated from your agent's actual permission reads, audit-trail and egress checks, and a live prompt-injection battery, then Ed25519-signed and entered in an append-only transparency log. No two reports are alike, and your buyer verifies every byte offline against your key. This is evidence a reviewer can check, not a templated PDF.

We already use Vanta or have SOC 2. Why this?

kolm does not replace your SOC 2 or your trust center. It is the fast, agent-specific evidence that clears the deal in front of you now, and every finding maps into the SOC 2, ISO 42001 and NIST AI RMF work you are already doing.
