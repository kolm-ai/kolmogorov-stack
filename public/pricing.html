Frequently asked

What you are actually paying for.

The plan structure follows the research: one product should own the path from real API behavior to governed, portable runtime artifacts.

How is a plan recommended?

The estimator calls GET /v1/pricing/estimate and returns a plan from the same backend catalog as the signup flow, treating gateway volume, compile work, seats, governance, SSO, and private deployment as first-class inputs.

Why are compile credits separate?

Compile work is visible by tier, so teams know when they are paying for artifact generation instead of hidden infrastructure. Each credit packages examples, recipes, evaluators, manifests, and receipts.

What maps to enterprise features?

Seats, RBAC, namespaces, provider policy, exports, and readiness gates are product controls tied to objects in the control center, not generic add-ons bolted onto a seat count.

Does evidence leave the dashboard?

Artifacts, manifests, receipts, reports, event streams, and procurement packets stay useful outside the UI. Plan data itself is published at /v1/plans and /v1/billing/tiers.
