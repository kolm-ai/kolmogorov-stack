Security · privacy · compliance

Your data, your weights, your boundary.

Kolm is an open-source compiler. It runs inside your own infrastructure, on-prem, or in an air-gapped enclave. Every artifact ships with a signed receipt that names the teacher, the base model, the eval pack, and the K-score axes. A security engineer, a compliance reviewer, or an auditor can verify the chain years later without trusting us.

Data-flow boundary

The default deployment never sends customer prompts, completions, or model weights to kolm.ai infrastructure. The compiler runs on your machines; only signed receipts and (optionally) public registry pointers leave the boundary.

What stays inside your network

Captures
Raw prompts and completions land in ~/.kolm/events or whichever path KOLM_DATA_DIR points to. Tenant-scoped by namespace and never replicated upstream.
Distilled datasets
Teacher-student pairs are generated by your distill workers against the teacher endpoint you configure. We do not see them.
Model weights
Quantized INT4/INT8 weights are written next to your captures. The .kolm artifact is a zip on your filesystem.
Eval pack runs
K-score evaluations execute against the artifact locally. The numeric scores and axes are computed in-process.
Logs and traces
Trace IDs, request bodies, and tool outputs are in your observability stack. Use the OTLP exporter if you want them in Datadog or Grafana.

What does cross the boundary (only if you opt in)

Receipt CIDs
If you publish to the public registry, a content-addressed hash of the receipt (no weights, no captures) becomes resolvable at kolm.ai/verify/<cid> for downstream verifiers.
Telemetry (off by default)
Opt-in usage counters help us prioritize. Set KOLM_TELEMETRY=0 to disable; the CLI never sends prompts or completions either way.
Anthropic / OpenAI calls
Only if you use the hosted teacher path. Bring-your-own-key and self-hosted vLLM, SGLang, and TGI teachers keep all traffic in your network.

Training data boundary

A rented GPU is someone else's machine. kolm enforces a simple rule before any corpus is uploaded: public data may train on a rented pod; sensitive or customer data may not. The rent path classifies the corpus and refuses to send PII, PHI, secrets, or other sensitive classes to a third-party pod. This is enforced in code, not policy text.

Where each kind of data trains

Public or open data
May train on a rented pod (RunPod, Vast, Lambda, and similar). Nothing else about your account leaves with it.
Sensitive or customer data
Trains on local hardware, an air-gapped host, or BYOC (your own cloud), so it never crosses your boundary. The compiler refuses a rented pod for this data unless you pass an explicit, logged override that acknowledges the data will leave.
On a rented pod
The corpus and adapter are encrypted at rest, and the workspace is shredded on teardown before the instance is released. No corpus is left behind on a machine you no longer hold.

Signing and verification

Every .kolm artifact ships with an Ed25519 signature over a content-addressed receipt. The receipt names the inputs that produced the artifact and the K-score that gated it. Verification is offline and reproducible.

  • Ed25519 over the receipt CID

    ALGORITHM · live

    The receipt is canonicalized to JCS, hashed (SHA-256), and signed. The signature, the public key fingerprint, and the receipt body all sit in the artifact zip. Any developer or auditor with the artifact can verify without contacting us.

  • kolm verify works air-gapped

    OFFLINE · live

    The verifier is a static binary. No network, no registry lookup, no truthfulness service in the middle. Hand a .kolm file to an auditor on a USB stick and they can verify the chain on a laptop.

  • Per-tenant signing keys

    KEY-ROTATION · live

    The CLI generates an Ed25519 keypair on first use and stores it at ~/.kolm/keys. Rotate with kolm keys rotate; previous receipts remain verifiable because the public key fingerprint is embedded.

  • Artifact hash binds to receipt

    TAMPER-EVIDENCE · live

    The artifact hash is computed over the canonical zip and embedded in the receipt. Editing weights, swapping an eval pack, or rewriting the K-score after the fact invalidates the signature.

Deployment modes

Pick the boundary that matches your regulator. The same compiler, the same CLI, the same receipts; only the network shape changes.

  • Inside your AWS, GCP, or Azure

    VPC-RESIDENT · default

    Compiler workers run in a private subnet. Teacher calls go to Anthropic, OpenAI, or your self-hosted endpoint over your VPC routes. Receipts written to S3/GCS with object-lock.

    • Default for SaaS teams with vendor data
    • BAA-eligible when paired with HIPAA-aware teacher path
    • One-VPC reference Terraform in enterprise

  • Your own hardware

    ON-PREM · regulated

    CLI installed on workstations, distill runs on an RTX 5090 or H100 box, artifacts written to a CIFS or S3-compatible store you operate. No outbound calls if you bring your own teacher.

    • Default for regulated payors and providers
    • Works with self-hosted vLLM, SGLang, TGI
    • See healthcare for HIPAA path

  • No network at all

    AIR-GAPPED · sealed

    CLI and runtimes are static binaries. Move artifacts and eval packs in on a sealed disk; the verifier works offline. Used by defense and intelligence pilots.

    • kolm verify is a single static binary
    • Eval packs are CIDs, not URLs
    • Receipts verifiable on a clean laptop

HIPAA · BAA available, PHI never crosses the kolm.ai boundary by default

For covered entities, business associates, and EHR vendors. The compiler is designed so PHI in your prompts and completions stays inside your own infrastructure or on-prem; the BAA covers the narrow surface where Kolm processes hashes and metadata.

HIPAA categoryWhere it livesWhat Kolm sees
Raw PHI in prompts / completionsYour VPC or on-prem (~/.kolm/events)Nothing — captures stay tenant-local, never replicated upstream.
Distilled teacher-student pairsYour distill worker (same network as the teacher endpoint)Nothing — pairs are generated against your teacher; we do not see them.
Quantized weights (INT4/INT8)Your object store next to the capturesNothing — weights live in the .kolm artifact zip on your filesystem.
Receipt CIDs (hashes, no PHI)Public registry (opt-in only)Hash + signature + K-score numbers. No prompt text, no PHI.
Eval-pack run telemetryYour local eval runnerAggregate K-score numbers; rows stay local. PHI from eval set never leaves.
Audit logs (your operational record)Your SIEM (Splunk, Datadog, OS log)Nothing unless you ship them to Kolm; we never poll your audit log.

Three things make this contract enforceable: (1) the compiler is open-source, so the network paths are auditable; (2) the receipt format binds the artifact hash to the signing key, so you can verify any deployed weights came from your own distill run; (3) the BAA explicitly enumerates the narrow surface where Kolm processes hashes — if the architecture changes, the BAA changes too.

// HIPAA-shaped deployment: same compiler, all PHI stays inside
$ export KOLM_DATA_DIR=/secure/phi-namespace/kolm
$ export KOLM_TELEMETRY=0                  # belt-and-braces: telemetry already excludes PHI
$ kolm capture log "<chart summary prompt with PHI>"  # lands at /secure/phi-namespace/kolm/events
$ kolm distill --teacher https://vllm.internal/v1   # teacher inside your own infrastructure, PHI never egresses
$ kolm compile && kolm verify          # signed receipt: only hashes + K-score axes
$ # optional, off by default:
$ # kolm publish <cid>                  # publishes hash to /verify/<cid>, no PHI

Business Associate Agreement · standard terms, narrow surface

The Kolm BAA covers the compiler-as-a-service path (hosted distill / hosted compile / hosted registry) and the audit log shipping path. It deliberately does not promise things we cannot enforce — like custody of PHI that never reaches our infrastructure.

  • Surface in scope

    SCOPE · narrow

    Hosted control-plane API (key issuance, namespace metadata), hosted registry (receipt CID resolution), hosted distill workers if you opt in (rare; most HIPAA tenants run distill in their VPC), audit-log shipping if you opt in. Default deployment uses none of these for PHI — the BAA still covers the metadata path because some sites need it for procurement sign-off.

  • Surface deliberately out of scope

    SCOPE · clear

    Captures on your machines, weights on your machines, teacher endpoints you operate, eval-pack runs inside your network. The BAA does not assign Kolm responsibility for storage we never touch — that would be a fiction that hurts you in incident response.

  • Breach notification · 60 days (HIPAA Breach Notification Rule)

    BREACH · 60 day

    If a breach affects the in-scope surface, Kolm notifies the covered entity within 60 days of discovery (the HIPAA statutory ceiling). In practice we commit to 72 hours in the contract attachment so customers can meet faster downstream notification windows.

  • Subcontractors

    CHAIN · documented

    All cloud subcontractors that touch BAA-scope data (AWS, Cloudflare for the hosted control plane only) sign downstream BAAs. The list is in the BAA appendix and updated when subcontractors change. No model-provider subcontractor sees PHI under the default deployment.

  • Termination · data return / destruction

    EXIT · clean

    On termination, Kolm returns or destroys all PHI in the in-scope surface within 30 days and provides a signed destruction certificate. Customer-resident data is unaffected because it never reached Kolm in the first place.

  • How to execute

    PROCESS · 5 business days typical

    Email security@kolm.ai requesting the BAA. We return either our standard form (covers 95% of cases) or accept yours with a redline pass. Typical signing window from request to fully-executed: five business days for our form, ten for customer-paper. See the BAA terms page for the standard-form text.

Key management

Signing keys are the trust root for every artifact. Kolm defaults to a local Ed25519 keypair so you can verify offline on day one, then upgrades cleanly to a managed KMS or an HSM when your security review requires it.

  • Default · local Ed25519 keypair

    LOCAL · live

    Generated on first kolm keys init at ~/.kolm/keys with 0600 perms. Public key fingerprint embedded in every receipt; private key never leaves the machine. Sufficient for solo engineers, hackathons, and the quickstart.

  • Managed · AWS KMS / GCP KMS / Azure Key Vault

    KMS · live

    Configure with kolm sign --kms aws://arn:... (also gcp://projects/... and azure://vaults/...). The private key never leaves the KMS; the CLI sends only the canonical receipt hash for signature. Per-call signing is auditable via your KMS provider's CloudTrail / Cloud Audit Logs / Azure Monitor.

  • Self-managed · HashiCorp Vault Transit

    VAULT · live

    For teams running Vault. Configure with kolm sign --vault https://vault.acme.internal --key-name kolm-prod. Same hash-only signing contract; key custody stays under your Vault policy.

  • HSM-backed · PKCS#11 (YubiHSM, AWS CloudHSM, Thales)

    HSM · enterprise

    For FedRAMP-Mod-plus deployments or any policy that requires FIPS 140-3 Level 3 hardware. Configure with kolm sign --pkcs11-lib /opt/cloudhsm/lib/libcloudhsm_pkcs11.so --slot 0 --key-label kolm-prod. Key material never extractable; tamper-evident audit logs from the HSM are the immutable record.

  • Rotation policy

    ROTATION · live

    kolm keys rotate generates a new keypair and writes a rotation receipt that links the new fingerprint to the prior one (signed by both). Previously issued artifacts remain verifiable because every receipt embeds the public key it was signed with. Recommended cadence: 12 months for local keys, per-KMS-policy for managed keys.

  • Forward · zero-knowledge receipts

    ROADMAP ·

    A privacy mode where the receipt commits to the artifact hash + K-score axes via a STARK / Groth16 proof, so an external verifier can confirm the gate verdict without learning the eval-pack contents or the per-axis numbers. Spec will land at /spec/zk-receipts when the back-end work in lands; until then this row is roadmap, not shipping — we do not link a URL we have not built.

Retention and deletion

Captures, receipts, and eval runs are tenant-scoped and namespace-scoped. You set retention; we never apply a default lifetime to your data.

  • Per-namespace retention

    CAPTURES · live

    Set retention_days per namespace via kolm namespace set-retention <ns> --days 90. Older captures are deleted by the local rotator. Receipts that reference deleted captures remain verifiable because they only embed hashes.

  • Subject deletion

    RIGHT-TO-DELETE · live

    Pass a deletion request to kolm capture delete --subject <id>. The CLI removes raw rows and writes a tombstone receipt so auditors can prove the deletion happened without recovering the data.

  • Artifact lifecycle

    ARTIFACTS · live

    .kolm artifacts live in your object store. The compiler never deletes them. Use your bucket lifecycle to age artifacts; the receipt CID stays verifiable as long as the receipt is preserved (the receipt is a few kilobytes).

  • Registry takedown

    REGISTRY · live

    If you publish a receipt CID to the public registry, you can request takedown at trust contact. Takedown removes the CID from the public index; downstream verifiers with the receipt zip continue to verify.

Threat model

What the compiler does and does not defend against, with the mitigation that handles each class.

  • Tampered weights post-compile

    INTEGRITY · defended

    An operator swaps the INT4 weights in a deployed artifact and tries to ship the same receipt.mitigation · artifact hash bound to signed receipt; kolm verify fails

  • Spoofed K-score

    INTEGRITY · defended

    A team edits the K-score JSON to claim a higher gate verdict than the eval pack produced.mitigation · K-score and axes signed in receipt; verifier recomputes if eval pack present

  • Stolen API key

    AUTH · defended

    A leaked CLI API key is used to publish to the hosted registry or call hosted teachers.mitigation · rotate with kolm keys rotate; per-tenant rate limits; audit log

  • Captures exfiltrated from disk

    OS · out-of-scope

    An attacker with filesystem access reads ~/.kolm/events directly.mitigation · out of scope for the compiler; use OS-level encryption (LUKS, BitLocker, FileVault)

  • Supply-chain on the CLI

    SUPPLY-CHAIN · defended

    A compromised release pushes a backdoored kolm binary.mitigation · releases signed; SBOM published; reproducible build script in repo

  • Prompt injection on captures

    RUNTIME · defended

    A captured prompt contains instructions that try to manipulate downstream distill or scoring.mitigation · eval pack runs are sandboxed; teacher calls are scoped per row

Operational security

How we run kolm.ai the service; how you should run the compiler in your environment.

  • Least-privilege access

    INTERNAL · kolm.ai

    Engineering access to the hosted control plane is SSO-gated, MFA-enforced, and reviewed quarterly. Production access is logged to an immutable audit store.

  • Vulnerability response

    INTERNAL · kolm.ai

    Critical CVEs in dependencies patched within 7 days; high within 30. Disclosure window is 90 days from a verified report. Email security@kolm.ai with a writeup.

  • Incident response

    INTERNAL · kolm.ai

    On-call rotation for the hosted gateway and registry. Post-incident reviews shared with affected enterprise customers within 14 days.

  • Network egress

    YOUR-SIDE · operator

    For VPC deployments, allowlist only the teacher endpoint(s) you use and (optionally) the public registry. Everything else stays inside.

  • Key custody

    YOUR-SIDE · operator

    Signing keys live in ~/.kolm/keys by default. For multi-engineer teams, move them to your KMS (AWS, GCP, Azure, HashiCorp Vault) and configure kolm sign --kms.

  • Audit log shipping

    YOUR-SIDE · operator

    The CLI emits structured JSON to ~/.kolm/audit.log. Tail it into Splunk, Datadog, or your SIEM. Receipts are the durable record; the audit log is the operational one.
