01 / How we hold your data

The data you send is redacted, scoped, and short-lived.

You are evaluating a vendor with your own logs. Reasonable people want to know exactly what leaves their side and how long it lives on ours. The answer is small: redacted input in, a signed report out, and very little kept in between.

What you send

Redacted logs, not raw traffic

A scan runs on logs you redact before upload. Secrets, customer identifiers, and payloads you strip stay on your side. We work from exactly what you choose to send.

ASR-3 · data egress

What we touch

Only what the engagement scopes

Processing is bounded by the scope in your engagement. Access is scoped per tenant with per-row fences, so one tenant's data is never in another's query path.

ASR-1 · least privilege

What we keep

The signed artifact, not your raw data

The output is one signed evidence report and its log entry. Raw uploaded logs are not retained past the run that produced it. The report is the record; the inputs are not.

ASR-6 · evidence

02 / Data handling

Redact. Process in scope. Retain only the artifact.

One short lifecycle. Redaction happens on your side first, processing stays inside the assessed scope, and what persists afterward is the signed report, not your uploads.

01 · Redact You strip it first

Logs are redacted on your side before upload. Field-level redaction removes secrets and customer identifiers before anything reaches our processing.

02 · Process We work in scope

Processing runs against the redacted input inside the assessed scope. Data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256), isolated per tenant.

03 · Retain We keep the report

What persists is the signed report and its log entry. Raw uploads are not retained beyond the run. Minimal retention by default, not by request.

Encryption

AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit

Data in the hosted control plane is encrypted at rest with AES-256 (AWS KMS, per-tenant data keys). Client traffic uses TLS 1.3 with HSTS.

Isolation

Per-tenant, per-namespace, per-row

Data is isolated at the tenant and namespace level, with per-row tenant fences inside every query handler. Defense in depth, not a single boundary.

Access

SSO-gated, MFA-enforced, reviewed quarterly

Engineering access to the control plane is SSO-gated and MFA-enforced, reviewed quarterly. Production access is written to an immutable audit store.

Scope is contractual. Permission posture, redaction and audit-trail integrity are assessed. Injection is tested and reported, not warranted.

03 / Signing key management

One Ed25519 key signs every report. The private half stays put.

The signature your buyer checks is only as good as the key behind it. We keep that key small, single-purpose, and rotated, and we publish the fingerprint so verification needs nothing from us at check time.

  • + Reports are canonicalized to JCS, hashed with SHA-256, then signed with Ed25519
  • + The private key stays in the signing service; only the public key fingerprint travels in the report
  • + Editing any field after signing invalidates the signature, in front of the reviewer
  • + Rotation runs via kolm keys rotate on a defined cadence
  • + Prior reports stay verifiable because each carries the fingerprint it was signed with

04 / Transparency log

Every signature lands in a Merkle log you can audit.

A signature you cannot situate in a record is a signature you have to take on faith. Each signed report is appended to a public log, so a reviewer can confirm it was issued and never quietly altered.

Each signed report is appended to a transparency log structured after RFC 6962. Entries are SHA-256 hashed into a Merkle tree, so no entry can be edited or removed without changing the root. Every report ships with an inclusion proof a reviewer checks independently.

This is a Merkle log, not a blockchain. An Ed25519 and SHA-256 append-only structure, with no tokens, no wallets, and no distributed ledger. The log is public and anyone can recompute the root.

05 / Responsible disclosure

Found something? Tell us, and you are covered.

If you find a security issue in our hosted services, signing pipeline, CLI, or evidence format, report it to dev@kolm.ai with a writeup, reproduction steps, and the impact you assessed.

  • We acknowledge within 24 hours and triage within 72 hours
  • Coordinated disclosure window of 90 days from a verified report
  • We aim to remediate well before the window closes, and we say so proactively if a fix needs longer
  • We do not pursue legal action against researchers acting in good faith under this policy

Dependency hygiene

Critical CVEs in 7 days

Critical CVEs in dependencies are patched within 7 days, high severity within 30. A CycloneDX SBOM ships with every CLI release, signed and verifiable against the release tag.

Supply chain

Signed, reproducible releases

CLI releases are signed and built from a reproducible script in the public repository, so a downstream consumer can audit the dependency tree and confirm the bytes.

Incident response

On-call, with a written follow-up

An on-call rotation covers the hosted gateway and signing pipeline. Post-incident reviews are shared with affected enterprise customers within 14 days of closure.

To be exact about kolm itself: we do not currently hold a SOC 2 report, and there is no published third-party penetration test of our own infrastructure. We will not list either until it exists and can be verified. What we offer today is evidence you can check yourself: the signing facts above, the Merkle log, and the open verifier. Questions?
