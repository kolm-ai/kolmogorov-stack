A distilled 7B parameter student model tuned for the medical domain: clinical NLP over physician notes, ICD-10 code suggestion, and patient intake triage. Reproducible build receipts; open weights at release.

What it is

kolm-medical-7b is a 7-billion-parameter foundation student distilled from a larger frontier teacher and tuned on a curated open medical corpus. The artifact targets three concrete workloads: extracting structured signals (problems, medications, allergies, vitals) out of free-text physician notes, suggesting candidate ICD-10 codes from an encounter summary, and triaging patient intake messages by acuity and routing.

The artifact ships as a signed .kolm bundle. Every download includes the manifest, the model weights at the quantization tier you request (BF16, INT8, INT4), the teacher origin record, and the K-Score receipt.

Training-data lineage

Three sources, all attributable in the manifest:

  • Synthetic teacher traces. Prompts derived from public medical text (MIMIC public-release surgical reports, n2c2 challenge corpora, PubMed abstracts); outputs generated by the frontier teacher; sampled into a training corpus.
  • Public reference text. ICD-10-CM tabular and index, SNOMED-CT public release, FDA drug labels (DailyMed), USPSTF guidelines. All public-domain or freely licensed.
  • Redacted captures. Opt-in synthetic captures only. No PHI is accepted into the training corpus. All medical-adjacent captures pass through a HIPAA-aware redactor that strips identifiers before the row reaches the lake.

How to download

Once the artifact is published to the public marketplace:

kolm pull kolm-medical-7b
kolm verify ~/.kolm/artifacts/kolm-medical-7b.kolm
kolm run kolm-medical-7b --prompt "Extract problems and medications from this note: ..."
Status: private beta. The marketplace is currently in private beta. To request early access, submit a proposal at /marketplace/upload. The artifact spec and K-Score receipts are reviewable in this repo today; the public download endpoint opens with the marketplace public launch.

Plain constraints

  • Not medical advice. Outputs are model-generated text grounded in the input context. They are not a diagnosis, not a treatment recommendation, and not a substitute for licensed clinical judgment.
  • No PHI in training. The training corpus contains zero protected health information. Tenant-contributed captures are HIPAA-redacted before ingest. If you fine-tune kolm-medical-7b on your own PHI, run that fine-tune inside your covered-entity boundary, not on a shared inference endpoint.
  • Clinician-in-the-loop required for clinical decisions. Any output that feeds a clinical decision (diagnosis, prescribing, dosage, triage routing that bypasses a clinician) must pass through a licensed clinician before action.
  • FDA status. kolm-medical-7b is not an FDA-cleared medical device. Use for non-device administrative, documentation, and decision-support workflows behind a clinician.
