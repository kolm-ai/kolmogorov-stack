A distilled 7B parameter student model tuned for customer support: ticket triage, intent classification, and drafted first-response replies. Reproducible build receipts; open weights at release.

What it is

kolm-support-7b is a 7-billion-parameter foundation student distilled from a larger frontier teacher and tuned on a curated support corpus. The artifact targets three concrete workloads: routing incoming tickets to the right queue with a confidence score, classifying customer intent against a configurable taxonomy, and drafting a first-response reply that an agent can edit and send.

The artifact ships as a signed .kolm bundle. Every download includes the manifest, the model weights at the quantization tier you request (BF16, INT8, INT4), the teacher origin record, and the K-Score receipt.

Training-data lineage

Three sources, all attributable in the manifest:

  • Synthetic teacher traces. Prompts derived from public support corpora (StackExchange, GitHub issues, public help-center snapshots); outputs generated by the frontier teacher; sampled into a training corpus.
  • Public reference text. Public help-center articles, open-source product documentation, and the Mozilla Common Voice support-tag annotations. All public-domain or freely licensed.
  • Redacted captures. Opt-in captures from tenants that elected to contribute, PII-scrubbed via the media-redact pipeline. Personal names, email addresses, account ids, and order numbers are stripped before the row reaches the lake.

How to download

Once the artifact is published to the public marketplace:

kolm pull kolm-support-7b
kolm verify ~/.kolm/artifacts/kolm-support-7b.kolm
kolm run kolm-support-7b --prompt "Triage this ticket and draft a reply: ..."
Status: private beta. The marketplace is currently in private beta. To request early access, submit a proposal at /marketplace/upload. The artifact spec and K-Score receipts are reviewable in this repo today; the public download endpoint opens with the marketplace public launch.

Plain constraints

  • Human-in-loop required for escalations. Any flow that escalates to refund, churn-save, credit, account closure, or a regulated workflow (medical, legal, financial) must require an agent to review and send. Auto-send is supported only for low-risk informational replies.
  • Drafted replies are drafts. The model produces a starting point. Agents are expected to edit before sending; the studio includes a one-click accept/edit/reject UI for that loop.
  • Taxonomy is yours. Out of the box the intent taxonomy is a generic SaaS support taxonomy. For best K-Score, fine-tune with 200-1000 of your own labeled tickets; the studio has a guided flow for this.
  • Knowledge cutoff. The training corpus is bounded by the capture window listed above. Product-specific knowledge after the cutoff is not in the weights; supply it at inference via retrieval context.
