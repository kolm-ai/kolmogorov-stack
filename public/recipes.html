Two audiences, one toolchain

Pick the lane that fits.

Both lanes ship through the same kolm build verb, the same K-score gate, the same Ed25519 receipt. The difference is what the recipe optimises for: parameter efficiency on consumer GPUs, or behavior under regulator scrutiny.

Frontier 7 recipes

For the LocalLLaMA & HF efficiency crowd.

Multi-teacher fused distillations, MTP speed-demons, sub-2-bit generalists, agentic workflow compilers, merge-first frankenmodels. Every recipe targets a real GPU class (5090, 4090, H100) with measured tok/s and K-score floors.

Reference points already in the wild: DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B quantizes from 61.0 GB to 17.9 GB INT4 in 125.3s on a single RTX 5090; Qwen2.5-7B lands at 5.2 GB and 24.5 tok/s.

Production Vertical 7 recipes

For the enterprise & regulated industry crowd.

HIPAA-clean medical summarisers, PCI-aware finance classifiers, contract redactors, SecOps incident summarisers, internal RAG specialists. Every recipe ships with a PII mode, a K-score floor, and a binder receipt an auditor can verify offline.

Vertical bases inherit the same Ed25519 chain as the frontier recipes; the difference is the eval pack (HIPAA-mapping, SR 11-7, NIST AI RMF) and the redaction policy baked into the binder.

Frontier recipes · 7

Frontier recipes.

Reasoning, coding, multi-teacher fusion, MTP, agentic, merge-first, low-bit. Each one is a single command. Each one writes a signed .kolm to ~/.kolm/artifacts/. Each one ships at or above its K-score floor, or it does not ship at all.

quant lib bitsandbytes 0.49.2 signing Ed25519 over JCS-canonical receipt gate K-score on held-out eval pack

  • Frontier reasoning, 8B

    reasoning · multi-teacher · dynamic-2.0

    Distill Claude Opus 4.7 into a Qwen2.5-7B student under a reasoning-mode trace. Ships at 4-bit dynamic-2.0 once the K-score clears 0.92 on chain-of-thought evals.

    $ kolm build reasoning --teacher=claude-opus-4-7 --student=qwen2-5-7b --bit=4.0 --mode=reasoning --kscore-min=0.92
    size~5.2 GB K-score floor0.92 targetRTX 4090+ evalchain-of-thought
    Compose in /forge

  • Frontier coding, 13B + MTP

    coding · mtp · aider + swe-bench

    DeepSeek-R1-32B teacher distilled into a Qwen2.5-14B student with multi-token-prediction. Benchmarks gated on Aider and SWE-bench Verified.

    $ kolm build coding --teacher=deepseek-r1-32b --student=qwen2-5-14b --bit=4.0 --mode=mpt --bench=aider,swe-bench
    size~9.4 GB decodeMTP-2 targetRTX 5090 benchaider + swe-bench

Author a recipe

Ship your own. Keep your share.

Every recipe in the gallery started as a single command on someone's laptop. If yours reproduces, signs, and clears its K-score floor, publish it to the marketplace and keep the revenue share configured on your tenant.

$ kolm recipe new my-recipe
  1. Edit the generated recipe.yaml: name the base, the teacher (if any), the PII mode, the K-score floor, the eval pack.
  2. Run kolm recipe publish; the marketplace lists your recipe with its signed receipt and the revenue-share terms from your tenant settings.
