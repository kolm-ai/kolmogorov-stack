01  EU residency by default

EU-resident compile, end to end.

Pick your region at job submission. The orchestrator pins every step of the build to compute and storage inside that region. Each step is countersigned by a region attestation in the manifest, so a buyer auditing the receipt can verify the job never crossed a border.

02  GDPR alignment

GDPR · Article 28 processor obligations.

Under GDPR, you are the Controller of personal data and kolm acts as Processor. Our standard data-processing agreement is annotated against Article 28 line by line. The product enforces the contract: no off-product training on your data without explicit opt-in, no silent sub-processor swaps, no retention drift.

Lawful basis

Controller / Processor split.

kolm processes Personal Data only on documented instructions from you. Recital 81 followed: every sub-processor is named in Schedule 2, with 30-day notice and an objection window before any addition takes effect.

03  EU AI Act readiness

EU AI Act · high-risk obligations.

For high-risk EU AI Act workloads, kolm turns technical documentation, data governance, transparency, and monitoring evidence into build artifacts instead of slideware.

Risk management (Art. 9)Each compile emits a signed risk record listing seed coverage, refusal classes, known failure modes, and the holdout score that gated the ship decision.
Data governance (Art. 10)Training data lineage is recorded as part of the receipt chain: source manifest, licence, redaction policy, version, and hash, all signed.
Technical documentation (Art. 11)The .kolm artifact ships with the spec, seeds outline, evaluation results, and supersession lineage in a single zip. That zip is your Annex IV file.
Record-keeping (Art. 12)Every inference is loggable with a hash receipt; the schema is open and replayable so an authority can audit a specific decision without raw data egress.
Transparency (Art. 13)Model card and behaviour are derivable from the artifact; nothing about the system is hidden behind a vendor API.
Human oversight (Art. 14)Refusal classes and confidence thresholds are first-class in the spec; you can configure mandatory human review for any output class.
Accuracy & robustness (Art. 15)K-score axes (correctness, calibration, refusal, drift, recovery) ship inside the manifest. Drift triggers an alert; supersession blocks let you roll forward without losing the audit chain.
04  Data sovereignty

Data sovereignty in EU regions.

Your tenant key encrypts training data and weights at rest in-region. If a regulator asks where data lives, the answer is the bucket name and region attestation in the receipt.

Encryption

Tenant-managed keys.

BYOK supported via AWS KMS and GCP KMS in EU regions. Default is kolm-managed keys scoped to your tenant and rotated quarterly.

Operator access

Break-glass with a witness.

Operator access to EU tenant data requires a customer-side approver, an emitted audit event, and a 24-hour automatic revocation.

Self-hosted option

Pull it on-prem.

If the regulator wants air-gap, deploy the kolm compile server inside your network. Same receipts, same K-score, no internet egress at all.
