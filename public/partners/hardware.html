Hardware partners

kolm runs distilled, quantized, kolm-compiled artifacts on the hardware a customer already owns - or on the GPU they bought this week. This page is the partner-facing surface: the "Powered by kolm" program, the buy-a-partner-GPU-get-one-year-kolm-Pro template, and an honest status on vendor badges (NVIDIA, Intel, AMD) for the contracts we have not signed yet.

Powered by kolm - hardware partner program

If you sell GPUs, AI accelerators, edge devices, or any silicon that runs neural inference, the "Powered by kolm" program is how we co-market the workbench customers already use to compile and ship models onto your hardware. It is opt-in, free for the partner, and revenue-share-free on the kolm side.

Brand "Powered by kolm" badge Static SVG and PNG marks for product pages, retail boxes, datasheets, and conference booths. Brand guidelines covered in brand.
Co-marketing Joint case studies and launch posts A real customer compiling a real model on partner silicon, with a real benchmark - co-bylined and cross-posted. Template at case-studies.
Support Dedicated partner support channel A shared Slack or email loop with the kolm engineering team, plus a partner-only escalation path for hardware-specific kernel and runtime issues.

Buy a partner GPU, get 1 year of kolm Pro

This is the partnership template - opt-in, per-SKU, signed per-partner. When a customer buys a covered GPU through a partner channel, we credit one year of kolm Pro at retail value. The numbers below are the canonical example used in partner conversations; actual SKUs and credit periods are settled in the partner agreement, not by this page.

Template - RTX 5090 example

The partner sells a covered SKU. The customer registers the serial number on the partner portal. kolm provisions a one-year Pro entitlement on the tenant of the customer's choice. The credit equals the retail value of a kolm Pro year so the partner can present it as a true "free year, no asterisk" benefit.

How a partner engagement works

  1. Intro call. Partner emails partners@kolm.ai. 30 minutes, no NDA required for the first call.
  2. Scope. We agree on the program tier ("Powered by kolm" badge only, or full GPU credit template), the SKU list, and the co-marketing cadence.
  3. Agreement. Short partner agreement - usually one page plus the SKU schedule. No revenue share, no exclusivity, no minimum volume.
  4. Launch. We swap the placeholder badge above for the partner logo, publish the joint case study, and activate the redemption route if the GPU credit template is in scope.
  5. Ongoing. Quarterly check-in. Customer escalations route through the dedicated support channel. Renewals are automatic unless either side opts out 60 days before the anniversary.

Frequently asked questions

Is the GPU credit funded by the partner or by kolm?

By kolm. The partner does not contribute cash. We provision a tenant credit equal to the retail value of one year of kolm Pro. The partner contributes co-marketing, badge placement, and channel awareness.

Do you take a cut of the hardware revenue?

No. The partner keeps 100 percent of the hardware sale price. kolm never touches the hardware transaction. Our return on this program is the Pro tenant on the year-two renewal, the case study, and the brand placement.

Can a partner co-design a custom kernel or quantization scheme?

Yes - that is the dedicated support channel. Custom kernels land in workers/ as opt-in modules with a clear vendor namespace so the rest of the workbench stays vendor-neutral. See docs/hardware for the runtime target list we ship today.

What if a vendor wants exclusivity?

We do not sign exclusivity agreements. The workbench has to run on whatever silicon a customer chooses to buy, and locking out a vendor breaks that promise. Co-marketing exclusivity for a launch window (e.g., 30-day post-announcement quiet period) is negotiable.

How is the GPU serial verified?

The activation route accepts the serial number printed on the box (or read from the device's PCI ID) and validates it against a list the partner uploads as part of the agreement. The validation logic is the same shape as the /v1/verify/:cid receipt path - signed, append-only, and auditable on both sides.
