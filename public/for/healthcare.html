The problem

How the hosted-LLM contract breaks in healthcare.

PHI residency

Your text prompts are protected health information.

A chart summary that mentions a member by demographics is PHI under 45 CFR 160.103. Sending it to a hosted API is a disclosure that needs a BAA, encryption-in-transit, and audit logging the vendor does not surface.

Cost

$0.03 per chart summary doesn’t end.

At 40,000 daily encounters that’s $1,200/day — $438k/yr — for a workload you already have training data for. The frontier model never gets cheaper. Your distilled specialist runs forever on a GPU you already bought.

Drift

You can’t freeze the model your validators reviewed.

When OpenAI swaps GPT-4o behavior overnight, your IRB-approved evaluation no longer describes the production system. SR 11-7-style change control needs an artifact you control, not a hosted endpoint.

Auditability

Your security team wants signed receipts, not vendor attestations.

A SOC 2 letter is a control narrative. A clinical safety officer needs a cryptographic record that this specific model returned this specific output to this specific clinician at this specific time. Kolm signs all three with Ed25519.

What kolm changes

A distilled specialist, signed, on your hardware, in three steps.

No new vendor in the PHI path. The capture, distill, and compile steps all run inside the perimeter you already own — on-prem, BYOC, or air-gapped. The output is a single signed .kolm file you can verify offline.

Step 01

Capture inside your own infrastructure

Wrap your existing model calls with the kolm gateway running in your AWS / GCP / Azure account. Captures land in your encrypted bucket, never in ours.

$ kolm gateway up --provider anthropic \
  --namespace charts.prod --vpc-only
Step 02

Distill against your golden set

Point distill at your reviewed capture queue and a frozen evaluation set built from your IRB-approved holdout. The student trains on your GPU.

$ kolm distill \
  --from charts.prod --eval evals/charts-holdout.jsonl \
  --student qwen2.5-7b --quantize int4
Step 03

Compile, sign, deploy

Single .kolm artifact: weights + frozen eval + bakeoff + Ed25519 receipt. Drop it on the inference node behind your existing endpoint. Verify offline.

$ kolm compile --sign --target rtx
$ kolm verify charts-specialist.kolm
ok · ed25519 · eval pass 0.91
