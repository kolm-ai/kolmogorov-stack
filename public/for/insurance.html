kolm for Insurance

How does kolm fit Insurance?

Insurers process claims and policy documents that cannot be sent to a public LLM API on every call. kolm lets an insurer compile a claims specialist that runs inside its own boundary, with PII redacted on capture and a signed receipt for every model build.

Why an owned model fits

Why does Insurance prefer an owned model over an external API?

Insurance carriers operate under model-governance and privacy obligations that favor inspectable, owned models over opaque external APIs. kolm's architecture supports those obligations: the gateway and lake are self-hostable, PII is redacted on the capture path, the compiled model runs inside your own boundary, and every build is recorded in a signed receipt you can produce for review.

  • NAIC model governance expectations
  • state privacy statutes
  • internal model-risk review

The kolm flow

How does the pipeline run in this setting?

Run the kolm gateway in your environment, capture (claim, decision) and (policy, answer) pairs with inline redaction, distill a specialist, gate it on a calibrated K-score, and compile a quantized artifact you serve on-prem. The whole sequence is self-hostable, so capture, distillation, and compilation stay inside your control boundary and the deliverable is a portable, signed model rather than a dependency on an external service.

FAQ

Does claimant data leave our network?
With a self-hosted gateway and a compiled local model, the pipeline runs inside your boundary, and PII is redacted on the capture path before storage.
How do we evidence a model build for examiners?
Each compile emits an Ed25519-signed receipt recording the input weight hashes, method, and K-score, replayable at /verify-prod.
Can we keep human review in the loop?
Yes. kolm produces a model artifact; how you route its outputs through adjuster review is your workflow decision.
