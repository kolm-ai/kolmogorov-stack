The problem

Why your per-ticket bill keeps climbing.

Token economics

Your prompt is 80% boilerplate you pay for every time.

Style guide, refund policy, escalation rules, brand voice — you ship the same 3,200 tokens of system context on every call. Frontier billing meters every one of those tokens. Distillation bakes them into the weights for free.

Vendor lock

Your retrieval index and prompts are not portable.

Switching vendors means rebuilding the retrieval pipeline, re-tuning the prompts, and re-running the eval. Kolm produces a single signed file; the deployment target is your choice (vLLM, llama.cpp, MLX, Ollama, WebGPU).

Latency

A round trip to a hosted endpoint is 600–1,400ms.

When a customer is typing, the human-perceptible budget for the first token is ~200ms. A distilled INT4 specialist on an in-region GPU returns the first token in 40–90ms. The chat experience changes from “wait” to “flow.”

PII leakage

Customer messages contain order IDs, addresses, payment hints.

Every ticket prompt forwarded to a hosted vendor is a disclosure. Kolm distills inside your perimeter; the production specialist never needs to leave it.

What kolm changes

From reviewed responses to a deployable specialist.

You already produced the training data: every accepted draft your agents shipped is a (ticket, ideal-response) pair. Kolm turns that corpus into a deployable specialist.

Step 01

Capture only what shipped

Wrap the existing Zendesk/Intercom hook so kolm records the (incoming ticket, agent-accepted response) pair into your namespace. Drafts that were edited get filtered out automatically.

$ kolm gateway up --provider openai \
  --namespace cx.shipped --capture-only-accepted
Step 02

Distill against the holdout

Last week’s tickets are your validation set. Hold them out, distill on the prior 90 days, and score the specialist against the agent-accept rate.

$ kolm distill --from cx.shipped \
  --student qwen2.5-3b --quantize int4 \
  --eval evals/last-week.jsonl
Step 03

Run on a single L40S

A 32B INT4 specialist serves your full ticket volume on one consumer GPU. The signed receipt lets your security team pin the exact version of the model in change control.

$ kolm runtime up cx-specialist.kolm --target vllm
listening on :8080 · p50 first-token 42ms
