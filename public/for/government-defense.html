The problem

Why hosted AI does not authorize.

Boundary

Your network has one boundary. A hosted LLM crosses it on every call.

RMF, DISA STIGs, and IL5/IL6 boundary controls all assume data does not exit the enclave. A hosted LLM call is, by definition, an outbound disclosure. Kolm’s entire data plane runs inside the boundary you already authorized.

Inspectability

Your A&A reviewer cannot inspect closed weights.

Authorization requires source-of-truth review. Kolm is Apache-2.0 from the gateway through the runtime; your reviewer can pin a commit and rebuild from source in CI you control.

Chain of custody

A hosted model has no signed provenance you control.

When a mission output is challenged, you must show which exact weights produced it. Kolm signs every artifact with Ed25519 keys you generate; the verifier runs offline, on the same box, with no network call.

Supply chain

A model update from the vendor is a supply-chain event.

You need SBOM, reproducible builds, and SLSA-grade provenance for every component. Kolm publishes an SBOM per release; the build is reproducible from the source tag.

What kolm changes

From cleared corpus to signed mission specialist.

Same recipe as commercial, but every step runs inside the boundary, against keys you control, with source open for your A&A reviewer to walk line by line.

Step 01

Capture inside the enclave

Kolm gateway runs on the cleared side. Captures land in your storage system; no metering call, no telemetry, no outbound traffic from the data plane.

$ kolm gateway up --air-gap \
  --namespace mission.corpus --metering off
Step 02

Distill against the cleared eval

Your evaluator builds the frozen evaluation set; distill runs on the cleared GPU. The teacher is whatever cleared model your authorization permits (open-weights or your own).

$ kolm distill --offline \
  --teacher /opt/models/cleared-70b \
  --student qwen2.5-7b --quantize int4
Step 03

Sign with your keys, verify on every load

The signing key never leaves your HSM. The runtime refuses to load any artifact whose signature doesn’t verify against the allowlist. The verifier is a single statically-linked binary that runs offline.

$ kolm compile --sign-with hsm://mission-sk
$ kolm verify --offline mission-spec.kolm
ok · ed25519 · key-id mission-sk-v3
