kolm for Legal services

How does kolm fit Legal services?

Law firms handle privileged documents that must not be sent to a third-party API. kolm lets a firm compile a contract or research specialist that runs inside its own boundary, with PII redacted on capture and a verifiable build receipt for every model.

Why an owned model fits

Why does Legal services prefer an owned model over an external API?

Legal practice carries strict confidentiality and privilege duties that make an owned, inspectable model preferable to an external API. kolm's architecture supports those duties: the gateway and lake are self-hostable, PII is redacted on the capture path, the compiled model runs inside your own boundary, and every build is recorded in a signed receipt you can produce for review.

  • attorney-client privilege
  • client confidentiality duties
  • data-residency commitments

The kolm flow

How does the pipeline run in this setting?

Capture (document, extraction) and (question, memo) pairs through a self-hosted gateway with redaction, distill a specialist tuned to your matter types, and compile a quantized artifact served on firm hardware. The whole sequence is self-hostable, so capture, distillation, and compilation stay inside your control boundary and the deliverable is a portable, signed model rather than a dependency on an external service.
