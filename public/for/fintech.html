The problem

How hosted LLMs fail SR 11-7.

SR 11-7 III.5

You cannot freeze the model your validators tested.

SR 11-7 III.5 (ongoing monitoring) assumes you know what changed. When the vendor silently swaps GPT-4o-2024-08-06 for GPT-4o-2024-11-20, your validation document describes a model that no longer exists in production.

OCC 2011-12

No independent validation possible against a black box.

OCC 2011-12 requires “effective challenge” by validators who can examine inputs, intended use, and limitations. A hosted endpoint behind an API key gives validators no examinable surface beyond outputs.

Data residency

NPI in prompts is a Reg P / GLBA disclosure.

A loan-decisioning prompt that contains a member’s SSN, income, or address is non-public personal information. Sending it to a hosted vendor without a written contract, audit rights, and use limitations is exactly the disclosure GLBA was written to prevent.

Auditability

Your auditors want a hash, not a vendor letter.

Internal audit will ask: “which model approved this loan?” The defensible answer is a 32-byte Ed25519 fingerprint over a frozen artifact — not “OpenAI’s SOC 2 says the inference was logged somewhere.”

What kolm changes

A frozen, signed, examinable specialist in three steps.

Each step is an artifact your MRMC can pin: a capture queue (the inputs), a frozen eval set (the validation), a signed .kolm (the production model). All three are inspectable on disk by your validators.

Step 01

Capture an approved corpus

Wrap your existing LLM calls. Validators review the capture queue, redact NPI, and approve a versioned namespace as the training source.

$ kolm gateway up --provider openai \
  --namespace loans.v3 --redact pii
Step 02

Freeze the evaluation set

Your MRMC builds a holdout against your champion outcomes (charge-off, default, fraud-confirm). The eval is a JSONL pinned to a content hash; you can re-score offline forever.

$ kolm eval freeze \
  --from loans.v3 --holdout 0.1 --seed 42 \
  --out evals/loans-validator-v1.jsonl
Step 03

Distill, sign, pin in production

A single signed .kolm file. Your runtime refuses to load any artifact whose hash isn’t on the MRMC’s allowlist. SR 11-7 III.5 monitoring runs against the frozen eval on a cron.

$ kolm distill --student qwen2.5-7b \
  --eval evals/loans-validator-v1.jsonl --sign
ok · loans-v3-spec.kolm · ed25519: b3:7f...
