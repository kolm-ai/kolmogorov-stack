kolm for Manufacturing

How does kolm fit Manufacturing?

Manufacturers want AI on the plant floor and in field service without a metered cloud round-trip. kolm lets a manufacturer compile a maintenance or quality specialist that runs at the edge, with a signed build receipt and no per-token bill.

Why an owned model fits

Why does Manufacturing prefer an owned model over an external API?

Manufacturing environments often isolate operational technology and demand offline reliability, which favors an owned model over a cloud API. kolm's architecture supports those constraints: the gateway and lake are self-hostable, PII is redacted on the capture path, the compiled model runs inside your own boundary, and every build is recorded in a signed receipt you can produce for review.

  • operational-technology isolation
  • supplier IP confidentiality
  • edge / offline reliability requirements

The kolm flow

How does the pipeline run in this setting?

Capture (symptom, fix) and (spec, answer) pairs through the gateway, distill a maintenance specialist, and compile a heavily quantized artifact that runs on edge hardware with no network. The whole sequence is self-hostable, so capture, distillation, and compilation stay inside your control boundary and the deliverable is a portable, signed model rather than a dependency on an external service.

FAQ

Can a kolm model run with no network?
Yes. The compiled artifact serves locally with no inference-time network dependency, suitable for isolated OT networks.
How small can the model get?
The verified matrix records sizes from 0.44 GB up. You choose the base and quantization to fit your edge hardware.
What proves the deployed model is correct?
The signed receipt records the exact weights and method; verify it at /verify-prod or with kolm verify before deploying to the line.
