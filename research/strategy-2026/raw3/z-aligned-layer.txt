**Aligned Layer (Aligned, alignedlayer.com) is a ZK (zero-knowledge) proof verification layer and broader Ethereum infrastructure provider, primarily functioning as an EigenLayer Actively Validated Service (AVS) for fast, low-cost verification and aggregation of ZK/validity proofs on Ethereum.**[[1]](https://blog.alignedlayer.com/announcing-the-aligned-eigenlayer-testnet/)[[2]](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2024/04/25/eigenlayer-powered-aligned-layer-raises-20m-to-make-zk-proofs-faster-cheaper-on-ethereum)

As of mid-2026 (based on docs, blogs, and explorer activity), its core offering is a **universal verification layer** that addresses Ethereum’s limitations in ZK proof verification—high gas costs, low throughput (~10 proofs/sec on L1), and restrictions on proof systems. It operates in two main modes:

- **Proof Verification Layer (fast mode)**: An EigenLayer AVS using restaked Ethereum validators/operators for optimistic, high-throughput verification (often >1,000 proofs/sec, with claims of 2,500+ in some reports). Operators verify proofs off-chain (in Rust or other languages for optimization) and attest via BLS signatures; a 2/3 majority posts results on-chain with cryptoeconomic security from restaking. This provides fast finality at low cost (significant reductions vs. direct L1 verification, e.g., ~90%+ for systems like Groth16).[[3]](https://docs.alignedlayer.com/)[[4]](https://fuzzinglabs.com/aligned-layer-security-assessment-completed/)
- **Proof Aggregation Service (slower/more secure mode)**: Uses recursive proof aggregation (similar to rollups) for batched settlement directly on Ethereum L1, providing stronger guarantees at the cost of some latency.[[5]](https://docs.alignedlayer.com/introduction/3_faq)

It supports a wide range of proof systems (Groth16, STARKs, and others like Kimchi or Binius that are impractical on raw Ethereum) and is stateless for simplicity. The project has evolved into a vertically integrated stack including Rollup-as-a-Service (RaaS), Wallet-as-a-Service, interoperability, and meta-proving services, with a focus on enabling “provable execution” for finance and other sectors.[[3]](https://docs.alignedlayer.com/) Mainnet beta for the verification layer was live by late 2024/early 2025, with aggregation features advancing into 2025–2026.[[6]](https://blog.alignedlayer.com/aligned-align-tokenomics-and-roadmap/)

It is explicitly positioned for **verifiable AI applications**, including zkML (zero-knowledge machine learning) and verifiable inference. Use cases include proving that AI model inference (or pipeline stages) ran correctly, met policy constraints, or produced specific outputs—then settling results on-chain for auditability, automation, or compliance. High-volume inference (e.g., agent marketplaces or micro-payments) suits the fast Verification Layer, while compliance-oriented or high-stakes events can use aggregated L1 proofs.[[7]](https://blog.alignedlayer.com/proof-verification-layer-vs-aggregation-service/)[[8]](https://docs.alignedlayer.com/introduction/2_use_cases)

It also supports **TEE (Trusted Execution Environment) attestation verification** (e.g., via partnerships or integrations like with Automata Network), allowing decentralized, on-chain verification of hardware attestations for verifiable compute. This extends beyond pure ZK to hybrid setups combining TEEs with cryptographic proofs.[[9]](https://blog.ata.network/on-verifying-tee-attestations-with-aligned-layer-867822e33380)[[9]](https://blog.ata.network/on-verifying-tee-attestations-with-aligned-layer-867822e33380)

**For a startup selling AI-agent security audit reports to enterprise security/compliance teams, anchoring attestations via an on-chain/ZK layer like Aligned would generally add *credibility* in specific high-trust scenarios but *add friction* compared to traditional Ed25519 signatures + Sigstore/Rekor transparency logs.** 

**Credibility benefits**:
- **Decentralized economic security**: Ethereum restaking (via EigenLayer AVS) provides cryptoeconomic guarantees and slashing potential, making tampering or false attestations costly. On-chain settlement offers public verifiability, immutability, and composability (e.g., triggering smart contracts or cross-chain actions).
- **ZK/TEE properties**: Stronger guarantees than signatures alone—proofs of correct computation/inference (not just “someone signed this”) or hardware-rooted attestations. Useful for verifiable AI outputs, audit trails resistant to key compromise, or scenarios needing public auditability without revealing sensitive details.
- **Enterprise appeal in regulated or blockchain-adjacent contexts**: Demonstrates cutting-edge verifiable compute, which can differentiate reports for forward-looking compliance teams (e.g., those dealing with AI governance, supply-chain security, or on-chain finance). TEE + Aligned verification has been explored for machine attestation in AI/decentralized systems.[[9]](https://blog.ata.network/on-verifying-tee-attestations-with-aligned-layer-867822e33380)

**Friction drawbacks**:
- **Complexity and adoption barriers**: Enterprises are often wary of blockchain/crypto integrations due to regulatory uncertainty, custody concerns, gas/token costs, latency, and the need for new tooling/expertise. Sigstore/Rekor is a mature, open standard widely adopted in software supply chains (e.g., Kubernetes, many OSS projects) with simple Ed25519 signing + append-only Merkle-tree transparency logs for tamper-evidence and key transparency.
- **Overkill for many use cases**: Traditional signatures + logs already provide strong, auditable evidence of signing events and integrity. They are lighter, faster, cheaper, and sufficient for most internal compliance reviews or non-on-chain workflows.
- **Operational overhead**: Integrating with Aligned (SDKs, batching, modes, potential fees) vs. standard signing libraries increases development and maintenance burden. Explorer data shows real usage for proof batches, but enterprise uptake of AVS-based systems remains niche compared to Sigstore.

**Candid recommendation**: Start with (or stick primarily to) **traditional Ed25519 signatures + Sigstore/Rekor** for core audit report signing and transparency—it delivers excellent credibility with minimal friction and broad enterprise familiarity. Layer in Aligned (or similar) selectively for premium offerings where on-chain/ZK verifiability adds clear value, such as:
- Reports tied to verifiable AI inference or TEE-protected execution.
- Customers in crypto, DeFi, or those wanting public on-chain audit trails/automation.
- Hybrid setups (sign traditionally, then optionally anchor key attestations or proofs on Aligned for extra assurance).

Test integration costs/latency in a pilot, and position the on-chain option as an “enhanced verifiable” tier rather than a replacement. This balances credibility gains against adoption friction. Monitor Aligned’s enterprise traction and any regulatory clarity around restaking/AVSs.
